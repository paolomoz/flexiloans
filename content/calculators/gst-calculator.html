What is GST- Goods and Services Tax?

Goods and Services Tax (GST) is an indirect tax in India, implemented on July 1, 2017, to replace multiple taxes like VAT, excise duty and service tax. It is a multi-stage, destination-based tax, collected at every stage of production and distribution, but is borne by the end consumer. There are primarily four types of GST in India - CGST, SGST, IGST and UTGST.

Total Price = Original Cost + GST Amount

Understanding GST Calculation

Imagine you own a small bakery that sells a popular cake for ₹500. As a business, you need to add GST to the selling price based on the applicable rate, which is 18% for this type of food product.

Step 1: Calculating GST Exclusive Price

  • Base Price: ₹500
  • GST Rate: 18%

Applying the GST calculation formula:

  • GST Amount = Base Price × (GST Rate / 100​)
  • GST Amount = 500×(18/100​) = ₹90

Step 2: Determining Total Price Including GST

Now, to find the final price that customers will pay, you add the GST amount to the base price:

  • Total Price = Base Price + GST Amount
  • Total Price = 500 + 90 = ₹590

What is the GST Exclusive Amount?

GST exclusive amount refers to the price of goods or services before GST is added. The tax amount is calculated separately and then added to determine the final price payable by the consumer.

What is the GST Inclusive Amount?

GST inclusive amount contains both the cost of the goods or services and the GST. The total price generated is the final amount payable by the consumer with the embedded tax component.

GST Calculation for Inclusive and Exclusive Amounts

Here are some examples of how 18% GST will be calculated for different values and GST types:

GST Exclusive Calculation:

When the price of a product or service does not include GST, you need to add the 18% GST to the base price.

  • Example:
  • If the price of the furniture is ₹1,000 (exclusive of GST), you calculate GST as:
  • GST Amount = ₹1,000 × 0.18 = ₹180
  • So, the final price, including GST, will be:
  • Final Price = ₹1,000 + ₹180 = ₹1,180
  • Using the GST formula, the amount payable comes out to be ₹1,180.

GST Inclusive Calculation:

If the price already includes GST and you want to find out the base price (before GST was applied), you can reverse-calculate by dividing the total price by 1.18 using the GST formula.

  • Example:
  • If the final price, including 18% GST, is ₹1,180, you calculate the base price as below:Base Price = ₹1,180/1.18 ​= ₹1,000
  • The GST amount is:
  • GST Amount = ₹1,180 − ₹1,000 = ₹180
  • Thus, the base price is ₹1,000, and the GST component is ₹180.

Types of GST in India

The tax structure under GST is divided into different components to facilitate the tax collection between the central and state governments. Here are the four types of GST implemented in India:

  1. CGST (Central Goods and Services Tax): CGST is levied and collected by the Central Government on the intra-supply of goods and services.
  2. SGST (State Goods and Services Tax): SGST is levied and collected by the state governments on the intra-supply of goods.
  3. IGST (Integrated Goods and Services Tax): IGST is levied and collected by the Centre on the inter-supply of goods and services.
  4. UTGST (Union Territory Goods and Services Tax): UTGST is restricted to Union Territories like Chandigarh or Lakshadweep. It is levied and collected by UT governments, along with CGST on goods or services sold.

For intra-state transactions, both CGST and SGST are charged, with each component being 50% of the total GST. This is how the structure works:

  • CGST + SGST: For intra-state transactions
  • IGST: For inter-state and international transactions
  • UTGST + CGST: For transactions within Union Territories
