What is a Business Loan EMI Calculator?

An EMI for business loan calculator is an online tool for calculating the Equated Monthly Instalment (EMI) for your business loan. The calculator takes into account factors like the loan amount, business loan interest rate and loan tenure to give you an estimate of your monthly payments.

How is Business Loan EMI Calculated?

The formula for calculating business loan EMI is:

E = P x r (1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n-1)

where

  • E: Total monthly payment (including principal and interest)
  • P: Principal amount of the loan (original amount borrowed)
  • r: Monthly interest rate (annual rate divided by 12)
  • n: Total number of payments (loan term in months)

Consider,

  • P = ₹5,00,000 (Principal amount)
  • Annual Interest Rate = 10%
  • Loan Tenure = 3 years (36 months)
  • r = Annual Interest Rate / 12 = 10 / 12 = 0.00833%

Consider,

E = 500000 × 0.00833 (1 + 0.00833) ^36/ (1+0.00833)36−1 =16,060

The EMI for a loan of ₹5,00,000 with a 10% annual interest rate and tenure of 36 months is approximately ₹16,060 per month.

Factors That Affect Your Business Loan EMI

1. Loan Amount

The larger the loan amount, the higher your EMI will be. Additionally, various loan types come with different EMI structures and repayment terms. It's crucial to borrow only what you need, ensuring you can comfortably manage the repayment.

2. Interest Rate

The interest rate is a crucial determinator for your EMI. A higher interest rate for business loans means a higher EMI, and vice versa. Compare interest rates and hidden charges (if any) from multiple lenders before you choose the one that best suits your needs.

3. Loan Tenure

The loan tenure is the period over which you will repay the loan. A longer tenure means lower EMIs but higher total interest payable. A shorter tenure means higher EMIs but lower total interest payable. It is essential to pick a tenure that balances your monthly cash flow with the total interest outgo.
