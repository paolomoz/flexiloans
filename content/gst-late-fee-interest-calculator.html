What are the Interests and Late Fees under the GST Act?

Under the GST regime, businesses need to file their returns and pay the corresponding taxes within the prescribed due dates. Failure to do so attracts late fees and interest on the outstanding tax liability. The late fee structure is as follows:

  • ₹50 per day of delay for GSTR-3B (maximum ₹5,000)
  • ₹20 per day of delay for GSTR-1 (maximum ₹10,000)
  • ₹200 per day of delay for GSTR-9 and GSTR-9C (maximum ₹10,000)

Additionally, interest is levied at 18% per annum on the unpaid tax amount. The government calculates this interest from the due date until the date of payment. It's important to note that interest is applicable even if you have filed your returns on time but haven't paid the taxes.

How to Calculate GST Late Fees and Interest

  1. Determine the due date for filing the GST return and making the payment.
  2. Note down the actual date of filing and payment.
  3. Calculate the number of days of delay from the due date to the actual date.
  4. Identify the applicable late fee rate based on the type of return (GSTR-3B, GSTR-1, GSTR-9 or GSTR-9C).
  5. Multiply the number of days of delay by the late fee rate to arrive at the total late fee payable.
  6. Calculate the interest by multiplying the outstanding tax amount by 18% per annum and the number of days of delay.

Alternatively, you can use a GST late fee calculator or GST interest calculator to automate these calculations. Simply enter the required details, and the calculator will generate the results instantly.

What is the interest rate under GST?

Along with a late fee for delayed filing of GST returns, the taxpayer also becomes liable to pay interest on the GST. The interest is calculated on the gross GST that the taxpayer is liable to pay. It is calculated from the next day of the due date. To save yourself from paying late fees and interest, try to file your GST returns on the due date only.

FAQs

What is a reverse GST calculator?

A reverse GST calculator is a tool that helps businesses determine the base price of a product or service by removing the GST component from the total price. This is useful when you know the final price (inclusive of GST) and want to calculate the original price before GST.

Is there a maximum limit on the late fees for GSTR-3B?

Yes, the maximum late fee for GSTR-3B is ₹5,000. This means that even if your delay exceeds 100 days (₹50 per day), you will not be liable to pay more than ₹5,000 as a late fee for GSTR-3B.

Can I use a SY GST calculator to determine my late fees and interest?

SY GST calculators are specifically designed for calculating the tax liability under the Composition Scheme (Sugam Yojana). To determine late fees and interest, you should use a dedicated GST late fee calculator or GST interest calculator.

Are there any other consequences of delayed GST payments apart from late fees and interest?

Yes, apart from late fees and interest, delayed GST payments can lead to penalties, prosecution and cancellation of GST registration in severe cases of non-compliance. It's crucial to stay compliant and up-to-date with your GST obligations to avoid these consequences.

Can I claim the input tax credit if I pay my GST liabilities late?

No, you cannot claim input tax credit if you pay your GST liabilities after the due date. To be eligible for input tax credit, you must file your returns and pay your taxes within the prescribed timelines. Late payments can result in the loss of input tax credit for the relevant period.
