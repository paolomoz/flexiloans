Falling second in line after the capital city Bangalore, Dharwad is the second largest city corporation in the state.  Tracing its roots long back to the era of Paleolithic times the city is home to a plethora of cultural and historical hidden charms. Furthermore, this beautiful city lying in the east of majestic “Western Ghats” offers a panoramic view surrounded by numerous lakes, hills and water bodies. In the year 1961, this city was merged with the industrial city, Hubli to form the twin cities of Hubballi-Dharwad. The strategic location of the district on NH 48 connecting the major metropolitan areas  Bengaluru and Pune in north Karnataka, offers a lot of untapped market opportunities.
