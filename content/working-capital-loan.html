What is Working Capital?

Net Working Capital (NWC) is the balance between a company's current assets (cash, accounts receivable, and unpaid bills from customers) and its current liabilities such as debts and accounts payable. It is a widely used measure of an organisation's financial stability and operational efficiency.

Lenders tend to assess a company's ability to use its resources efficiently to fulfil its short-term financial obligations by seeing how well it uses its resources. If a corporation has positive working capital, it generally means that it has sufficient assets to meet its short-term liabilities. On the other hand, if a company has negative working capital, it will likely encounter financial difficulties since its current liabilities are greater than its current assets.

Concept of Working Capital

Working capital helps to maintain a balance between profitability and liquidity in an organisation. Secured Working Capital Loans

Secured working capital loans offer lower interest rates and higher borrowing limits. They involve collateral, which is a valuable asset that you pledge to the lender as a security for the loan. Common forms of collateral include real estate, equipment and inventory.

The value of the collateral that the borrower pledges to the lender determines the approved loan amount. If the borrower doesn't pay back the loan on time, the lender has the right to sell the asset and recoup their investment.

Although secured loans have lower interest rates, they are more challenging to get due to either a lack of available collateral or difficulties with property ownership regulations. Additionally, because assessing and pledging the collateral needs more time, the application process for a secured working capital loan takes considerably longer to complete.

Unsecured Working Capital Loans

An unsecured working capital loan is a type of loan that does not require collateral. Instead, the lender grants loans based on the borrower's creditworthiness, financial history and business strength. Before approving unsecured loans, the bank conducts a comprehensive check of the company's financial records.

Unsecured loans may have a higher interest rate than secured loans, but because there is no collateral involved, the application review and loan disbursal processes are significantly quicker. These loans are available to a wide range of businesses, especially those that do not possess any valuable assets to pledge.
