Types of Business Loans for Sole Proprietors

FlexiLoans offers a diverse selection of credit which comprises:

  • Collateral-free loans:

This loan category is aptly named due to its lack of need for collateral. This is advantageous for businesses that lack tangible assets to offer as collateral.

  • Term loans:

Term loans are a good choice for businesses requiring a significant amount of capital for projects, such as expanding their business. The loan is repaid through EMIs, usually for 2-5 years.

  • Loan against POS:

This loan category is ideal for businesses with lots of credit card transactions. The loan is secured to the POS machine, and the funds can be used for whatever the company needs.

  • Vendor financing:

This loan offers businesses a financing option to pay their suppliers in instalments. It allows them to retain more working capital to allocate to other operations.

  • Line of credit:

This pre-approved line of credit allows businesses to access funds when necessary. It is ideal for companies that have irregular cash flow requirements.

  • Overdraft credit:

An overdraft limit account is a current account that facilitates deposits and withdrawals at any time, subject to a predetermined limit. The lender then applies a fixed interest rate to the borrower's overdraft balance.

Benefits of Business Loan for Sole Proprietors

The advantages of securing a loan for a proprietorship are outlined below:

  1. Financing options to meet your needs

Regarding business finance, proprietors can opt for a more tailored approach. They can borrow only what is necessary rather than a predetermined lump sum.

This financing category is referred to as a 'business line of credit'. It is a fantastic tool for effectively managing financials as it only requires interest payments on the funds or amount borrowed, not the total sum.

  1. Optimize cash flow management.

As a proprietorship, positive cash flow may not always be possible during the startup period or when operating a seasonal business. In such a scenario, bills and salaries may not be paid promptly.

Taking out an advance or a loan can help to ensure a consistent cash flow, even during months of reduced income.

  1. Manage business risks effectively.

Sole proprietors may consider seeking external investor funding to expand their business. In this case, investors can demand a significant stake in the company in addition to the principal amount.

It may result in a reduction of the proprietor's ownership stake to a minority share. An alternative to this situation is obtaining a credit, where repayment of EMI is the only ask.

  1. Facilitate expansion of business.

Business loans offer a range of possibilities, from branching out into new operations to investing in resources. With competitive interest rates, securing significant amounts of capital is possible. This can prove vital for sustaining and growing a proprietorship.
