How is MSME Loan EMI Calculated?

The EMI (equated monthly instalment) for an MSME loan is calculated using a standard formula:

EMI = P * r * (1+r)n / (1+r)(n-1)

Where:

  • P represents the principal amount of the loan.
  • r denotes the monthly interest rate, which is calculated by dividing the annual interest rate by 12.
  • n refers to the tenure of the loan expressed in months.

How to Calculate Interest on an MSME Loan?

The interest on an MSME loan typically depends on two key factors:

  • principal amount
  • loan tenure

The interest rate may differ based on whether the loan is a secured term loan or an unsecured business loan. Here’s a breakdown of how to calculate interest using an example:

  1. Principal Amount: Total loan amount you’ve applied for (e.g. ₹15 lakh)
  2. Annual Interest Rate: 10% per annum
  3. Loan Tenure: 3 years

The formula to calculate interest is:

Total Interest = P * r * n / 100

Where:

  • P is the principal amount
  • r is the interest rate
  • n is the loan tenure (in years)

For a ₹15 lakh business loan with a 10% interest rate over 3 years, the total interest would be:

Total Interest = 15,00,000 * 10 * 3 / 100 = ₹4.5 lakh

This formula gives you the total interest payable. However, the EMI calculator also breaks down the interest into monthly payments, making it easier to manage your cash flow.

Frequently asked questions

1. What is the maximum loan amount I can get under the MSME loan scheme?

The maximum amount under the MSME loan scheme varies from lender to lender. Most lenders offer MSME loans up to ₹5 crore. However, under the government's CGTMSE scheme, MSMEs can avail collateral-free loans up to ₹2 crore.

2. What is the minimum turnover required to apply for an MSME loan?

The minimum turnover required to apply for an MSME loan varies from lender to lender. Most lenders require a minimum turnover of ₹50 lakh per annum. However, some lenders may have lower or higher turnover requirements depending on the amount and the borrower's profile.

3. Can I get an MSME Loan without collateral?

Yes, MSMEs can avail collateral-free loans up to ₹2 crore under the government's CGTMSE scheme. Many lenders also offer unsecured business loans up to ₹50 lakh without any collateral requirement. However, secured loans may offer better interest rates and higher amounts.

4. How long does it take to get an MSME loan approved?

The time taken for approval varies from lender to lender. Some lenders offer instant approval within 59 minutes for loans up to ₹1 crore. Others may take 3-7 working days to approve the loan, depending on the amount and the borrower's credit profile. Lenders also offer doorstep document collection and online loan application to speed up the process.

5. What documents do I need to apply for an MSME Loan?

KYC documents (PAN card, Aadhaar card, etc.) Business registration proof (Udyam Registration certificate, GST certificate, etc.) Income tax returns (ITRs) for the last 2 years GST returns for the last 6 months Bank statements for the last 6 months Ownership proof of the business premises Other financial documents as required by the lender

6. Why is using an EMI calculator important for business loans?

Using an EMI calculator for business loans helps you plan your repayments and manage your finances efficiently. It provides accurate estimates of your monthly payments, ensuring you know how much you need to set aside for loan repayment.

7. What does an MSME Loan EMI Calculator do?

It helps you estimate your monthly EMI based on the loan amount, interest rate, and tenure. This allows you to assess affordability and plan your cash flow before applying.

8. Is the EMI shown by the calculator accurate?

The EMI shown is a rough estimate based on standard lender formulas. Actual EMI may vary slightly after lender evaluation, but the calculator provides a reliable projection.

9. Can I calculate EMI for any type of MSME loan?

Yes, you can use the calculator for working capital loans, machinery loans, expansion loans, or any MSME business loan. The calculation logic remains the same across loan types.

10. Does the calculator include processing fees?

No, the EMI shown does not include processing charges or additional fees. These are displayed separately when you receive a loan offer from the lender.

11. How does changing tenure affect EMI?

A longer tenure reduces the EMI amount but increases total interest paid. A shorter tenure increases EMI but lowers the overall cost of borrowing. The calculator helps you compare both.

12. Why should MSMEs calculate EMI before applying?

It helps avoid repayment stress by understanding monthly outflows in advance. EMI calculation also helps businesses choose the right loan amount and tenure based on cash flow.

13. Can I calculate EMI for different interest rates?

Yes, you can quickly compare EMI values for multiple interest rates. This is useful when evaluating offers from different lenders or checking affordability at different rates.
