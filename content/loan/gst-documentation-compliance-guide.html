Frequently asked questions on GST compliance and documentation

What documents are required for GST registration in India?

A business needs a PAN card, Aadhaar card of the proprietor or authorised signatory, proof of business address, bank account details with a cancelled cheque, and a passport-size photograph. Partnerships and private limited companies must additionally submit a partnership deed or certificate of incorporation. As of 2026, all registrations are processed through the GST portal at gstin.gov.in.

How should a small business respond to a GST notice?

Start by identifying the notice type, as ASMT-10, ADJ-01, and REG-03 each require a different response. Gather the relevant invoices, GSTR filings, and payment challans for the period mentioned and submit your response within the deadline on the GST portal. Ignoring a notice can result in ex-parte orders and penalties. For notices involving large tax demands, consult a GST practitioner or chartered accountant.

What makes a GST invoice compliant?

A compliant invoice must include the supplier's name, address, and GSTIN, a unique invoice number and date, the recipient's GSTIN if registered, HSN or SAC code, taxable value, and the applicable GST amount split into CGST, SGST, or IGST. Missing any of these fields can make the invoice non-compliant and block the recipient's Input Tax Credit claim.

What GST documents do lenders review during a business loan application?

Lenders typically review the GST registration certificate, GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B filings for the last 12 to 24 months, and GST payment challans. These documents help assess turnover consistency, filing regularity, and overall compliance health. Clean GST records are especially important for MSMEs that may not have audited financial statements.

Why does the Input Tax Credit get blocked even after paying the vendor?

ITC eligibility depends on what appears in your GSTR-2B, which is auto-populated from your vendor's GSTR-1 filings. If a vendor does not file on time, the ITC will not reflect in your GSTR-2B and cannot be claimed. Regularly verifying vendor GSTIN compliance on the GST portal before the filing deadline helps prevent this.
