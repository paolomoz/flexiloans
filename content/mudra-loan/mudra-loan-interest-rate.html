Recognizing the importance of the sector, the Government launched the MUDRA scheme in April 2015. The MUDRA (Micro Units Development & Refinance Agency) scheme was aimed at addressing the financing needs of the MSME sector by providing easier access to loans. Since then, the scheme has received a huge response, especially from micro-enterprises.

What is the MUDRA scheme?

The MUDRA scheme, also known as the PMMY (Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana), provides much-needed credit to unorganized small and micro enterprises. Under the scheme, loans up to INR 10 Lakhs can be availed by businesses, based on their specific requirements. Read on to know the different offerings under the MUDRA scheme!

Types of loans offered under MUDRA and and Loan Limits Under Mudra Yojana

Types of loans offered under MUDRA and and Loan Limits Under Mudra Yojana
The three categories under the MUDRA scheme are named 'Shishu', 'Kishore' and 'Tarun'. The names of the categories signify the operational stages of the businesses and the number of funds they require for their operations.

1. Shishu
As the name suggests, 'Shishu' is for financing the entrepreneurs who are about to start their businesses or are at their very early stage of operations. Under the 'Shishu' category, the maximum loan that can be availed of is INR 50,000. If you are thinking of starting or have just started a micro-enterprise, then this category would apply to you.

2. Kishore
The 'Kishore' category provides loans ranging from INR 50,000 to INR 5 Lakhs. If you are a small business owner who is looking at a limited expansion to your operations, you could look at applying under this category. The tenure of the loan may vary according to the lender.

3. Tarun
If you are a business owner looking at raising between INR 5 Lakhs to INR 10 Lakhs, the 'Tarun' category under the MUDRA scheme would be useful for you. This product is particularly for businesses that are looking towards expanding their operation to a larger scale.

The success of the MUDRA scheme

Since its introduction in 2015, MUDRA loans have provided loans to several small businesses that are otherwise denied credit by many traditional lenders. According to the provisional data from the Government of India's MUDRA website, over 4.48 crore MUDRA loans have already been sanctioned during this financial year. MUDRA acts as a refinancing entity and the loans are extended through various lending institutions such as NBFCs and Micro Finance Institutions.

MUDRA loans Eligibility

The primary and intended target for the MUDRA scheme is any small and micro-enterprise. Further eligibility for Mudra loan is as given below:

Business Eligibility:

While applying for the loan, the business entity must be at least operational for 2 years. The monthly turnover of the business should be at least INR 2 Lakhs.

Industry Type:

Considering the nature of micro-enterprises, MUDRA loans are available for entities without any sector or industry limitations. You can apply for financing under the scheme for a variety of industries as below:

Documentation Required

Apart from filling in the application details, the following documentation is required for applying for the MUDRA loan:

Individual Documents:

  • PAN card
  • Address proof - Either one of - Aadhaar / Passport / Voter ID / Rent agreement
  • ITR filings for the past 3 years
  • Good CIBIL score

Business Documents:

  • PAN card of the Business
  • Bank statement of the business for the past 6 months
  • Tax filings of the business for the past 2-3 years
  • Business registration - Shop & Establishment license (S&E) / Gumasta certificate, Udyam Aadhar, GST certificate

Mudra Loan Calculator

For the ones looking for availing Mudra loans, Mudra Loan Interest Calculator can be used to determine the monthly instalments that need to be paid against the loan amount. This may help you plan your expenses and budget well in advance to not skip any instalment. Using this calculator is very easy. Just enter the loan amount, rate of interest and the tenure of the loan, and there you go! This will give you the exact amount that needs to be paid as Mudra Loan interest rate, your loan EMI and total repayment in the form of principal and interest.

The formula for calculating EMIs for business loan is E = P x r x (1+r)n / (1+r)n-1
where,
E = EMI amount
P = Principal amount
r = Rate of interest (per month)
n = Tenure of the loan (in months)

However, manually calculating EMIs using the above formula is prone to mistakes and time-consuming as well. Therefore, the EMI calculator eases EMI calculation - one just has to enter the loan amount, tenure of loan, and interest rate. Let's understand with the help of an example:

Loan Amount: Rs 5,00,000
Tenure of Loan: 3 years
Rate of Interest: 10% p.a.
Therefore, tenure in months would be 36 months (3 years*12 months), and the rate of interest per month shall be 0.833% (10%/12months).

EMI using the above formula: E = 5,00,000 x 0.833/100 x (1+0.833/100)36 / [(1+0.833/100)36-1]
Therefore, the EMI will be Rs 16,134.
