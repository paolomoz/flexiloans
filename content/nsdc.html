What is NSDC?

The National Skill Development Corporation (NSDC) stands for a dedicated body that promotes skill development and vocational training across various sectors in India. It was set up in 2009 by the Ministry of Finance as a public-private partnership to help provide skilled workers to various industries.

The body works with various training partners, both private and government, to create training programmes and initiatives with the goal of equipping the Indian workforce and making them job-ready. It also plays a crucial role in providing financial support and incentives to both training providers and trainees, making skill development more accessible and affordable across the country.

NSDC Schemes and Initiatives: A Detailed Overview

Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY)

Under the umbrella of the NSDC, the Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY) stands out as a prominent scheme aimed at empowering the youth of India through skill training and development. Let's delve into the key aspects of this scheme:

Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Kendra

Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Kendra (PMKK) is a model training centre that has been set up in every district of India to cover all parliamentary seats. Led by the Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship (MSDE), PMKK is part of several skill development programmes run by the NSDC.

Seekho Aur Kamao

Seekho Aur Kamao (Learn and Earn) is an NSDC scheme aimed at providing skill development training and employment opportunities to the youth in minority communities. Under the scheme, individuals can avail of vocational training in various sectors such as textile, electronics, food processing, construction, and healthcare.

Upon completion of the training, candidates can avail of job placements or opportunities to start their own businesses. Seekho Aur Kamao also offers financial support in the form of loans and subsidies to encourage entrepreneurship.

Technical Intern Training Program (TITP)

The Technical Intern Training Program (TITP) is a Japanese government initiative in association with the NSDC that allows Indian nationals to gain technical skills and work experience in Japan. The TITP provides an opportunity for Indian individuals to undergo technical intern training in Japan for a specific period of time. During the training, participants acquire practical skills and knowledge in various fields such as engineering, healthcare, agriculture, hospitality, and manufacturing.

Skilled Workers Arrival Database for Employment Support (SWADES)

SWADES aims to support the employment of Indian workers who are returning from working overseas. At its heart, it is a database that collects and maintains information about returning skilled workers, their educational qualifications, and their job preferences. The database then matches these workers with suitable job opportunities in India, in order to facilitate their reintegration into the workforce.

With its knowledge and large network, the NSDC provides key support for the SWADES scheme, helping people get the training and certification they need.

Specified Skilled Worker (SSW) scheme

The Specified Skilled Worker (SSW) scheme involves a residency status introduced by the government of Japan in April 2019, under which Indian youth can accept a job and stay in Japan. Working in tandem with the NSDC, the Japanese government is looking to attract Indian workers with specialised skills to work in specific industries facing labour shortages in Japan.

Key Projects by NSDC

Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY): Empowering Youth through Skill Development

The Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY), which is a major NSDC programme, is run by the Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship (MSDE). It is a scheme that helps young people learn new skills and get certifications that will help them in their fields.

Skilled Workforce

The main goal of PMKVY is to make sure that every industry has enough skilled workers to meet its changing needs. The programme aims to help young people learn new skills or improve the ones they already have so they can get jobs and make more money. PMKVY tries to close the gap between the number of skilled workers needed and the number of skilled workers available by giving training in different fields and jobs.

Training and Certification

Under PMKVY, eligible individuals can enrol in NSDC courses offered by authorised training centres across the country. These programmes cover a wide range of sectors including healthcare, information technology, retail, hospitality, automotive, construction, and banking. PMKVY ensures that the training provided is of high quality and meets the standards set by the industry.

Placement and Entrepreneurship Support

PMKVY not only helps people get better at what they already know, but it also helps them find jobs with the help of NSDC certificates. The programme collaborates with industry partners and employers to match trained individuals with job opportunities. PMKVY also encourages people to start their own businesses by giving support and advice to people who want to be entrepreneurs.

Monitoring and Evaluation

PMKVY uses strong monitoring and evaluation systems to make sure that the training programmes work well. Training centres are regularly judged on several criteria such as their facilities, the qualifications of their trainers, the results of their training, and the feedback they get from their clients. This monitoring helps make sure that the training is optimal and that the goals of the scheme are met.

Progress and Impact

Since its inception, PMKVY has made a lot of progress in helping young people learn new skills and find jobs. The programme has trained millions of people in many different fields and helped them get good jobs or start their businesses. The success stories of PMKVY recipients show how the programme has helped improve people's lives and contributed to India's social and economic growth.

Rozgar Melas: Connecting Job Seekers and Employers for a Brighter Future

Rozgar Melas, also known as job fairs, are events held by the NSDC to help people find jobs. These events bring together people who are looking for work and people who are looking to hire. Here's why it's important to have Rozgar Melas:

Empowering Job Seekers

Rozgar Melas provide a platform to job seekers to showcase their skills to potential employers. They bring together job seekers, employers, and experts in the field so that all parties can talk in person about job opportunities.

Direct Interaction

Rozgar Melas allow job seekers to talk to their potential employers directly. Employers can also find good candidates, interview them, and assess their skills. This makes it easier to find good candidates and speed up the hiring process.

Wide Range of Opportunities

Rozgar Melas cover a wide range of fields and industries, with job openings in fields like healthcare, IT, retail, hospitality, automobiles, construction, banking, and more. This makes sure that people from different backgrounds and with different skills can find suitable jobs.

Equal Opportunities

Rozgar Melas provide equal opportunities for all job seekers, regardless of their background. They accept people with different levels of education, such as those who have dropped out of school or college, graduated, or have work experience or vocational training. The goal of Rozgar Melas is to give everyone an equal chance to find a good job.

Skill Development

Rozgar Melas emphasise how skills are important in the job market. Job seekers who have received skill training from NSDC and its partners can showcase their skills to employers who are looking for such candidates.

Economic Growth

Rozgar Melas play a very important role in economic growth. By providing a platform for both job seekers and providers, these fairs help in lowering the unemployment rate. At its core, NSDC stands for making a skilled workforce that can meet the needs of businesses and help the country move forward.

Project AMBER: Empowering Youth with Inclusive Skill Development

Project AMBER (full form: Accelerated Mission for Better Employment and Retention) is a joint effort between the NSDC and the Generation India Foundation (GIF). This project is also supported by the Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship. The goal is to create a model for skill development that is sustainable, scalable, improves results, and makes institutions more effective.

Job Opportunities

The main goal of Project AMBER is to give young people in India industry-relevant skills and more job opportunities. The goal of the project is to come up with skill-building programmes that meet the changing needs of the job market and make sure that the training given matches the changing needs of employers. Through this, NSDC is looking to close the gender gap in skill development and give women more power by giving them equal opportunities.

Funding and Duration

The Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship's SANKALP programme and private donations both help fund Project AMBER. Through this, the NSDC's goal is to train 30,000 youth over two years, with a focus on empowering women. The goal is for women to make up 50% of the trainees, which helps promote equal skill development across genders.

Inclusive Skill Development

Project AMBER focuses on making sure everyone has the same chance to learn new skills. By focusing on women trainees, the project hopes to close the skills gap between men and women and make it easier for more women to join the workforce. This not only helps them improve their own lives, but it also helps the country's economy and society develop as a whole.

Industry-Relevant Training Programs

Through Project AMBER, the NSDC is providing skill-building programmes that meet the changing needs of the job market. The project looks for skills that are in demand as well as trends in the industry to make sure the training is in line with what employers want. Through NSDC courses, young people gain a mix of classroom training, hands-on practice, and exposure to the business world. This gives them the technical skills and behavioural attributes they need to be successful in the workplace.

Retention and Continued Support

Project AMBER aims to make sure that trained young people stay in the job market and flourish there. It helps trainees find jobs and build successful careers by giving them support after training, mentorship, and help with placement.
