What is an MSME Loan Interest Rate?

An MSME loan interest rate is the percentage charged by a lender on the borrowed amount, determining the cost of credit for small businesses. It represents the lender’s compensation for providing funds and the risk involved in the transaction.

Interest rates are primarily categorized as fixed or reducing:

  • Fixed Interest Rate: In this type of loan, the interest is calculated on the initial principal amount and remains constant throughout the loan tenure.
  • Reducing Interest Rate: Interest is calculated on the outstanding principal amount, which decreases as the borrower repays the loan.

Factors Affecting MSME Loan Interest Rates

Providing collateral reduces lender risk and may result in lower interest rates.

How to Calculate the MSME Loan Interest Rate

What Goes Into Interest Rate Calculation?

  • Loan Amount: Larger loans may attract lower rates.
  • Loan Tenure: Shorter tenures often get better rates.
  • Credit Score: Higher scores improve loan terms.
  • Type of Loan: Secured loans usually have lower rates.

MSME Loan Interest Rate Formula

EMI = [P × R × (1+R)^N] ÷ [(1+R)^N - 1]

Where:

  • P = Loan Amount
  • R = Monthly Interest Rate
  • N = Loan Tenure in months

Example Calculation

Loan Amount: ₹5,00,000
Annual Interest Rate: 12%
Tenure: 24 months

The approximate EMI would be ₹23,537.
