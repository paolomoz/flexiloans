What is an MSME Loan Interest Rate?

An MSME loan interest rate is the percentage charged by a lender on the borrowed amount, determining the cost of credit for small businesses. It represents the lender’s compensation for providing funds and the risk involved in the transaction.

Interest rates are primarily categorized as fixed or reducing:

  • Fixed Interest Rate: In this type of loan, the interest is calculated on the initial principal amount and remains constant throughout the loan tenure. This ensures predictable repayments, making it easier for businesses to budget and plan their finances.
  • Reducing Interest Rate: In a reducing interest rate structure, interest is calculated on the outstanding principal amount, which decreases as the borrower repays the loan. This means the interest cost reduces over time, providing significant savings for borrowers.

Factors Affecting MSME Loan Interest Rates

Understanding the key MSME loan interest rate factors can help borrowers secure the most competitive rates and reduce financial burdens. Below are the primary factors influencing interest rates:

Credit Score

A high credit score for MSME loans reflects the borrower’s financial discipline and repayment capability. Lenders prefer applicants with strong credit histories, offering lower interest rates as the risk of default diminishes. Maintaining a credit score above 750 can significantly reduce borrowing costs.

Business Turnover

Lenders assess a business’s annual revenue to evaluate its repayment capacity. A higher business turnover demonstrates strong financial performance, which builds lender confidence and leads to more favourable rates. Consistently high revenues may position borrowers for lower loan rates.

Loan Tenure

The length of the loan impacts interest rates. While longer tenures reduce monthly EMIs, they often result in higher total interest paid. On the other hand, shorter tenures may come with better rates, making them cost-effective for businesses capable of faster repayments.

Collateral Offered

Providing security in the form of collateral reduces the lender’s risk, often resulting in lower interest rates. Collateral-backed loans are particularly attractive to lenders, allowing businesses to secure funds at competitive rates. For those unable to offer security, FlexiLoans provides collateral-free loans with low-interest options tailored to MSME needs.

Evaluating these factors and optimising your application can help you secure the most advantageous interest rates, ensuring better cash flow and business growth opportunities.

How to Calculate the MSME Loan Interest Rate

What Goes Into Interest Rate Calculation?

Consider several factors when determining the MSME loan interest rate:

  • Loan Amount: Larger amounts may attract slightly lower rates due to the lender's ability to generate more income despite reduced margins.
  • Loan Tenure: Shorter tenures are less risky for lenders, often resulting in better rates compared to longer repayment periods.
  • Credit Score: A strong credit score reflects reliability, encouraging lenders to offer lower rates to borrowers with high creditworthiness.
  • Type of Loan: Collateral-backed loans typically have lower rates due to reduced risk for the lender. Conversely, collateral-free loans may carry higher rates, as lenders assume more risk.

MSME Loan Interest Rate Formula

The Equated Monthly Installment (EMI) formula calculates repayment amounts with MSME Loan Calculator:

EMI = [P × R × (1+R)^N] ÷ [(1+R)^N-1]

Where:

  • PP = Loan Amount
  • rr = Monthly Interest Rate (Annual Rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100)
  • nn = Loan Tenure (in months)

Example Calculation
Loan Amount: ₹5,00,000
Annual Interest Rate: 12% (Monthly Rate = 1%)
Tenure: 24 months

Substituting these values into the formula:

EMI=5,00,000×0.01×(1+0.01)^24 / (1+0.01)^24−1

The approximate EMI for this loan would be ₹23,537.
