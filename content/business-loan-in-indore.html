Rate Of interest On Business Loan

When we talk about the rate of interest on business loans, it is complicated to put a fixed number on it. Every lender has different interest rates, and they depend on multiple factors. You will have to do thorough research on your lender's interest rate policies. Below we will discuss some of the common factors that will affect your rate of interest on business loan.

Nature of Business

If you look at it, the lender will classify all businesses based on its nature. They will classify it as a priority and non-priority sector, where the latter will have a higher interest rate.

Business Existence

If your business has existed for a longer time, it will be better for you, as you will get a lower interest rate. There is a higher risk with the new business, which is why the interest rates are also high.

Credit Score

The credit score of a business is similar to the credit score of an individual. It depends upon your past credit payments. A good credit score will get you a loan with a lower interest rate. If your credit score is not acceptable, then you might get a higher rate. Sometimes it so happens that you might not get anything at all, and your application might get rejected.

Business Turnover

The creditors look at your business turnover so that they can judge the profits the company is making monthly. If the business is making a good revenue, you can get a loan at a lower interest rate. If not, then you might end up with a high-interest rate for your loan.
