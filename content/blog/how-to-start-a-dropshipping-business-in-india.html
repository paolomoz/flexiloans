What Constitutes Dropshipping

Dropshipping is an e-commerce business model. In this model, when a customer orders the product, the retailer purchases the item from a third party—usually a wholesaler or manufacturer—who then ships the product directly to the customer.

How it works

  1. The process starts when a customer orders the product on the retailer’s website. 
  2. The retailer shares the order details to the supplier, who could be a wholesaler, distributor, or manufacturer. 
  3. The supplier charges the retailer the wholesale price, plus a dropshipping fee. 
  4. The retailer, who has already received payment from the customer, keeps the profit margin. 
  5. The supplier then fulfils the order directly to the customer under the retailer’s business name.

Benefits for MSMEs

  • This model does not require the products to be purchased upfront, hence you don’t need a large amount of term loan to start. 
  • You can quickly adapt to market trends by adding or removing products from the business’ website without needing additional business loan for inventory
  • You don’t need to worry about logistical challenges and additional capital requirements when order volume increases. The supplier handles the extra workload.

Steps to start A dropshipping business in India

1. Choosing Right Products

Product selection is an important aspect of dropshipping business. A thorough market research is important to understand region-wise consumer preference in India. Use tools like Google Trends, Amazon Best Sellers, and social media analytics to identify popular items. For example, In southern India, stainless steel kitchenware is preferred over non-stick options. A dropshipper targeting this region with a range of high-quality stainless steel pots and pans could tap into this market.

2. Finding Reliable Suppliers

The success of your dropshipping business depends on your suppliers’ reliability. In India, you have both domestic and international options. Domestic suppliers offer advantages like faster shipping times and easier communication. International suppliers offer higher profit margins but longer shipping times. 

You could use a business loan to secure inventory from domestic suppliers for fast-moving items, and dropshipping slower-moving, higher-margin products from international suppliers. For fast-moving items, you can stock inventory from domestic suppliers by taking a business loan. For slower-moving, higher-margin products you can consider dropshipping from overseas suppliers. This strategy allows you to balance your cash flow and business loan repayments.

3. Setting Up an Online Store

Given that a significant portion of Indian shoppers use smartphones, investing in a visually appealing, mobile-responsive theme is important. A business loan can cover costs for premium themes, apps, and possibly a developer to customize your store. For instance, a store dropshipping ethnic wear could invest in a theme that showcases product details and offers a virtual try-on feature, enhancing the customer experience.

4. Legal Considerations

In India, registering your business and obtaining a GST (Goods and Services Tax) number is mandatory for online sellers. The GST threshold for mandatory registration is an annual turnover of ₹40 lakhs for most goods. Also, it’s important to understand import duties and customs regulations if you’re dropshipping from international suppliers. Some dropshipper use business loan to consult with customs brokers. This ensures smooth importation and avoids unexpected costs that could reduce business profit margin.

The goal should be sustainable growth, not just increased sales volume. For example, if a popular beauty product is priced at ₹999 on a competitor platform, consider pricing yours at ₹949. Overcome common challenges

Dropshipping has emerged as a lucrative business model for new entrepreneurs. However, you will have to navigate through common challenges such as longer shipping times, supplier relationships, and a competitive market. Let us understand about these challenges and strategies to tackle them.

Dealing with longer shipping times

Longer shipping times are one of the primary challenges of the dropshipping business model. A few strategies to tackle this challenge are discussed below.

  1. Be upfront about shipping times on your product pages, checkout process, and order confirmation emails.
  2. Consider partnering with suppliers who offer faster shipping methods or use a quick business loan to buy popular items in advance, and store them in a small warehouse.
  3. Customers are often willing to wait longer for unique or personalized items. Use a business loan to invest in marketing of these products and highlight their uniqueness instead of delivery speed.

Managing supplier relationships

The success of your dropshipping business depends on your suppliers. A delayed or defective product can lead to negative reviews. A few strategies to tackle this challenge are discussed below.

  1. Don’t put all your eggs in one basket. Use a portion of your business loan to order sample products from multiple suppliers. This way, if one supplier fails, you have backups. 
  2. With the help of a business loan, negotiate better terms with suppliers. This could mean higher dropshipping discounts, branded packaging, or priority processing. 

Standing out in a competitive market

Dropshipping business is highly competitive and populated because of low entry barriers. To thrive, you need to have a differentiated strategy.

  1. Instead of a generic store, offer the same product with some degree of differentiation. For example Instead of a generic home decor store, specialize in eco-friendly home decor.
  2. Excellent customer service can set you apart in your dropshipping business. Invest your quick business loan in a responsive customer service tool or a small team. 
  3. Use your business loan to invest in a multilingual website or region-specific product lines. Regional touches on your website gives a personalized experience during festival seasons.
