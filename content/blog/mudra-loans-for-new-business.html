What are Mudra Loans for New Business?

The Mudra Loan for new business is part of the Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY), a government initiative that began in 2015 to provide financial assistance to non-corporate, non-farm small/micro enterprises. The acronym ‘MUDRA’ stands for Micro Units Development and Refinance Agency Ltd., which is responsible for managing the scheme. Mudra Loan for new business seek to empower small companies by providing financing that can be utilized for multiple operational expenses, such as acquiring machinery, expanding operations, or managing working capital.

What are the Different Modes of Funding Under the New Business Loan PM Yojana?


The Mudra Loan for new business scheme encompasses three distinct types of financing, accommodating the diverse monetary requirements of companies at various stages of development. These are:

  1. Shishu (up to ₹50,000):
    • Target Audience: Startups and micro-enterprises in their early phases of operation.
    • Purpose: Designed for enterprises that require a small amount to begin operations, like opening a shop, acquiring basic equipment, or paying early working capital needs.
  2. Kishor (₹50,001 to ₹5,00,000):
    • Target audience: Established organisations aiming to expand their operations.
    • Purpose: Ideal for companies seeking quick business loan cash to expand operations, invest in innovative equipment, or enhance production capacity.
  3. Tarun (between ₹5,00,001 to ₹10,00,000):
    • Target Audience: Established companies seeking significant investment for development or diversification.
    • Purpose: Ideal for enterprises with great growth potential that require a huge infusion of funds to invest in high-cost machinery, enter new markets, or execute large-scale projects.

What are the Mudra Loan Eligibility Documents for New Businesses?

The following documents are required when applying for Mudra Loan for new business:

  • Proof of Identity:
    • Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Voter ID, Driving License, or Passport.
  • Proof of Address:
    • Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, Utility Bills (Electricity, Water, Gas), or Rental Agreement.
  • Business Plan:
    • A detailed business plan outlining your idea, target market, revenue projections, and how you plan to use the amount.
  • Proof of Business:
    • Registration certificate, GST registration, or any other proof of the organisation entity.
  • Bank Statements:
    • Bank account statements for the last 6-12 months.
  • Income Proof:
    • Income Tax Returns, financial statements, or any other proof of income, if applicable.
  • Photographs:
    • Passport-sized photographs of the applicant.
  • Other Relevant Documents:
    • Any other documents required by the bank, such as licenses, permits, or partnership deeds.

How to Apply for a Mudra Loan?

The Mudra Loan for new business application process is very simple and a straightforward. You can apply using both online and offline methods. Here’s a step-by-step guide:

Offline Application Process

  • Step 1: Branch Visit- Visit the nearest bank, MFI, NBFC or Seva Kendra that provides Mudra Loans.
  • Step 2: Fill out the application form with all the required details and submit.
  • Step 3: Attach the required documents like as identity proof, address proof, and a business plan with the application form.
  • Step 4: The creditor will verify the papers and determine your eligibility. If all is in order, your application will be approved.
  • Step 5: Once approved, the MSME loan amount will be disbursed to your bank account within a week.

Online Application Process

  • Step 1: Visit the bank’s, NBFC or MFI’s official website and apply for Mudra Loan for new business.
  • Step 2: Fill out the online application form with all of your personal and business information, with the required amount.
  • Step 3: Upload any necessary documents, such as proof of identity, address, and business plan.
  • Step 4: The creditor will check your documentation and eligibility. You may be contacted for more information if necessary.
  • Step 5: Once approved, the MSME business loan money is deposited in your bank account.

Looking for an alternative to a Mudra loan?

If the Mudra Loan does not satisfy your needs, here are some alternate funding options:

Funding from Private Banks: Many private banks provide required financing at competitive interest rates and flexible terms.

Micro-Finance Institutions (MFIs): MFIs offer modest funding, prioritising rural regions and MSMEs, with minimal documentation and speedy payouts.

Peer-to-Peer (P2P): P2P links borrowers to individual lenders, suitable for individuals who may not be eligible through traditional sources.

Venture Capital and Angel Investors: If you have a fast-growing organisation, you should consider obtaining investment from venture capital companies or angel investors who offer funds in return for shares in your company.

Crowdfunding: Crowdfunding enables you to raise a modest amount of money from a large number of individuals. This can be an efficient approach for new firms, especially if it offers a distinctive product or service.
