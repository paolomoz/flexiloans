How to Lock the Data on Your Aadhaar Card

Locking your card data prevents unauthorized access. The Unique Identification Authority of India (UIDAI) provides a feature to lock your number, ensuring no one can use it without your consent. Follow these steps to secure your information:

  1. Go to the official UIDAI website and select the ‘Aadhaar Lock and Unlock Service’ option.
  2. Enter your UID number and the captcha code. You’ll receive a One-Time Password (OTP) on your registered mobile number.
  3. Use the OTP to log in and access the locking feature.
  4. Select the option to lock your Aadhaar. Once you lock, no one can use it for authentication until you unlock it.

Locking your card ensures no one can misuse your identity for transactions or other activities. It’s a proactive step to enhance your security, especially if you suspect any compromise of your details.

How to Unlock Information on Your Aadhaar Card

Unlocking your data allows you to use it for services or transactions as needed. Here’s how you can unlock your card:

  1. Return to the ‘Aadhaar Lock and Unlock Service’ section on the UIDAI website.
  2. Enter your UID number and captcha code, and receive the OTP on your registered mobile number.
  3. Use the OTP to log in and proceed to unlock your Aadhaar.
  4. Choose the ‘Unlock’ option. This temporarily unlocks your Aadhaar, allowing its use for a specified period or until you decide to lock it again.

Unlocking lets you complete necessary transactions while maintaining control over your personal information.

Why You Should Consider Locking Your Aadhaar Card


Locking adds an extra layer of security to your identity. Here’s why it’s beneficial:

  1. Prevent Unauthorised Access: Locking prevents anyone from using your Aadhaar for authentication without your knowledge.
  2. Protect Sensitive Data: Your Aadhaar card links to various services, including financial accounts. Locking it safeguards this information.
  3. Reduce Fraud Risk: With identity theft on the rise, locking reduces the likelihood of fraud, such as unauthorised financial transactions.
  4. Gain Peace of Mind: Locking it also ensures you maintain control of your identity, especially when you have security concerns.

Can Aadhaar Card Be Misused?

Yes, someone can misuse your card if they get hold of it. Misuse can take several forms, including identity theft, fraudulent transactions, and unauthorised access to services. Here are some ways that someone might exploit it:

  1. Identity Theft: Fraudsters may impersonate you, using your UID number to access financial accounts or apply for services in your name.
  2. Unauthorised Transactions: Gaining access to your eAadhaar-linked accounts enables unauthorised financial activities, potentially resulting in significant losses.
  3. Misuse of Government Benefits: Fraudsters can claim government benefits linked to your Aadhaar, depriving you of your rightful assistance.
  4. Opening New Accounts: Your details could be used to open new bank accounts, apply for credit cards, or even take out loans, leaving you responsible for debts you didn’t incur.

How Can I Know If Someone Is Using My Aadhaar Card?

Monitoring your card usage helps you detect any unauthorised activity. Here’s how to check if someone is using it:

  1. UIDAI provides a way to review all authentications done using your UID number.
    • Access the UIDAI website.
    • Log in using your Aadhaar and OTP.
    • Navigate to the ‘Aadhaar Authentication History’ section.
    • Examine the list of transactions.
  2. Regularly check your Aadhaar-linked bank accounts for any suspicious transactions.
  3. If you receive government benefits tied to your Aadhaar, ensure there are no unusual claims in your name.
  4. Many banks offer transaction alerts for Aadhaar-linked accounts. Set these up to stay informed.

Report any suspicious activity immediately to UIDAI and the relevant authorities. Quick action can prevent further misuse and protect your identity.

Is It Safe to Give an UID number to Anyone?

Sharing your UID number requires caution. While it’s necessary for services like applying for loans or opening bank accounts, you should be aware of potential risks. Here’s what to consider:

  1. Only share your Aadhaar with trusted entities that require it for legitimate purposes. Verify the authenticity of the request before providing your details.
  2. Never share your UID on public platforms, such as social media or unsecured websites.
  3. If possible, use a masked Aadhaar card to hide the first eight digits of your UID number. This reduces the risk of misuse while allowing you to provide necessary information.
  4. When possible, use other identity documents like your PAN card or voter ID.

Being cautious about sharing your UID number helps prevent potential misuse.

What If Someone Knows My UID number?

Knowing your UID number doesn’t automatically put your information at risk, but taking precautions is wise. Here’s what you can do:

  1. Locking prevents unauthorized use, even if someone knows your number.
  2. Regularly check your Aadhaar authentication history to ensure no one is using it without your consent.
  3. Monitor your Aadhaar-linked accounts and benefits for any unusual activity.
  4. If you suspect misuse, report it to UIDAI and the relevant authorities.

These steps help mitigate risks associated with someone knowing your UID number.

Can Someone Use My Aadhaar Card Without My Permission?

While it’s difficult for someone to use your card without your permission, it’s not impossible. Here’s how unauthorised usage might occur:

  1. Forged Documents: Fraudsters could forge documents using your UID number for illegal activities.
  2. Unauthorised Access: If someone gains access to your UID number, they could potentially access your accounts or benefits linked to your card.
  3. Phishing Scams: Scammers may use phishing tactics to obtain your Aadhaar details and misuse them.

To protect yourself, always verify requests for your UID number, monitor your accounts, and report any suspicious activity.

Conclusion

Protecting your Aadhaar card from misuse is crucial to safeguarding your identity. By locking it, monitoring its usage, and being cautious when sharing your UID number, you minimise the risk of unauthorised access and fraud. If you ever suspect misuse, take immediate action by reporting it to UIDAI and securing your information.
