GST Slabs in India and GST Rates Chart

Let’s understand what GST is to better our understanding of the GST rates list.

The Goods and Services Tax (GST) is an indirect tax levied on goods and services purchased in India. With the exception of a few state taxes, GST has absorbed all other indirect state and central taxes. As a result, it is a multi-staged and all-encompassing tax. It replaced the previous point-of-origin taxation policy with a point-of-destination or consumption taxation policy. Because it is a multi-stage tax, it is levied at each stage of production, but it is refunded to all parties involved in production except the final consumer.

The revenue from an intra-state business transaction is split 50-50 between the centre and the state. For example, if Maharashtra earns Rs. 10 crores in tax revenue, the state keeps Rs. 5 crores while the central government receives Rs. 5 crores. The GST Act grants the state the authority to import goods or services in order to collect the tax in any interstate transaction involving the movement of goods and services across state lines. Consider the following scenario: In an inter-state transaction, goods are exported from Maharashtra to Gujarat. In this case, Gujarat is the state that receives the goods and collects the taxes, which are then divided equally among the centre and state.

There are 4 extant GST slabs in India, 5%, 12%, 18% and 28%. There’s also the 0% exemption slab used in financial accounting. Slab Rate of GST Revision in 23rd GST Council Meeting

The 23rd GST council meeting convened on the 10th of November 2017 and made several changes. One of them was that GST Rates for 178 items were reduced from 28% to 18%. Here are some of the modifications made in the GST law in this meeting:

  • The GST rate for manufacturers and traders under the composition scheme is 1%.
  • Decreased from 28% to 18%: Shampoo, perfume, tiles, and watches as of November 15th, 2017.
  • The proportion of wet grinders and tanks has been reduced from 28% to 12%.
  • The GST rate of condensed milk, refined sugar, and diabetic food reduced from 18% to 12%.
  • The percentage rate of desiccated coconut, idli dosa batter, and coir products has been reduced from 12% to 5%.
  • The percentages of duar meal, khandsari sugar, and dried vegetables have been reduced from 5% to 0%.
  • The GST rate is 5% for restaurants within hotels and rooms with a room tariff of less than Rs. 7,500.
