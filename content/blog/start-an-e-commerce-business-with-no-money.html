Types of E-commerce/Online Business Models

E-commerce has brought about tremendous change in how businesses function and connect with their customers. These days, just having a website and a few products isn’t enough; you need a good business model to ensure success.

An e-commerce business model is a blueprint that gives your plan structure and direction to help it generate the desired revenue. The e-commerce models below will give you valuable insights and help you choose the right one to ensure long-term success for your online business. 

1. Affiliate Marketing Model

Affiliate marketing is very popular these days among content creators and influencers. Under this model, you promote the products and services of other businesses on your online platform. You can earn a commission from sales generated via your referral link as an affiliate.

2. Retail Model

Also known as the Direct-to-Consumer (D2C) model, it directly sells products to customers through the online store. The business has complete control over every aspect, i.e., from inventory to sales to customer service.

3. Dropshipping Model

When you use this model while starting your online business with no money, you need not stock up on inventory. The suppliers you partner with will handle inventory management and shipping.
As a seller, you will buy the products from your supplier only when you receive an order. This enables starting an online business from home without significant costs.

4. Marketplace Model

Under this model, you use various marketplace platforms that connect buyers with sellers and facilitate transactions. The business acts as an intermediary and offers sellers the platform to display and sell their products. Some good examples of this model are Amazon and eBay.

5. Subscription Model

As a business, you offer products/services on a subscription basis, i.e., monthly, quarterly, semi-annually, or annually, per the customer’s requirement. In return, customers pay subscription fees to access customised products/services and exclusive content.
This free online business model encourages customer loyalty and gives you a predictable revenue stream.

6. Peer-to-Peer (P2P) Model

This business model allows direct transactions between individuals via an online platform. There is no intermediary in this model. A good example of this would be a platform for freelance services or sharing accommodations/vehicles.

7. Crowdfunding Model

Crowdfunding is quite a popular way to raise funds. Entrepreneurs use this model to raise capital from people who trust their idea/product. This group receives exclusive rewards in return or even gets early access to the products.

8. Private Labelling Model

Also known as White labelling, under this model, you purchase products from a manufacturer, rebrand them and sell them as your product.

9. Hybrid Model

You can combine different models to create a customised business model that suits your online business requirement. For instance, you can combine a subscription model with a D2C model.

How to Start Online Business in India with No Money?

Starting an online store without any money? It seems impossible and quite a challenge. However, by using your skills and the free resources and tools available online, and your skills, you can tap into the online business opportunities available and make it a success too.

To get you started, we have prepared a step-by-step guide to help you launch your online business from home without any investment.

  1. Determine the Right Approach
    The first step is identifying whether you want to sell a physical product or a service to your target audience. Consider your expertise, skills, and interests, and identify a profitable niche.
    Services requiring minimal or no investment include writing, consulting, virtual assistance, or graphic designing. Another cost-effective and profitable business is developing and selling digital products such as online courses or e-books.
  2. Conduct Market Research
    Once you have identified the niche area for your free online business, conduct market research on your competitors and their product/service offerings, current market trends, and the demand for your product/service.
    This helps identify gaps and opportunities to create a unique selling proposition (USP) for your business. You can also research funding institutions that offer business loans that you could avail of to expand your business in the future.
    For instance, FlexiLoans offers collateral-free loans requiring minimal documentation and an easy online process.
  3. Create a Business Plan
    A well-crafted plan will guide you through starting an online store with no money and your business’ growth blueprint. A business plan outlines your objectives and details your company and the products/services offered, its marketing strategies, logistics, operations, and budget.
    So, to start a business online, start working on your business plan immediately!
  4. Online Presence
    Choose from various free or affordable e-commerce platforms like Shopify, WooCommerce, or Wix to develop your online business website and build your online presence.
    Most of these platforms offer many free templates that you can customise to display your brand identity. While creating your website, make it SEO-friendly and ensure the design is mobile-friendly and user-friendly.
  5. Register Your Business
    It is important to obtain the necessary licences and permits in compliance with Indian regulations. Registering your Online Retail Business adds credibility, protects your brand, and helps with future expansions.
    You can enter contracts, safeguard your intellectual property (IP) rights, and ensure your business functions smoothly once you register.
  6. Utilise Affordable Tools
    Starting an online store with no money calls for caution regarding unwanted expenditures. You can utilise free or low-cost tools for managing inventory, customer relations, and accounting. Examples include Zoho Inventory, HubSpot CRM, and Wave Accounting.
  7. Marketing and Promotion
    Create engaging content to showcase your products. Use platforms like Facebook, Instagram, and Pinterest to generate traffic and sales without spending on advertising.
    For those starting an online store without money, use free marketing tools like Canva for social media graphics, Mailchimp for email marketing, and Google Analytics to track performance. Attract customers without overspending on advertising.
  8. Leverage Local Marketplaces
    Take advantage of popular Indian e-commerce platforms like Flipkart, Amazon India, and Snapdeal. These marketplaces provide access to a vast customer base and can boost your online retail business.
  9. Continuous Learning
    Stay updated on industry trends, marketing tactics, and consumer preferences. Ensure you dedicate time to learning new skills and use feedback to modify your online business strategies.
  10. Collaborate and Network
    Collaboration and networking are other important steps in starting an online business without money. You must build a good relationship with other businesses, influencers, and entrepreneurs in your business niche. You can also collaborate with others on specific projects or indulge in cross-promotions.
    This helps in expanding the presence of your free online business without any financial investment. By following the guide and staying committed to your vision, your business will soon thrive. Starting an online business without investment takes time and effort, but persistence and dedication will lead you to success.
