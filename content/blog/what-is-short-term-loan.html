What is a Short-Term Business Loan

A short-term business loan is a financial product that provides quick funding for urgent business needs. Its repayment period ranges from a few months to one year. These loans help businesses cover immediate needs like cash flow gaps, urgent expenses, or inventory stocking. While these loans usually get approved quickly, they may come with higher interest rates.

Documents Required for Short-Term Loans

To apply for a short-term business loan, you’ll need to submit certain documents, such as:

  • Business Registration Documents: Certificate of incorporation, partnership deed, or proprietorship registration to prove legal business existence.
  • Business Plan: Objectives, financials, market analysis, and repayment strategy to show business viability.
  • KYC Documents: ID and address proofs like Aadhaar, PAN, passport, voter ID, or license for all owners.
  • Financial Statements: Profit & Loss, Balance Sheet, and cash flow statements from past years to reveal financial health.
  • Bank Statements: Recent 6-12 months of bank transactions to demonstrate cash flow.
  • GST Documents: GST registration and returns for applicable periods for compliance.
  • Proof of Ownership/Lease: Ownership deed or lease agreement to confirm business location.
  • Collateral Documents: Property or asset proofs if applying for a secured loan.
  • Business Licenses: Necessary permits based on industry and regulations.
  • Debt Obligations: Current loan details to evaluate repayment ability.
  • Projected Financials: Revenue, expenses, and cash flow forecasts to gauge impact of the loan.
  • Business References: References to vouch for credibility and reputation.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Business’s Financial Health: Lenders review past profits, expenses, and debts. A turnover of ₹2,00,000 per month is generally enough for approval.
  • Credit Score: A high score from timely debt repayments can mean instant approval and lower rates, while a low score may lead to denials or higher rates.
  • Loan Amount and Tenure: Bigger loans or longer repayment periods carry more risk. Shorter terms increase EMIs, so use an EMI calculator to budget.
  • Business Vintage: A longer business history suggests stability and can ease approval. Even a one-year vintage may qualify with strong financials.
  • Business Type: Stable industries get lower rates and faster approval. Riskier sectors might face higher rates or denials.

Interest Rates and Charges

The business loan interest rate for short-term loans is generally higher than long-term loans due to the shorter repayment tenure and higher risk perception. Interest rates can range from 15% to 30% per annum, depending on the lender and your business’s risk profile.

Types of Short-Term Business Loans

  1. Working Capital Loans: Businesses use these loans to cover daily expenses like salaries, rent, and inventory.
  2. Invoice Financing: This option helps businesses access funds tied up in unpaid invoices, ensuring smooth cash flow.
  3. Merchant Cash Advances: Businesses receive upfront funds in exchange for a percentage of future sales, making it ideal for those with variable revenue.
  4. Bridge Loans: Companies rely on these loans to secure immediate funding while waiting for permanent financing.
  5. Business Credit Lines: These provide a revolving credit facility that businesses can draw from as needed, paying interest only on the amount used.
  6. Equipment Financing: Designed to help businesses purchase or lease equipment, with the loan secured against the equipment itself.
