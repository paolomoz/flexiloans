What is a Small Business Loan?

A small business loan is a type of MSME loan that offers loans of financing to small businesses. These small business loans specifically cater to small businesses that are in their early phase of growth and expansion. As a business owner, it helps you grow your business, buy new assets and pay salaries.

Where to Spend Small Business Loan?

Unlike traditional loans that help cover day-to-day expenses, this business expansion loan gives you a lump sum to fund major milestones in your business journey. Here is how you can use a small business loan in India:

  • Open a new store, branch, or franchise
  • Purchase new equipment, assets, machinery, inventory or raw materials
  • Invest in marketing campaigns and advertising expenses
  • Hire skilled staff or manage payroll
  • Manage cash flow during off-seasons
  • Launch a new product, service, or offering
  • Meeting urgent or unexpected financial obligations

What is a Working Capital Loan Meaning?

A working capital loan is a type of loan that offers financing for day-to-day operations of a business. It helps business owners cover costs of salaries, accounts payable and more. It’s designed to help you manage day-to-day operational expenses, especially when cash flow fluctuates.

Where to Spend Working Capital Loans?

Every business, on average, has a number of daily expenses they need to cover. All these expenses add to a big sum with every passing day. This is why the credit guarantee cover for micro and small businesses was raised from ₹5 crore to ₹10 crore in the Indian government’s 2025 budget. Here is how you can use a working capital loan in India:

  • Paying vendors or suppliers
  • Covering rent and utility bills
  • Paying payroll during the off-season
  • Buying raw materials or inventory in bulk
  • Managing day-to-day operational expenses
  • Settling outstanding vendor bills
  • Covering seasonal cash flow gaps
  • Meeting short-term marketing and advertising needs
  • Repairing or servicing machinery and equipment

When Should You Choose a Small Business Loan?

A small business loan is ideal when your plans are long-term and growth-oriented. Let’s say you’re an MSME business owner running a thriving bakery in Pune, and you want to open a second outlet. You need funds for rent, equipment, branding, and hiring. That’s where a business growth loan steps in.

  • Expanding your product line or opening new branches
  • Investing in heavy machinery or infrastructure
  • Hiring high-cost talent or consultants
  • You have a clear revenue plan that will repay the loan over time

The best loan for business expansion is one with a longer tenure, a flexible EMI structure, and an interest rate that fits your budget. If your financial records are clean and your growth trajectory is solid, this is a great funding tool to scale up without giving away equity.

When Should You Choose a Working Capital Loan?

Not every business challenge is about growth. Sometimes, it’s just about making ends meet between cash inflows. That’s where working capital for small businesses becomes a lifesaver as it helps you cover day-to-day expenses.

Imagine you run a garment export business. Your orders are seasonal, and payments come in 60–90 days after shipment, but you still need to pay workers, buy raw materials, and keep things running. A managed cash flow loan helps you bridge that gap. This loan type is ideal for the following:

  • Your business is seasonal or invoice-based
  • You face delays in customer payments
  • You need quick liquidity to grab a bulk deal from a vendor
  • Your revenues fluctuate month-to-month
