Introduction   

Every resident of India is given an Aadhaar, a 12-digit unique identity number issued by the Government of India’s Unique Identification Authority of India (UIDAI). Aadhaar Card is also a biometric documentary proof that enters your data into a database maintained by the government. An Aadhaar card is linked with the Income Tax Department’s Permanent Account Number (PAN) and voluntarily with Election ID.

As a biometric identity card, Aadhaar is used for transferring various government welfare scheme benefits to genuine beneficiaries. Every resident of India is eligible to apply for an Aadhaar number under the Aadhaar Act 2016.

A person is considered a resident if they have lived in India for at least 182 days in the year before the date of their application for enrolment. If you meet this requirement, you must provide specific documentation to prove your eligibility for Aadhaar.

The process is pretty simple, and there aren’t many documents that need to be provided at UIDAI-authorised centres. 

There are four categories of documentation required for an Aadhaar card. You must present your identity proof, address proof, date of birth, and proof of relationship.

Identification documents required for an Aadhaar card

When you go to an Aadhaar enrollment centre, ensure to bring one of the following documents to serve as your identity proof:

  • Election ID card. 
  • Passport. 
  • Ration card.
  • Identification cards issued by government authorities.
  • PAN card.
  • ID card with a photo from a financial institution.
  • Driving licence. 
  • Weapons licence.
  • A valid photo ID issued by a recognised academic institution.

Address proof documents required for Aadhaar enrolment

For both new and existing Aadhaar, a set of documents is required for a change of address in the Aadhaar card. The Aadhaar card is sent to the address provided by the applicant on the application, so these documents must be accurate.

The applicants may not receive their Aadhaar cards if they do not provide their postal address proof or update it. The UIDAI accepts the following documents as address proof:

  • A bank statement with a photograph.
  • A valid passport.
  • Ration card. 
  • Postal service passbook.
  • Voter ID card.
  • Driving licence.
  • Other identity cards with pictures issued by the government.
  • Landline telephone bills of the residence for the past three months (if applicable).
  • Electricity bills for the past three months of the place of residence.
  • The water bill (should not be older than three months).
  • Property tax receipt of your place of residence for the past three months.
  • A copy of your insurance policy.
  • Your credit/debit/any other banking card statement for the past three months
  • Government PSU issued a service picture identity card with the address.

Documents required to confirm the date of birth for Aadhaar registration?

The acceptable documents as proofs of date of birth for Aadhaar card registration are as follows:

  • Certificate of birth.
  • Passport.
  • SSLC Book/Certificate.
  • PAN card.
  • Marksheet issued by any recognised university or educational board.
  • Order for State or Central Pension payment.
  • I-Card or certificate with a person’s DOB and a photo that a government official has attested. It includes, but is not limited to, a Caste Certificate, Domicile Certificate, Arms Licence, Ration card, Marriage Certificate, Pensioner card, etc.
  • A photo ID card with the person’s date of birth issued by a recognised educational institution.

Documents required as proof of relationship for Aadhaar enrolment?

The following documents are needed to verify the applicant’s link to the family’s head while applying.

  • Job Card for MNREGA.
  • Ration card.
  • Birth certificate and passport.
  • Health card issued by the government.
  • Retirement ID.
  • A canteen card for soldiers.
  • Certified marriage licence.
  • State or federal government-issued documentation of family entitlement.

Documents required for children’s Aadhaar enrolment

Children as young as newborns and up to the age of 15 are all entitled to receive an exclusive Aadhaar card. There are two age-based categories for children’s Aadhaar cards. Children from each group must therefore present unique documentation to verify their legitimacy.

Documents required for Aadhaar card for a child below five years

Children’s Baal Aadhaar are distinct from those of adults. The enrolment procedure can be completed with less paperwork. It contains:

  • The child’s birth certificate and the parents’ or guardians’ Aadhaar cards.
  • Since biometrics cannot be collected before the age of five, the card will be connected to the parent’s Aadhaar.

However, children above the age of five must present some additional documentation.

Documents required for an Aadhaar card for a Child Above 5 Years 

Children older than five are mature enough to provide their biometrics. As a result, the representative at the enrolling office collects the following documents in addition to the biometrics of their fingers and irises.

  • The children’s birth certificates and their parents’ Aadhaar numbers.
  • Student ID card issued by a recognised institution.

If they already have a Baal Aadhaar card, the Aadhaar number will remain the same in this situation. New biometric data will be gathered to update the Aadhaar.

Here are some of the documents required for the Jan Aadhaar Card:

  • Identification document (UIDAI Aadhaar, ration card, or electricity bill)
  • Age-verification evidence
  • Address verification (PAN card, passport, or Aadhaar card)
  • Real contact information

Documents required for amending Aadhaar after marriage

As issued by the marriage registrar, the marriage certificate is the document required for name and address change in the Aadhaar card after marriage. This is the simplest document to use as simultaneous proof of marriage and name change.

Newlyweds can use it and also use it to update other pertinent paperwork. In addition to the marriage certificate, you can use the following documents for name changes:

  • PAN card
  • Passport
  • Gazette of India notification.
  • Driving licence. 
  • Voter identification card
  • Bank ATM cards with photos 
  • Credit cards with photos 
  • Any photo identity cards issued by the Indian government

What documents are required for updating an Aadhaar Card?

For updating or changing details in an Aadhaar Card, you have to furnish the same documents used for your enrolment along with a copy of your old Aadhaar card.

In case you have to change or update your name or place of residence on the Aadhaar card, you will have to submit the supporting documents.
