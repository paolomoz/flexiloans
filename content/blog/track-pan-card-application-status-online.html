How to Track Your PAN Card Application Status Online?

Applying for a Permanent Account Number (PAN) card is crucial for financial transactions and taxation purposes in India. Once you have submitted your PAN application, the next step is to track your PAN card status. Knowing how to check your PAN application status can help you stay informed about your PAN card delivery. Here’s a comprehensive guide on how to check PAN card status online.

Why Is It Important to Track Your PAN Card Application Status?

Tracking PAN card status is essential to:

  • Know if your PAN application was received and if it is under process.
  • Check if there were any issues with your application or documents.
  • Get an estimated timeline of the arrival of your PAN card.
  • Identify if you need to reapply due to rejection or discrepancies.

Methods to Check PAN Application Status Online

1. Check PAN Application Status on the NSDL Website

Here’s how to track PAN card status on the National Securities Depository Limited (NSDL) portal:

  1. Visit the official NSDL PAN status tracking page: https://tin.tin.nsdl.com/PANtan/StatusTrack.html
  2. Select your application type:
    • New PAN
    • Changes / Correction in PAN Data
    • Reprint PAN Card NSDL
  3. Enter your unique 15-digit acknowledgment number.
  4. Fill in the captcha code displayed on the screen.
  5. Click “Submit” to view your PAN card status.

2. Check PAN Application Status on UTIITSL Website

The Income Tax Department of India has entrusted UTI Infrastructure Technology and Services Limited (UTIITSL) with multiple financial services, one of which includes the PAN application status check. Follow these steps to track PAN card status through UTITSL:

  1. Go to the UTITSL PAN inquiry page: https://www.utiitsl.com/UTIITSL_SITE/PAN/index.html

The remaining steps are the same as for the NSDL website.

3. Check PAN Application Status via SMS

  • For new PAN applications: SMS NSDLPAN<15-digit acknowledgement number> to 57575.
  • For changes / corrections in PAN: SMS NSDLPANC<15-digit acknowledgement number> to 57575.
  • For reprinting PAN card: SMS NSDLPANR<15-digit acknowledgement number> to 57575.

4. Check PAN Application Status by Phone Call

  1. Dial the NSDL PAN call center at 020-27218080.
  2. Follow the IVR prompts.
  3. Input your 15-digit acknowledgement number.
  4. Listen carefully to the automated voice message informing about your PAN card application status.

Understanding Different PAN Card Application Status Messages

When tracking your PAN application status online, you will encounter one of these status messages:

Status Message
What It Means
PAN Application is Under Process
Your PAN application has been received and is under process, which usually takes 15-20 days.
Documents Verified / PAN Processed
Your application and documents have been verified; your PAN is generated and will be dispatched soon.
PAN Card Dispatched
Your printed physical PAN card is on the way to your communication address through speed post; expect delivery in 15 days.
PAN Application Rejected
Your application has been rejected due to errors in the form or discrepancies in submitted documents; re-apply with rectified details.

If more than 20 days have passed and your PAN card status still shows “under process,” contact the NSDL or UTITSL customer support for assistance.

Things to Keep in Mind When Tracking Your PAN Card Status

  • Carefully note down the 15-digit acknowledgement number generated after submitting your application; it is needed to track the PAN card.
  • Wait at least 10-15 days before checking status to allow sufficient processing time.
  • If your PAN card application gets rejected, promptly re-apply with accurate information and valid documents.
  • Once the track PAN card status shows “Dispatched,” expect to receive it at your registered address within 15 days by post.

Documents Required for PAN Card Application

  1. Proof of Identity (any one of the following):
    • Aadhaar Card
    • Voter ID Card
    • Passport
    • Driving License
    • Ration Card with photograph
  2. Proof of Address (any one of the following):
    • Aadhaar Card
    • Voter ID Card
    • Passport
    • Driving License
    • Utility bills (electricity, water, gas) not older than 3 months
    • Bank account statement or passbook
    • Post office passbook
  3. Proof of Date of Birth (any one of the following):
    • Birth Certificate
    • Matriculation Certificate
    • Passport
    • Marriage Certificate
    • Driving License
    • Pension Payment Order
  4. Proof of Income (for applicants paying taxes):
    • Salary slip
    • Bank statements
    • Income Tax Assessment Order
  5. Photograph: 2 recent passport-sized photographs (for physical application)

Importance of Business PAN

A Business PAN card is an important and unique identification document for every business organisation in India, especially when seeking financial help through business loans.

  • PAN promotes openness and accountability in all financial transactions, making it a critical component for business operations and growth.
  • Having a Business PAN makes loan acceptance easier by presenting correct financial data to the lender. A transparent tax record represented in the PAN can fast-track your business loan application and maybe help lower interest rates and increase credit eligibility.
  • It can assist your business in obtaining tax breaks on loan repayments, which has a direct impact on your profit margins and operating costs.

How can a PAN Card Enable Business Loans?

Having a PAN card is not only essential for tax purposes but also enables access to crucial business loans. Most lenders require a business PAN card to assess business loan eligibility, especially with unsecured business loans.

Lenders use your PAN to verify your identity, track your credit history and assess your creditworthiness when you apply for business loans.
