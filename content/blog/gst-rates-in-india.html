The introduction of the Goods and Services Tax (GST) in India has revolutionised the country’s indirect tax system. As a business owner or consumer, it is crucial to understand the various GST rates and GST percentages in India. This comprehensive guide will walk you through the different GST rates in India, the importance of GST HSN code list and how to calculate GST charges for your goods and services.

Importance of HSN Codes Under GST

The Harmonised System of Nomenclature (HSN) codes play a crucial role in making the GST system systematic and globally accepted. Using the GST HSN code list eliminates the need to upload detailed descriptions of goods, saves time and simplifies the filing process, as GST returns automate the process.

Dealers and service providers must provide an HSN / SAC-wise summary of sales in their GSTR-1 form if their turnover falls within specified slabs. This makes the GST HSN code list an essential tool for businesses to ensure compliance and accuracy in their GST filings.

GST Rate Structure

The total GST percentage is the sum of the Central Goods and Services Tax (CGST) and the State Goods and Services Tax (SGST) for intrastate supply or the Integrated Goods and Services Tax (IGST) for interstate supply. To calculate the GST charges on a tax invoice, multiply the assessable value of the supply by the applicable GST rates in India.

In addition to the standard GST percentage, the law levies cess on the sale of certain items, such as:

  • Cigarettes and tobacco products
  • Aerated water
  • Petrol
  • Motor vehicles

Cess rates vary widely, ranging from 1% to 204%.

Calculating GST Amount

  1. Determine the applicable GST percentage for the goods or services being supplied using the GST HSN code list.
  2. Calculate the GST amount using the formula: GST Amount = (Original Price × GST Rate) ÷ 100
  3. Add the GST amount to the original price to arrive at the final price.

For example, if a product costs ₹1,000 and the applicable GST rate is 18%, the calculation would be:

GST Amount = (1,000 × 18) ÷ 100 = ₹180

Final Price = 1,000 + 180 = ₹1,180
