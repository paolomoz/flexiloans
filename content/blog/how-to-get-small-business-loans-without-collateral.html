What is a Collateral-Free Business Loan?

When a bank approves a loan, it seeks something of value for security in case the borrower fails to repay the loan. This something of value is what collateral is, which can be the property like an office or building you own, assets like vehicles or equipment, inventory or accounts receivable and more.

A collateral-free business loan, also known as an unsecured business loan, is a type of financing where the bank, NBFC or lender doesn’t ask you to pledge any asset. Even if you don’t own a property or equipment, the lender approves a loan if you meet the set eligibility criteria.

How does Unsecured Business Loan Work?

In secured business loans, the collateral you provide, among many other factors, makes you eligible for a small business loan. In small or MSME loan without collateral, on the other hand, lenders assess your application based on your creditworthiness, business history and financial performance. The lenders also assess your loan application based on the following factors:

  • Your business performance
  • Financials
  • Monthly turnover
  • Credit history or CIBIL score
  • Vintage (how long your business has been running)

Unlike secured business loans or equity financing, unsecured business loans don’t put your assets at risk and reduce your ownership. This is why these are popular among new business owners. NBFCs, fintech platforms including Flexiloans, and even banks now offer an MSME loan without collateral through streamlined digital processes.

Eligibility for Unsecured Business Loan

If you’re running a small or medium enterprise, chances are you’re eligible, but there are a few key criteria for unsecured loans you’ll need to meet:

  • Business Vintage: Your business must be in operation for at least 12 months.
  • Monthly Turnover: The monthly turnover of your business should be over ₹1.5-2 lakhs.
  • Age: You, as a business owner, must be between 21 and 65 years old.
  • Business type: Sole proprietors, partnerships, private limited companies, and LLPs can apply.
  • Credit Score: You and your business credit score must be over 700.

Lenders prefer applicants with clean financial records, even if they don’t have collateral. If your business earns steadily and your documents are in order, lenders are more likely to approve your loan.

Types of Unsecured Business Loans Available

Depending on your business requirements, you can apply for different types of business loans without collateral. Whether you need funds to cover daily expenses or pay salaries, here are the types of unsecured business loans you can apply for:

  1. Working Capital Loan: A working capital loan without collateral helps you cover daily operational expenses like salaries, rent, utilities, and short-term cash gaps.
  2. Line of Credit: It is a revolving flexible credit line that you can use whenever you need to and repay it when you can while paying interest only on the amount used.
  3. Term Loans: It is a type of business loan in which a lender lends a certain amount of money for a predetermined duration of time that the borrower repays in regular payments.
  4. Merchant Cash Advance: It is a short-term financing in which you get a one-time payment and repay the loan with a portion of future revenue/sales via credit and debit cards.
  5. 5. Invoice Financing: Invoice financing helps B2B businesses unlock cash tied up in pending invoices.

All these types of business loans offer their own features and benefits. The key is choosing based on your cash flow, repayment ability, and business model type, not just what’s trending.

Documents Required for MSME Loan without Collateral

As compared to secured loans, you have to submit a few documents for unsecured business loans. Here is a list of all the necessary documents for MSME loan you must submit during application process:

  • ITR (only if you’re going for a larger loan amount)

Common Mistakes to Avoid

36% of business loans in India face rejection due to poor credit scores. It is one of the many reasons why MSME loans get rejected in India, making it important for you to ensure you avoid the most common mistakes. Here is how you can improve chances of loan approval:

  • Incomplete Documents: Lenders flag and reject applications with missing GST returns, outdated bank statements or poor documentation. While applying for an unsecured business loan, ensure all documents are latest and free of any errors.
  • Low Credit Score: A good credit score gives a lender insight into a borrower’s repayment commitment and spending behaviour. If your CIBIL score is less than 700, improve it by paying credit card bills and loan EMI on time.
  • Over-borrowing: Ensure that you know exactly how much amount you need to cover business needs. You should avoid applying for amounts higher or lower than necessary. If you already have multiple loans, you might come off as financially stretched.
