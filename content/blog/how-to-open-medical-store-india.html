2. Documents required for opening a medical store

Here is a list of the typical documents and requirements needed to open a medical store in India:

1. Educational Qualifications: You, as the proprietor or the appointed pharmacist, should have the necessary educational qualifications, which usually include a Bachelor of Pharmacy (B.Pharm) or a Diploma in Pharmacy (D.Pharm) from a recognized institution. A registered pharmacist is required to manage the store.

2. Location and Premises: Provide documents related to the premises where the medical store will be located, including the lease agreement or ownership documents.

Ensure that the location complies with the guidelines set by the local regulatory authority, such as the State Drug Control Department or the Food and Drug Administration (FDA). 

3. Identity and Address Proof: You will need to submit your identity and address proof, such as Aadhar card, passport, voter ID, or driver’s license.

4. Passport-sized Photographs: Provide passport-sized photographs of the applicant(s), including the pharmacist.

5. PAN Card: You must provide a PAN (Permanent Account Number) card for the business entity or individual.

6. Business Entity Registration: If you are operating as a business entity (e.g., a sole proprietorship, partnership, or private limited company), you’ll need the registration documents for that entity. 

7. Declaration Form: A duly filled declaration form for starting a pharmacy business.

8. Ownership Proof: If you own the premises, you need to provide ownership documents. If it’s on a lease, you should provide the lease agreement.

9. Affidavit: A duly signed affidavit stating that the premises will only be used for the pharmacy business and not for any other purpose.

10. No Objection Certificate (NOC): An NOC from the landlord or the property owner, stating that they have no objection to the premises being used for a pharmacy business. 

11. Storage Facility Details: Details regarding the storage facility for medicines, including photographs of storage racks, refrigerators (if applicable), and other storage equipment. 

12. Shops and Establishment Registration: Registration under the Shops and Establishment Act may be required, depending on the state in which you intend to open the medical store. 

13. Medical Council Registration Certificate: If you are a registered medical practitioner and intend to sell Schedule X drugs, you may need to provide your Medical Council Registration Certificate.

14. Local Municipal Corporation Clearance: Check with your local municipal corporation for any additional documents or clearances required for operating a business in that area.

15. Fees: Pay the necessary fees for the drug license application and any other charges as required by the regulatory authority.

It’s crucial to contact your local State Drug Control Department or the FDA for the most up-to-date information and guidance on the documents and requirements for opening a medical store in your specific location. Medical Store Registration Process

Business Entity:

Decide on the type of business entity you want to establish. Most medical stores in India are registered as sole proprietorships, partnerships, or private limited companies. Choose the one that best suits your business structure and objectives.

Location and Premises:

Find a suitable location for your medical store. Ensure that the premises meet the requirements and guidelines set by the local regulatory authority, which is often the State Drug Control Department or the Food and Drug Administration (FDA).

Pharmacist:

Appoint a registered pharmacist who holds a valid license to operate the pharmacy. A qualified pharmacist is a legal requirement for running a medical store.

License:

You’ll need to obtain the necessary licenses and permits. The primary license required is the Drug License, which can be of two types:

a. Retail Drug License: This license is necessary for selling prescription and over-the-counter medicines.

b. Wholesale Drug License: This license is required if you intend to sell medicines in bulk to other retailers.

Application:

To apply for a drug license, you need to submit an application to the State Drug Control Department or the FDA. The application should include the necessary documents, such as proof of pharmacist’s qualification, premises details, and other required documents.

Inspection:

After you submit your application, authorities may conduct an inspection of your premises to ensure it complies with the regulations and standards.

Fees:

Pay the requisite fees for the license application and any other fees as required by the regulatory authority.

Verification:

After all the formalities are completed, the drug control department or FDA will verify your application and documents.

Issuance:

If your application is approved and all requirements are met, you’ll receive the drug license, allowing you to operate your medical store legally.

Compliance:

Make sure to adhere to all the legal and regulatory requirements, including maintaining proper records, following good storage practices, and selling only approved and licensed medications.

It’s important to stay updated with any changes in the regulations, as they may vary by state and over time. Consulting with a legal expert or industry association, such as the All India Organization of Chemists and Druggists (AIOCD), can also be helpful in navigating the registration process.
