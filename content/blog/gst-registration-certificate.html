What is a GST Registration Certificate?

A GST Registration Certificate is an official document issued by the Goods and Services Tax authorities upon successful registration under GST. It legally proves that the government has registered the business under GST and authorised it to collect and remit taxes. Businesses must display this certificate at their place of operation to ensure compliance with tax regulations.

Who Needs a GST Registration Certificate?

Businesses meeting the following criteria must obtain a registration certificate:

  • Turnover Threshold: Businesses with an annual turnover exceeding ₹20 lakh for services and ₹40 lakh for goods (₹10 lakh for special category states).
  • Interstate Trade: Any business involved in supplying goods or services across state borders.
  • E-commerce Sellers: Businesses selling on platforms like Amazon, Flipkart, or their own websites.
  • Casual & Non-Resident Taxable Persons: Businesses operating occasionally or without a fixed place of business in India.
  • Agents & Input Service Distributors (ISD): Businesses acting as agents or distributing input tax credit.

Understanding Form GST REG-06

The government issues the GST registration certificate in Form GST REG-06, which contains:

  • GSTIN (Goods and Services Tax Identification Number).
  • Legal name and trade name of the business.
  • Principal and additional place(s) of business.
  • Date of registration and validity period (for specific categories like casual taxable persons).

This certificate remains valid unless canceled or surrendered, except for temporary registrations like casual taxable persons, which require renewal after 90 days.

How to Get a GST Registration Certificate Online? (Step-by-Step Guide)

Obtaining a GST Registration Certificate online is a straightforward process through the official GST portal. Follow these steps to complete your registration:

Step 1: Visit the GST Portal

Go to the official GST portal – gst.gov.in. Click on “New Registration” under the ‘Services’ tab.

Step 2: Enter Business Details

Fill in the required details, including:

  • Business name (as per PAN)
  • Type of business (Proprietorship, Partnership, Company, etc.)
  • PAN (Permanent Account Number)
  • State and district
  • Email ID and mobile number (for OTP verification)

Step 3: Upload Required Documents

Provide the necessary documents based on your business type, such as PAN, Aadhaar, business address proof, and bank details.

Step 4: Verify Through OTP-Based Authentication

You will receive an OTP (One-Time Password) on your registered mobile number and email. Enter the OTP to verify your application.

Step 5: Receive ARN (Application Reference Number)

After successful submission, you will receive an Application Reference Number (ARN) via SMS and email. Use this ARN to track your application status on the GST portal.

Step 6: Download the GST Registration Certificate

Once the GST officer approves your application, you can download the GST Registration Certificate (Form GST REG-06) from the GST portal:

  1. Log in to the GST portal.
  2. Click on “Services” > “User Services” > “View/Download Certificate.”
  3. Download the certificate in PDF format for your records.

This certificate proves GST registration and requires display at your business premises.

Documents Required for GST Registration

To successfully register for GST, businesses must submit specific documents based on their legal structure. Below is a categorised list of required documents:

For Proprietorships:

  • PAN Card & Aadhaar Card of the proprietor
  • Address Proof of Business (Electricity bill, rent agreement, or property tax receipt)
  • Bank Details (Cancelled cheque, bank statement, or passbook)

For Partnerships & LLPs (Limited Liability Partnerships):

  • Partnership Deed (Agreement detailing the business structure)
  • PAN & Aadhaar Cards of All Partners
  • Business Registration Certificate (Issued by the Registrar of Firms or Ministry of Corporate Affairs for LLPs)
  • Address Proof of Business (Utility bill, rent/lease agreement)

For Private Limited Companies:

  • Certificate of Incorporation (Issued by the Ministry of Corporate Affairs)
  • PAN & Aadhaar Cards of Directors
  • MOA & AOA (Memorandum & Articles of Association) – Company’s legal framework
  • Board Resolution or Authorisation Letter (Appointing an authorised signatory)
  • Business Address Proof (Utility bill, rent agreement, or ownership deed)

Submitting accurate documents ensures a smooth GST registration process, avoiding delays or rejections.

How to Download the GST Registration Certificate?

Step 1: Login to the GST Portal

  • Visit gst.gov.in.
  • Enter your username and password to log in to your account.

Step 2: Navigate to the Download Section

  • Click on “Services” in the top menu.
  • Go to “User Services” and select “View/Download Certificates.”

Step 3: Download Form GST REG-06

  • Locate your GST Registration Certificate.
  • Click on the download option to save the certificate in PDF format.

Validity & Renewal of GST Certificate

  • The GST registration certificate remains valid indefinitely for regular taxpayers unless canceled or surrendered.
  • The certificate is valid for ninety days and needs to be renewed before then for both casual taxable people and non-resident taxable persons.

Displaying your GST Registration Certificate at your business premises ensures compliance with GST regulations.

Common GST Registration Issues & How to Solve Them

While the GST registration process is straightforward, businesses may face challenges such as application rejection, incorrect details, or delays in approval. Here’s how to resolve these common issues:

1. Application Rejected – Reasons & Fixes

Common Reasons for Rejection:

  • Incorrect or mismatched details (PAN, Aadhaar, or business name).
  • Incomplete or invalid documents.
  • Business address proof not meeting GST guidelines.

How to Fix:

  • Reapply with correct and updated information.
  • Ensure uploaded documents are clear, valid, and match the application details.
  • Double-check the PAN and Aadhaar details before submission.

2. Incorrect Details on GST Certificate – How to Modify?

If your GST certificate contains errors (business name, address, or GSTIN details), you can request a correction through the GST portal.

Steps to Modify GST Registration:

  1. Login to GST Portal
  2. Go to “Services” > “Registration” > “Amendment of Registration (Core Fields).”
  3. Update the incorrect details and upload supporting documents if required.
  4. Submit the request and track the status under “Track Application Status.”

3. Delayed Approval – How to Track GST ARN Status?

After submitting your GST application, approval generally takes 7-10 working days. However, delays may occur due to document verification or government backlogs.

How to Track GST ARN Status?

  1. Visit the GST portal and go to “Services” > “Track Application Status.”
  2. Enter your ARN (Application Reference Number).
  3. Check for updates on approval or any queries raised by the GST officer.

For quicker processing, ensure that all required documents are correctly uploaded and respond promptly to any clarification requests from the GST department.

GST Registration Fees & Processing Time

GST Registration Fees

  • Government Fees: GST registration is completely free for most businesses when applied through the official GST portal (gst.gov.in).
  • Professional Fees (Optional): If you choose to apply through a Chartered Accountant (CA), tax consultant, or online service provider, they may charge professional fees, typically ranging from ₹500 to ₹5,000, depending on the complexity of the application.

Processing Time for GST Registration

  • Standard Processing Time: 7-10 working days from the date of application submission.
  • Possible Delays: The processing time may extend if documents are incorrect, additional verification is required, or if there is a backlog at the GST department.

To ensure a quick approval, businesses should provide accurate details and valid documents during registration.
