What is a CIBIL Score?

Before we dive into how to get your CIBIL score, let’s understand what it is. Your CIBIL score is a 3-digit number ranging from 300 to 900, that represents your creditworthiness. It is based on your credit history, which includes factors like your repayment track record, credit mix and credit utilisation. A higher CIBIL score, typically above 750, indicates good credit health and can improve your chances of securing a loan for business with favourable terms.

Why Check Your CIBIL Score?

  1. Loan Eligibility: Your CIBIL score is one of the primary factors lenders consider when evaluating your loan application. Therefore, a good score can increase your chances of approval for a term loan or line of credit.
  2. Interest Rates: A high CIBIL score may help you negotiate better interest rates on your loans for business, potentially saving you significant money over the loan tenure.
  3. Credit Health Monitoring: By regularly checking your CIBIL score for free, you can keep track of changes in your credit profile and take corrective measures if needed.
  4. Error Identification: Sometimes, credit reports may contain errors that negatively impact your CIBIL score. By checking your CIBIL report for free, you can identify and dispute any inaccuracies.

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Check CIBIL Score Free Online

  1. Visit the official CIBIL website at cibil.com.
  2. Click on ‘Get Free CIBIL Score and Report’.
  3. Provide your details, including:
    • Name
    • Email ID
    • Password
    • ID proof (passport, PAN card, Aadhaar card or Voter ID)
    • PIN code
    • Date of birth
    • Mobile number
  4. Click on ‘Accept and Continue’.
  5. Enter the OTP received on your registered mobile number.
  6. Select ‘Go to Dashboard’ and log in to view your CIBIL score and free CIBIL report.

Note that you can get your CIBIL score for free once a year. For additional reports, there is a fee:

  • Monthly subscription: ₹550
  • 6-month subscription: ₹800
  • 12-month subscription: ₹1,200

On the other hand, a score above 750 can significantly improve your chances of getting approved for a term loan or line of credit with competitive rates. Factors That Affect Your CIBIL Score

  1. Payment History: Paying your loans and credit card bills on time is essential for keeping a strong CIBIL score.
  2. Credit Utilisation Ratio: You need to maintain your credit utilisation ratio (the amount of credit you use compared to your credit limit) below 30%.
  3. Length of Credit History: A longer credit history with a consistent payment record can positively impact your CIBIL score.
  4. Credit Mix: Having a diverse mix of credit types (secured and unsecured) can demonstrate your ability to manage different types of credit responsibly.
  5. New Credit Inquiries: Applying for multiple loans or credit cards within a short period can temporarily lower your CIBIL score.

Tips to Improve Your CIBIL Score

  1. Pay your bills and EMIs on time.
  2. Maintain a healthy credit mix.
  3. Keep your credit utilisation low.
  4. Avoid applying for multiple loans simultaneously.
  5. Regularly check your CIBIL report for free.

By following these practices, you can gradually improve your CIBIL score and enhance your creditworthiness in the eyes of lenders. Better creditworthiness can result in the approval of a business loan at a lower interest rate.

Benefits of Checking Your CIBIL Score

While you can access your CIBIL score and report for free once a year, it’s highly recommended to check your CIBIL score more frequently. Banks, financial institutions, and credit agencies update your credit report on a monthly basis. By regularly monitoring your score, you can stay informed about any changes and take early action. Doing so allows you to:

  • Detect errors or suspicious activity promptly, ensuring your financial safety.
  • Understand how your credit behavior influences your score over time, allowing for more informed financial decisions.
  • Take timely corrective actions to improve your score when necessary, helping you maintain a healthy credit profile.
