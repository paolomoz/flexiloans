What is IMPS?

Immediate Payment Service (IMPS) is a fast payment service introduced by National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI) in 2010. It provides a 24/7 instant domestic funds transfer facility. India was the fourth country to introduce the IMPS payment system, after South Korea, South Africa, and the United Kingdom.

The National Payments Corporation of India, or NPCI, provides the facility of IMPS transactions. This helps to operate all the retail payments and settlement systems in India. The system also provides real-time transfer of funds between the remitter and the beneficiary with a deferred net settlement between the banks.

You can transfer funds via IMPS using various channels like Internet banking, mobile banking apps, Bank branches, and ATMS. Using IMPS, you can transfer money using minimal information, like your mobile number or the recipient’s bank account number and IFSC code.

IMPS Transfer Limit Per Day

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) sets the daily limit of all IMPS transactions. However, your bank or payment service provider sets the maximum amount you can transfer daily. Banks or payment service providers put a daily limit to manage risk and fraud.

  • There is no minimum limit in an IMPS transaction. You can send as low as ₹1.
  • In a day, the maximum amount you can transfer using IMPS is as high as ₹5 lakh.
  • However, it can range between ₹ 2 – ₹5 lakhs depending on the bank or payment service provider.

If you’re a new beneficiary, some banks place a limit for the first 24 hours. Depending upon your bank, the limit can range from ₹30,000 to ₹50,000.

IMPS Timings

The round-the-clock availability of the IMPS system is its biggest advantage. Unlike RTGS (which works only during banking hours) and NEFT (which follows batch-wise settlement), IMPS operates 24/7, 365 days a year, including Sundays and national holidays.

Transfer Time

  • Speed: IMPS systems take a few seconds to a few minutes to complete a transaction.
  • Service Downtime: During system maintenance by the banks or NPCI, IMPS transactions may face a server downtime.

What is an IMPS Wallet Number?

IMPS wallet number, also known as a Mobile Money Identifier (MMID), is a unique 7-digit code assigned by banks to each mobile banking account. MMID is required when adding beneficiaries for IMPS transactions. This allows immediate fund transfers even on bank and public holidays. MMID is used to make error-free transfers. If you enter the wrong MMID, the funds won’t transfer.

There are multiple ways to find your MMID. You can sign in to your mobile banking app and select the Know your MMID option. Alternatively, you can receive your MMID via SMS by sending a message to the number provided by your bank.

Peer-to-peer (P2P) transfers allow users to send and receive money directly to and from mobile wallets like Paytm, PhonePe, Mobikwik, and others. Two essential elements were needed for transfers to mobile wallets in previous iterations of IMPS: the recipient’s Mobile Money Identifier (MMID) and cellphone number.

IMPS to Wallet Transfer

New-age digital wallet companies like Paytm, PhonePe, and MobiKwik allow IMPS transfers. These companies provide a wallet feature to facilitate direct transfers from your bank account using your account number and IFSC code. For example:

  • Paytm Bank Account Number: Usually your mobile number with a prefix/suffix.
  • IFSC Code: Assigned by your wallet provider (e.g., PYTM0123456).

You can transfer money from your bank account to a wallet using your account number and IFSC code via IMPS. This feature ensures fast and secure wallet recharges.

Use Case of Wallet Number:

  • You can quickly pay merchants.
  • You can add funds to your wallet for UPI transactions.

How to Do an IMPS Transfer (Step-by-Step)?

Transferring money via IMPS is a quick and easy process. You can transfer money in real-time via various channels like a mobile banking app, net banking, and an ATM.

1. Mobile Banking App

  • Open the mobile application of your bank.
  • Find and select the ‘IMPS’ option.
  • You can choose an existing contact or manually add the recipient.
  • Enter the amount and proceed.
  • Confirm your transaction.

2. Net Banking

  • Log in to your internet banking account.
  • Add the account number and IFSC code of the beneficiary.
  • Wait for the beneficiary activation.
  • Go to IMPS Transfer.
  • Enter the amount and provide OTP authentication to approve the money transfer.

3. ATM

  • Visit your bank ATM. Select language, enter pin and proceed.
  • Select services like fund transfer and click on IMPS.
  • Enter the mobile number of the beneficiary.
  • Enter the MMID and transaction amount.
  • Verify details and confirm your transaction.
