Types of Online Business Loans Available for 10 Lakh

Working capital loans are ideal for covering day-to-day operations/expenses, whereas term loans are ideal for business expansion plans. There are different types of business loans online that cater to your specific business needs. Here are the different types of business loans online for 10 lakh:

Term Loans

Term loans provide a fixed amount that you can pay off in fixed installments. These loans are ideal for businesses wishing to invest in long-term assets. With this 10 lakh business loan in India, you can expand operations, purchase machinery or open a new branch.

Working Capital Loans

A working capital loan of 10 lakh, on the other hand, assists in funding day-to-day operational costs such as rent, stock, or staff salaries. This loan type is particularly helpful in seasonal dips or high-demand periods.

Secured vs Unsecured Loans

  • Secured Loans: These demand pledging collateral such as property, equipment, or inventory and offer lower interest rates because of it.
  • Unsecured Loans: These do not demand collateral, which makes them suitable for small-scale businesses or newly established companies.

Government Schemes

Owing to the importance of small businesses in India, the government offers loan schemes such as Mudra loans online under Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana (PMMY) and PMEGP. These schemes allow small businesses to avail of loans for business growth and expansion. The loan disbursement is done through online channels with other added advantages such as reduced interest rates and longer tenures.

Common Mistakes to Avoid When Applying for a 10 Lakh Business Loan Online

Though it’s quick and easy to apply for a 10 lakh business loan online, most applicants end up making mistakes that delay the approval process or, worse, face rejection. Here are some mistakes to avoid in a business loan application for a better chance of getting approved for a business loan:

1. Incomplete or Incorrect Documentation

Incomplete or poor documentation is the most common cause of loan application rejection. Make sure that all the necessary documents are properly filled up, updated, and consistent. These documents include ID proof, business registration, bank statements, GST returns, and financials. Small differences in signatures, different PAN numbers, or old addresses can ring alarm bells for lenders.

2. Not Checking Loan Terms Thoroughly

Most borrowers get blinded due to quick approvals and ignore the terms and conditions. However, you should always check the interest rates, processing charges, prepayment penalties, EMI schedules, and any additional charges before signing the loan agreement. What seems like a good deal at first glance can prove expensive later if not carefully considered.

3. Ignoring Eligibility Criteria

Every lender has its own set of business loan eligibility criteria like minimum turnover, age of business, credit history, and other financial reports. Applying for a loan without these documents can lead to loan rejection. You should always do pre-verification of your eligibility using online tools in advance.

4. Not Considering Repayment Capacity

Borrowers often try to get as much money as they can. They don’t consider whether they can repay that amount comfortably or not. Don’t make this mistake. Use EMI calculators to calculate your monthly repayment liabilities and match them with your cash flow. Over-borrowing creates financial stress, leads to EMI defaults, and hurts your credit history.
