Apply for CGSS – Credit Guarantee Scheme for Startups

Launching a startup in India is exciting, but getting access to funding can be a real hurdle, especially without assets to back a loan. That’s where the Credit Guarantee Scheme for Startups (CGSS) steps in.

So, what is CGSS scheme? It’s a government-backed initiative launched by the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT). The scheme aims to provide collateral-free loans to startups, giving them the financial push they need to grow.

Why is this important? Many early-stage startups struggle to get loans from banks and NBFCs due to a lack of credit history or security. Therefore, CGSS solves this by offering a guarantee cover of up to ₹10 crore, giving lenders the confidence to support startups.

DPIIT-recognised startups in tech, manufacturing, or services can apply for the scheme. If you are building something new and ambitious, CGSS might be the right support system for you.

Therefore, this makes it one of the most accessible and promising startup funding in India options available today. Whether you are a first-time entrepreneur or scaling your idea, the CGSS startup loan could help you move forward—without putting your personal assets at risk.

Key Features and Benefits of CGSS

Collateral-Free Loans

With CGSS, startups can secure loans without pledging any assets. This is ideal for founders who don’t own property or heavy equipment.

Guarantee Coverage up to ₹10 Crore

Each startup can get a credit guarantee covered up to ₹10 crore. This ensures significant support for high-potential businesses.

Open to All Sectors

Be it manufacturing, fintech, e-commerce, or services—this scheme welcomes startups from all sectors. Therefore, it encourages innovation across the board.

Backed by Government Trust

The National Credit Guarantee Trustee Company (NCGTC) manages the trust fund that runs the scheme. Hence, this builds lender confidence and improves loan disbursal.

Boosts Lending Confidence


Since CGSS reduces the risk for lenders, more banks and NBFCs are willing to offer credit to early-stage startups. Thus, this helps bridge the funding gap many founders face.

Flexible Loan Terms

Lenders can design the loan tenure and structure around the startup’s growth plans and business needs. Hence, this ensures manageable repayments.

Eligibility Criteria for CGSS

Wondering if your startup can apply for the scheme? CGSS supports innovative and scalable businesses, but not every startup qualifies. From DPIIT certification to financial history, there are a few key conditions to meet. Here’s a quick look at the CGSS eligibility criteria:

Who is Eligible?

  • DPIIT-registered startups under the Startup India initiative.
  • Startups working on innovation, technology, or scalable business models.
  • Businesses that meet the turnover limits set by the government. As per the latest norms, this includes startups with an annual turnover of less than ₹100 crore for any financial year since incorporation.

Therefore, if you meet these, you have fulfilled the criteria for a Startup India loan eligibilityunder CGSS.

Who is Not Eligible?

  • Startups without DPIIT certification cannot apply.
  • Startups in sectors excluded by RBI guidelines, such as gambling or speculative activities.
  • Businesses with a poor financial history, tax defaults, or non-compliance with statutory requirements.

Therefore, it’s important to meet both the operational and financial norms. If you are unsure who can apply for CGSS, consult your financial advisor or a participating lender.

How to Apply for CGSS: Step-by-Step Process

Step 1: Get DPIIT Recognition

Register your business on the Startup India portal and apply for DPIIT certification. This is the first and most crucial step.

Step 2: Prepare Business Documents

Have your paperwork ready. Key documents include:

  • Business plan and financial projections
  • GST registration and PAN card
  • KYC documents of directors/partners

Step 3: Approach an Eligible Lending Institution

Reach out to banks, NBFCs, or financial institutions that are part of the CGSS network. Digital lenders like FlexiLoans also support this scheme with quick processing.

Step 4: Submit Loan Application and Documents

File your CGSS loan application with all the required documents. Clearly explain how you plan to use the funds and how you will repay them.

Step 5: Loan Approval and Credit Guarantee Issuance

Once your startup loan application process is complete and the loan is approved, the National Credit Guarantee Trustee Company (NCGTC) will issue a guarantee to the lender on your behalf.

The CGSS application process is simple and designed to help startups succeed with the right financial support.

Documents Required for CGSS Application

Before you apply, make sure you have this CGSS application checklist ready. Remember, proper documentation speeds up the process.

  • Business Registration Proof: DPIIT certificate and MSME registration (if applicable).
  • Financial Statements: Recent audited balance sheets, profit and loss statements, and income tax returns.
  • Loan Application Form: A complete and signed loan application form with all business details.
  • KYC of Directors/Partners: Aadhaar card, PAN card, and address proof for each promoter.
  • Business Plan and Revenue Model: Detailed forecasts, projected cash flow, and future growth plans.
  • Bank Statements: At least 6 to 12 months of business bank account statements. Therefore, having the right documents required for CGSS loan can make or break your application. So, double-check your startup loan documentation to avoid delays.
