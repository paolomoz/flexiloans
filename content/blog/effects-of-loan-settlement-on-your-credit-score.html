Loan Settlement and Its Impact on your Credit Score

If you cannot pay your term loan due to unavoidable reasons, you can approach your lender with your side of the story. The lender will do a complete assessment of all your assets and determine whether what you are claiming is true or not. Once the lender confirms that you really cannot repay your debt, you can ask the lender to give you some time off before restarting the payment, or the lender might offer you a Loan Settlement.

In a loan settlement, the lender agrees to settle for an amount that is less than the actual amount to be repaid. This amount is usually the amount paid by you to date, including the interest rates. Suppose you have taken a loan of Rs. 45 Lakhs. You had to repay it within the next 7 years. This is your year 4 of repayment, and you are no longer able to pay the EMI. But the amount repaid by you till now, including the interest rates, is Rs. 30 Lakhs. In a loan settlement, the lender will agree to settle the loan for Rs. 30 Lakhs, and you will not have to pay any additional amount.

This decision is not reached lightly. The lender sits and talks with you and gathers proof of your inability to repay term loans. They consider a waiting period of six months before taking this decision. Then finally, you get to settle the loan with the bank.

The lender gives you a certain time to collect the settlement amount and pay the lender. However, this may be in your favour. It is not. Because it negatively impacts your CIBIL Score. And having a negative impact on your CIBIL score is not always worth the settlement.

When you agree upon a loan settlement, the loan is ‘settled’ and not ‘closed’. When a lender agrees to settle a loan, the information is sent to CIBIL and other similar agencies that maintain track of your credit score. When you accept a loan, financial institutions assume that you would be unable to repay what you promised. This has a negative impact on your credit score.

On settlement of a loan, your credit score can decrease by 100 points. CIBIL keeps this information on your record for the next seven years. So if you apply for any form of credit within seven years of the loan settlement, financial institutes could deny it. The reason is your inability to close your last debt.

On average, financial institutes consider a CIBIL score of more than 750 for lending a loan. When you opt for loan settlement, your score can easily go below 750. That will make you not eligible for a loan from most financial institutes.

How to remove loan settlement status from CIBIL Score?

Even if you get a negative impact on your CIBIL score due to a loan settlement, you always have the option of turning it around. Suppose your financial condition improved a bit within 2 years of the loan settlement; then you can work your way to improve your credit score.

There is one final approach you can try to raise your CIBIL credit score to be eligible for more loans. You can request a repayment plan with the lender of your previous debt. The lender will mark your loan as closed once you have repaid the total loan amount. Loan closure is not the same as loan settlement. In reality, loan repayment has a beneficial effect on your credit score. In this manner, you can mitigate the negative impact of loan settlement on your credit score.
