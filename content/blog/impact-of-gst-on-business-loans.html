What is GST, and How Does It Relate to Business Loans?

Understanding the Basics of GST

To understand how GST affects loans, let’s first briefly review what GST entails. GST is a unified tax levied on the supply of goods and services. It replaced indirect taxes like service tax, VAT, excise duty, and others, creating a cohesive system. Businesses registered under GST can also claim Input Tax Credit (ITC), which helps reduce their overall tax burden.

Why GST Matters in Loan Transactions

Now, you might wonder: how does this relate to loans? Although loans are financial instruments, not goods or services, lenders’ ancillary services are considered taxable under GST. This includes processing, legal assistance, prepayment handling, and documentation support.

Does GST Apply to Business Loans?

Understanding GST on business loans in India requires breaking down which components attract tax and which remain exempt. While loans are financial instruments, the associated services often attract tax.

The impact of GST on business loans depends on the type of charges involved. While the borrowed amount and interest are exempt, service-related components fall under GST.

No GST on Loan Principal and Interest

Loans are financial transactions and not categorised as goods or services. Therefore, GST on loan interest and loan principal does not apply. Borrowers do not pay any GST on these two core components of a business loan.

GST on Loan Processing Fees and Other Charges

Borrowers pay processing fees, foreclosure penalties, and documentation charges, all of which lenders treat as taxable services. Lenders apply 18% GST on loan processing fees, which increases the overall borrowing cost, especially for MSMEs and startups.

GST Impact on NBFC and Bank Loan Services

Lenders, whether banks or NBFCs, must apply GST to all service-related charges. Digital lenders like FlexiLoans disclose these charges and help borrowers understand the GST on business loans in India, ensuring complete transparency.

Example
Suppose you take a loan with a processing fee of ₹10,000. If the processing fee is ₹10,000, lenders will add ₹1,800 as GST, bringing the total to ₹11,800.

Understanding these components is crucial for financial planning and helps MSMEs assess the impact of GST on business loans effectively.

Impact of GST on MSME and Startup Loans

Burden of Higher Processing Charges

Startups and MSMEs, often operating on thin margins and limited capital, may find the additional GST burden on service fees challenging. For example, a ₹50,000 loan with ₹2,000 in processing charges now incurs ₹360 extra due to GST. For businesses with multiple loans or top-ups, these costs compound over time.

Input Tax Credit (ITC) Benefits for Registered Businesses

GST-registered businesses can claim Input Tax Credit on the GST paid for loan-related charges. While the cost may increase initially, businesses can offset this amount against their GST liabilities, ultimately lowering their net expenditure.

Applicability Across Government Loan Schemes

It’s also important to note that loans offered under government-backed initiatives like CGTMSE, PMEGP, and MUDRA are not exempt from GST on processing and other service fees. Even if the principal is subsidised or guaranteed, such as in government schemes, borrowers still need to pay GST on related charges.

Can Businesses Claim GST Input Tax Credit (ITC) on Loans?

For GST-registered businesses, understanding Input Tax Credit (ITC) on loan-related charges is essential. While GST does not apply to the loan amount or interest, certain charges linked to borrowing are eligible for credit.

No ITC on Loan Principal and Interest

The loan principal and interest payments are exempt from GST. Since authorities do not treat loan principal or interest as taxable supplies, borrowers cannot claim any GST refund on loan processing fees or interest.

ITC Can Be Claimed on Processing Fees and Charges

Businesses registered under GST can claim related services such as processing, legal, and documentation. This helps reduce the effective borrowing cost, particularly for entities using loans to support taxable supplies.

Who Can Claim ITC on Loan GST Charges?

Only businesses using the borrowed funds for taxable business operations are eligible. For example, if a manufacturing unit takes a loan to upgrade machinery used in production, the GST paid on service charges can be offset against future tax liabilities.

Example of ITC on GST for a Business Loan

Let’s say a borrower pays ₹10,000 as a processing fee and ₹1,800 as GST. The business can claim this ₹1,800 against its output GST, reducing the net tax payable.
