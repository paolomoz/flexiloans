What is a Term Loan?

A term loan is a fixed amount of money lent to the borrower for a specific period on a secured or unsecured basis. These loans come with fixed and variable interest rates, and as a borrower, you are free to choose either of the options. However, one must repay the loan in regular instalments, or EMIs, along with the interest amount spread over a predetermined term. 

Eligibility criteria for FlexiLoans Term Loan 

  1. Ongoing business for more than a year with monthly total sales of at least $2 million.
  2. One must be between 21 years and 65 years of age.
  3. Individual applicants must have 3 years of industry experience and overall 5 years of experience.
  4. The business should be tax compliant.

Advantages of a term loan 

  1. Interest on a term loan is tax-deductible.
  2. Ability to select tenure based on your income.
  3. Less expensive source of money for the medium-term. 
  4. EMIs reduce the burden on the borrower.
  5. Negotiable terms & conditions.

What is an Overdraft Loan?

An overdraft loan or overdraft is a facility offered by banks/financial institutions that allow customers to withdraw over and above their account balance up to a certain limit. This loan is best to fulfil financial emergencies. Businesses need to pay interest only on the amount borrowed and an overdraft fee paid at the beginning of the year. The best part about the loan is that lenders can repay the loan (maximum within 12 months) and continue borrowing as and when required.  

Eligibility criteria for FlexiLoans Overdraft Loan 

  1. Individuals between 18 years and 65 years of age.
  2. Your current account value defines the credit limit.
  3. A minimum monthly income.
  4. A healthy CIBIL score.

Advantages of an overdraft loan

  1. Less paperwork and document verification as compared to other loans.
  2. Aids in financial emergencies.
  3. It offers flexibility as one can avail loan at any time for any amount up to a specific limit.
  4. No security or collateral.

How to choose: overdraft vs term loan?

Factors based on which you can choose between the term loan and overdraft loan. 

Amount required 

Opting for a term loan is your best bet if you need considerable money. On the other hand, overdraft facilities work like credit cards; thus, the limit is comparatively less. So, if you want a small loan, go for an overdraft loan.

Purpose

Generally, businesses use overdraft facilities to fulfil working capital requirements and to cater to emergency financial needs. Apply for a term loan when you want to use the loan amount to expand your business by purchasing machinery, relocating the business, starting a new product line, and more.  

Interest rate

The interest rate on overdraft loans is higher than on term loans. At the same time, you can choose between fixed and variable interest rates if you opt for a term loan.  

Repayment 

The repayment of overdraft loans is more flexible than term loans. This is because failure to pay any of the EMIs can negatively impact your credit score.  

Conclusion 

Term loans are ideal when you want a vast amount of money for large investments, while an overdraft facility will help you fulfil immediate financial and short-term funds requirements.
