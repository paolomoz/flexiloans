Types of MSME Loans Available

As there are different needs of small businesses, there are different types of MSME loans available to meet those needs, including the following:

  • Working Capital Loan: It is a type of MSME loan that offers money to cover a business’s ongoing operating costs. You can purchase raw materials, manage inventory, and pay salaries.
  • Term Loans: A term loan provides funds for long-term capital expenditures like buying equipment and machinery or expansion of business operations.
  • Equipment Financing: It provides funds only for buying, replacing, or upgrading machinery and equipment.
  • Overdraft Facility: This MSME loan type provides a variable credit limit according to the financial stability of your company, enabling you to take out money as required.
  • Mudra Lending: MUDRA Yojana is a government-backed lending program that provides startups and small enterprises with up to ₹10 lakhs in funding.
  • Invoice Financing: It is a credit facility that allows businesses to raise money by pledging their unpaid invoices to a bank or lender at a discount.

Eligibility Criteria for MSME Loan in 59 Minutes

Before you apply for a 59-minute MSME loan, you must ensure that your business meets the eligibility criteria for MSME loan. From business vintage to turnover, you must meet all the MSME loan requirements. Here are the complete MSME loan eligibility criteria:

  • Type of Business: Your business should be categorized as a Micro, Small, or Medium Enterprise (MSME) under the MSMED Act. Even though your business may be small in size or a start-up, you might still fit the bill.
  • Turnover Criteria: You must be a GST-registered business with an annual turnover of up to ₹10 lakh.
  • Financial Documents: You must provide basic documents like GST returns, ITR  for a minimum of one year (XML format), and bank statements for the last 6 months (PDF format).
  • Business Vintage (Age): You don’t need to fulfil the minimum vintage (business age) for ₹10 lakh. It only implies that new companies can apply as well, provided they have other minimum requirements.
  • Credit Score: Your business must have a credit score of 750 or above for the PSB Loan in 59 Minutes program in order to increase your chances of being approved for the loan.

Common Challenges in MSME Loan Approval and How to Overcome Them

Although the MSME Loan in 59 Minutes portal makes it easier, not all applications are successful. Some of the most common challenges in MSME loan approval can lead your application to rejection. While these MSME loan problems persist, you can avoid them during the application process in the first place. Here are some of the common MSME loan application issues and how to resolve them:

1. Low Credit Score

Banks consider your CIBIL score to evaluate your creditworthiness. If it is low (less than 650), it could raise an alarm. You can resolve it by paying your current loans on time and maintaining your utilization rate under 30%. You must also avoid making repeated applications in quick succession and check your credit report for inaccuracies and dispute them. These small improvement areas can go a long way in raising the chances of approval.

2. Incomplete or Mismatched Documents

The majority of MSME loan applications face rejection in India because the documents provided are either incorrect or mismatched, such as non-matching PAN details being non-matching GST records. During application, you must verify all entries in documents before uploading and revalidate old records before applying. You must also ensure that your bank statements, ITRs, and GST returns have the same turnover numbers.

3. Bad Financial Records

Bad financial records are often considered a red flag by lenders. In order to maintain a good financial record, you must keep comprehensive profit and loss accounts and regularly file taxes. You must also use accounting software to keep reporting simple and show business growth unequivocally.
