Problem #1: Difficulty in Meeting Eligibility Criteria

One of the biggest problems small business owners face when applying for business financing is meeting eligibility criteria under the business loan requirement rules set by lenders. If you don’t meet the criteria, your business loan approval can get delayed or even rejected.

Traditional lenders look at many things before giving you a loan. Some important business loan eligibility criteria include:

  • Applicant’s Age: You must be between 21 and 65 years old
  • Business Type: The type of business you run (like a proprietorship, private limited company, or partnership)
  • Business Age: Your business age must be at least 2 years
  • Turnover: Your business turnover must be at least ₹1 crore
  • Business Documents: Good financial records like bank statements, audited accounts, or GST returns
  • CIBIL Score: A CIBIL score above 700 and a steady income

Private and public banks traditionally have strict eligibility criteria. However, many NBFCs now offer collateral-free business loans with flexible business loan requirements. NO matter the situation, here are the tips you can follow to improve your chances of business loan approval:

  • Keep your finances clean and updated, as it shows a lender a clear picture of the business’s transactions.
  • File your taxes and GST on time, as it shows that your business is compliant, stable, and transparent.
  • Use the banking system regularly to make cash flow analysis easier for lenders.
  • Pay your EMIs and credit card bills on time to keep a good credit score.

High interest rates and unfavourable business loan terms are becoming major issues for businesses. As loan repayment rates rise, it becomes difficult for MSMEs to face financial situations. With higher monthly payments, businesses struggle to meet obligations, leading to a higher risk of default.

When interest rates go up, businesses have to pay more money on their loans without making more money to balance it out. This can be challenging for small businesses to cover essential costs like payroll, rent, and inventory.

Problem #3: Insufficient Loan Amounts

Obtaining insufficient funding when they apply for a business loan is one of the most common problems that business owners face. Even if the bank approves their loan, the loan amount for business is often too small to cover all the costs, like buying equipment, hiring staff, or growing operations. This makes it hard for the business to survive or grow.

For example, a small business might need ₹10 lakhs for either machinery or raw materials, but only get ₹3 lakhs approved. This puts pressure on the owner to cut corners, negatively affecting growth, business operations, and investment in essential resources. Underfunded loans also lead to cash flow issues and missed opportunities.

Being a small business owner, you can struggle with insufficient loan amounts. The solution is to explore other funding sources like government schemes, NBFCs, or online lenders like FlexiLoans. You can also consider combining a small loan with a grant or investor business funding. In a nutshell, when you plan the funding mix smartly, you’d better avoid being stuck with too little money.

Problem #4: Lengthy Approval Process

A delay in business loan approval time affects businesses that need quick access to funds, especially during emergencies or peak demand periods. Here are key pointers on how long loan approval times can negatively affect businesses in urgent need of funding:

  • Lost Opportunities: Loan process delays can cause you to miss time-sensitive deals, partnerships, or growth chances.
  • Cash Flow Stress: Without quick funding, you will struggle to pay suppliers, employees, or manage inventory.
  • Poor Credibility: Inability to deliver due to cash constraints can harm relationships with clients and vendors.
  • Operational Disruptions: Essential activities like production, marketing, or expansion will stall.
  • Competitive Disadvantage: While you wait, competitors with faster funding may capture your market share.

Problem #5: Repayment Stress and Cash Flow Issues

When you manage business loan repayment, it fosters healthy cash flow management. However, it can also become a major source of financial stress. If your monthly EMIs are high or your income is inconsistent, your business might face difficulty covering daily expenses. This can lead to late payments, reduced creditworthiness, and disruptions in operations. To reduce this stress, you can follow FlexiLoans tips to improve cash flow management:

  • Set clear repayment goals and timelines.
  • Track your expenses regularly to avoid surprises.
  • Automate EMI payments to prevent missed deadlines.
  • Build a financial buffer or emergency fund.
  • Reassess and adjust your budget as business needs change.

Additionally, if you’re juggling with loan repayment stress, handling multiple loans or facing tight repayment terms, consider refinancing or consolidating your loans. This can lower your interest rates or extend your repayment tenure, making cash flow more manageable.
