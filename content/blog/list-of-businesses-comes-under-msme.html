What is the Role of MSME in India?

It would not be an overstatement to say that the Indian MSME sector is the backbone of the Indian economy. The MSME sector has worked relentlessly to protect the country’s economy in the face of various global crises. Several potential adversities caused by external factors have been warded off due to the thriving MSME sector in our country.

The list of MSME-registered companies is set to touch 63.4 million in India soon. The businesses under MSME contribute 6.11% of the manufacturing GDP of India. Apart from that, 24.63% of GDP from the service sector and 33.4% of the manufacturing output in India comes from the MSME sector. 

The MSME sector is one of the largest sources of livelihood in our country, employing over 120 million people. As most of the MSME units exist in rural areas, this sector is a significant source of rural employment.

What is the classification of MSME?


The MSME segment consists of micro, small, and medium enterprises. There are specific parameters under which each section has been classified. Below, please find the table showing the parameters of classification:

How do MSME Loans Work?

Despite being the backbone of the economy, MSME sector businesses have always been deprived of the benefits of formal banking. Due to this, these units are majorly dependent on private sources of funds whenever they require additional money. The union government identified this gap and introduced the Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY) on April 8, 2015, to cater to the MSME segment.  The PMMY is a collateral-free business loan scheme targeted at the MSME segment. Apart from PMMY, many other business loan schemes have also been introduced to boost this segment.

What is MSME Business Loan?

As an entrepreneur, you may need additional funds to meet some business needs, such as executing a large order. Also, you may have plans for expansion or diversification, which requires money. In cases such as this, it is advisable to go for a business loan rather than diluting your own funds. Most leading banks and non-banking financial companies offer secured and unsecured business loan packages. You may choose the scheme most suited to your needs and apply for a business loan.

One of the major factors to consider when choosing your financier is the business loan interest rates they offer. Every financial institution has its business loan packages posted on its official website. Perusing official websites will help you determine that particular financier’s business loan eligibility criteria. Also, official websites of banks have online business loan EMI calculators embedded on their websites for public consumption. This calculator can help you determine your potential quantum of monthly repayment via EMI, thus letting you better plan your finances.
