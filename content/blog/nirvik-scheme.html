NIRVIK Scheme – (Niryat Rin Vikas Yojna) – Application Process, Advantages

The NIRVIK Scheme, or Niryat Rin Vikas Yojna, is a groundbreaking initiative by the Indian government to boost the export sector by providing enhanced insurance coverage and reducing business loan interest rates.

This scheme is specifically designed to streamline the export credit process, making it more accessible and efficient for exporters, thereby significantly bolstering India’s international trade landscape.

Key Features of the NIRVIK Scheme

The NIRVIK Scheme is characterised by several key features designed to support exporters:

  1. High Insurance Coverage: Exporters have insurance coverage up to 90% of the principal and interest amount. This coverage encompasses both pre-shipment and post-shipment credit, offering comprehensive protection against potential financial losses.
  2. Competitive Interest Rates: The scheme ensures that credit interest rates remain below 4% for foreign currency loans and are capped at 8% for rupee loans. This competitive rate structure significantly reduces the cost of borrowing for exporters.
  3. Special Provisions for High-Value Sectors: For exporters in high-value sectors like gems, jewellery, and diamonds, with amounts exceeding ₹80 crore, the scheme applies a higher premium rate due to the elevated loss ratio. This ensures that the insurance coverage is adequately priced according to the risk involved.
  4. Affordable Premium Rates: Exporters with account limits of up to ₹80 crore are charged a premium of 0.60% per annum. Those with higher account limits face a slightly higher premium of 0.80% per annum, making it affordable for most exporters to obtain necessary insurance coverage.
  5. Loss Recovery: If export losses exceed ₹10 crore, the ECGC (Export Credit Guarantee Corporation of India) will inspect the exporter. During this period, the bank continues to cover the monthly premium, ensuring the interest remains outstanding.

Benefits of the NIRVIK Scheme

The NIRVIK Scheme provides substantial benefits to exporters, which include:

1. Increased Export Credit: 

The scheme significantly assists small-scale exporters in boosting their export volumes by providing high credit disbursements. This support helps exporters expand their market reach and increase their revenue.

2. Enhanced Liquidity: 

With provisions for instant claim settlements and continuous working capital availability, exporters benefit from improved liquidity. This financial stability is essential for meeting operational expenses and investing in business growth.

3. Competitive Edge: 

The scheme enables small-scale Indian exporters to compete more in international and domestic markets by offering lower insurance premiums. This competitive edge is vital for securing new contracts and expanding market presence.

4. Job Creation: 

A thriving export sector fosters a healthy business environment, increasing job opportunities and economic growth. The scheme’s support for small-scale exporters contributes to broader economic development.

5. Enhanced Insurance Coverage: 

The scheme provides extensive insurance coverage to exporters, drastically reducing the risk of non-payment by international buyers. This coverage helps build confidence among exporters, who know that their financial interests are well-protected.

6. Reduced Business Loan Interest Rates: 

The scheme helps lower the business loan interest rates by offering a government-backed guarantee on export credits. This reduction in interest rates makes financing more affordable for exporters, enabling them to invest more in their business operations and growth.

7. Simplified Procedures: 

The scheme simplifies the procedures involved in obtaining export credit. This ease of process ensures that exporters can secure the necessary financing without navigating through overly complex or bureaucratic hurdles.

8. Improved Liquidity: 

The NIRVIK Scheme facilitates access to affordable business loans, which exporters can use to maintain better liquidity. This liquidity is crucial for managing day-to-day operations and ensuring the smooth running of export activities.

Application Process for the NIRVIK Scheme

Applying for the NIRVIK Scheme is a structured process designed to be straightforward for small-scale exporters. Here’s a step-by-step guide to help you navigate through the application:

Step 1: Check Eligibility

Before starting the application process, ensure your business meets the eligibility criteria:

  • Business Size: The scheme is available only to small-scale businesses.
  • Citizenship: The owner of the export business must be a citizen of India.
  • Bank Account: The business must have an active bank account in India.

Step 2: Gather Required Documents

Collect all necessary documents required for the application:

  • Identity Proof: Valid identity proof such as an Aadhar card, passport, etc.
  • Business Registration: Documents to prove that the business is legitimate and the applicant is the actual owner.
  • Business PAN Card: A PAN card registered under the name of the export company.
  • GST Certificate: The business’s GST registration certificate.
  • Insurance Policies: All documents related to existing insurance policies for the owner and the company.
  • Existing Loan Documents: If the business has an active bank loan, provide all documents related to the existing loan.

Step 3: Visit the ECGC Office or Website

You can visit the nearest Export Credit Guarantee Corporation of India (ECGC) office or their website. The ECGC is responsible for implementing the NIRVIK Scheme.

Step 4: Fill Out the Application Form

Obtain the NIRVIK Scheme application form from the ECGC office or download it from the official website. Fill out the form with accurate information, ensuring all details match the documents you provided.

Step 5: Attach Supporting Documents

Attach all the required documents to your application form. Ensure that all copies are clear and legible to avoid any delays in processing.

Step 6: Submit the Application

If applying online, submit the completed application form along with the attached documents to the ECGC office or upload them through the ECGC website.

Step 7: Verification Process

Once submitted, the ECGC will review your application and documents. During the verification process, they may contact you for additional information or clarification.

Step 8: Approval and Coverage

After successful verification, you will receive confirmation of your application’s approval. Your business will then be covered under the NIRVIK Scheme, which provides enhanced insurance coverage and reduced business loan interest rates.

Step 9: Maintain Compliance

Ensure ongoing compliance with the scheme’s requirements, such as paying the necessary premiums on time and adhering to any reporting obligations. This will help maintain your coverage and benefits under the NIRVIK Scheme.
