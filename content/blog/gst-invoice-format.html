WHAT IS AN INVOICE AND WHAT ARE THE NEW THINGS TO BE ADDED TO A GST INVOICE?

An invoice is like a bill which has the summary of all the goods and services that have been/will be provided with a sum of the amount that is due. An invoice includes the actual charge of the goods and services along with different charges such as VAT, Indirect Tax, Import/Export charges etc.

With GST being rolled out from the 1st of June, 2017 all the miscellaneous tax charges and overheads will be combined under just one head mainly known as the – Goods and Service Tax. Under this, the charges are divided as either Central and State or Central and Inter-State Tax.

Key Components of a GST Invoice

  1. Name, address and GSTIN of the supplier
  2. Name, address and GSTIN of the recipient (if registered)
  3. Unique invoice serial number
  4. Date of issue
  5. HSN code for goods or accounting code for services
  6. Description and quantity of goods or services
  7. Total value of supply before tax
  8. Taxable value after factoring discounts
  9. GST rate (including CGST, SGST, IGST or cess)
  10. Place of supply and state (for inter-state transactions)
  11. Signature or digital signature of the supplier

Types of GST Invoices

  1. Regular GST Invoice: Issued for taxable supplies to registered recipients.
  2. Bill of Supply: Suppliers issue a bill of supply for exempt goods or services or when the recipient is unregistered, provided the transaction value is below ₹200.
  3. Debit Note: Issued to increase the taxable value or GST amount on an original invoice.
  4. Credit Note: Issued to decrease the taxable value or GST amount on an original invoice.
  5. Self-Invoice: Issued by the recipient in case of reverse charge transactions where the recipient pays GST.

WHO SHOULD ISSUE A GST INVOICE?

Every individual who has registered themselves on the GST portal and fall under the GST rule needs to issue a GST invoice for their suppliers and dealers.

If you are a small business/enterprise and do not fall under the minimum GST bracket, it is not necessary for you to issue a GST invoice. Check the List of GST Tax Slabs to find which business/enterprise falls under which GST slab. However, your suppliers and dealers registered under GST will provide you with a GST invoice.

WHAT ARE THE MANDATORY FIELDS OF THE GST INVOICE?

The GST invoice can be in different formats and have personalized value additions depending on firm to firm. However, there are certain fields that are mandatory and common between all the invoices. Below is the list of all the mandatory fields one needs to ensure on their GST Invoice.

  • Name, address, and GSTIN of supplier
  • Invoice number and Date
  • Type of invoice – Tax Invoice, supplementary invoice, revised invoice etc.
  • Customer and Taxpayers GSTIN (If registered under the GST Portal)
  • HSN or SAC code for goods and services provided
  • Details of goods and services provided – i.e. Description of goods and services, Quantity, Unit, Final Value
  • Total amount value of the goods and services provided
  • Amount of discounts if any
  • Tax Rate for every item
  • GST Rate % and total value under Central and State or Central and Inter-State (CGST, SGST, IGST)
  • Place of supply and name of supplying state
  • If GST is payable on a reverse charge basis
  • Address of delivery (If different from the place of supply)
  • The total amount of goods and services include discounts and taxes
  • Signature/Digital Signature of the supplier or authorized person

WHAT ARE THE TYPES OF GST INVOICES?

Bill of Supply

There are a few goods and services that come under the 0% tax bracket. Due to this, the suppliers cannot charge any tax but this does not mean they do not issue any proof of document. Such dealers have to issue a Bill of Supply. A bill of supply is issued when GST is not applicable to a transaction or when GST is not to be recovered from the customer.

Aggregate Invoice

Quite often the value of multiple bills is less than a particular value and the buyer is not registered under the GST portal. Here, the supplier can issue an aggregate bill or an aggregative invoice. This is actually a single invoice with values over a period of time.

Debit and Credit Notes

A debit note is issued when the amount paid to the supplier increases while a credit note is issued when the amount paid to the supplier decreases. There are various conditions and instances under which debit and credit notes are issued.
