What is the Udyogini Scheme?

The Udyogini Scheme is a government-sponsored scheme that provides business loan facilities on a subsidised basis to women entrepreneurs. It primarily aims at empowering women belonging to the economically weaker sections of society. It includes those from Scheduled Castes (SC), Scheduled Tribes (ST), living in rural areas and falling under the Below Poverty Line (BPL) category.

Women nearly make up 50% of India’s population but contribute only 18% to the country’s GDP. Although the number of Indian women entrepreneurs is rising, they still face obstacles such as restricted access to capital. The Udyogini Scheme offers them easier access to capital.

The Government of Karnataka initially introduced the scheme. The Central Government later adopted it across India to support women in establishing businesses, becoming self-reliant and increasing the participation of women in economic activities by providing low-interest or interest-free loans.

Key Features of the Udyogini Scheme

Here are the key features of the Udyogini Scheme you must consider before you apply for the Udyogini Scheme:

  • Loan Amount: The scheme enables you to get business loans for women of up to ₹3 lakhs to address multiple business requirements.
  • Collateral Free: The scheme offers collateral-free business loans, which eliminates the need to provide any assets as security.
  • Subsidy: You receive a 30 % subsidy (Special benefit for SC/ST and BPL), which varies based on the category.
  • Interest Rate: As compared to the formal lending options, the interest rate of the Udyogini scheme is low and starts at only 6% per annum.
  • Institutional Support: There are many government agencies and financial institutions that back the scheme, giving you more lenders to choose from.
  • Sector Diversity: The Udyogini scheme focuses on different sectors, including agriculture, handicrafts, retail, and various service industries.
  • Economic Self-Reliance: The scheme promotes financial independence among women entrepreneurs by giving them easy access to business capital.

Benefits of the Udyogini Scheme

The unit cost for women who are members of Scheduled castes and Scheduled tribes ranges from ₹1,00,000 to ₹3,00,000. 50% of the loan amount is subsidised. Also, the family’s annual income cannot exceed ₹2,00,000.

The maximum unit cost for women in the general and special categories is ₹3,00,000. Special category women get a 30% subsidy, whereas general category women receive a maximum of ₹90,000, with specific recipients receiving EDP training. Here are the key financial benefits of the Udyogini scheme that you must know as a woman entrepreneur:

  • Comprehensive Financial Support: It provides capital for both new business ventures and the expansion of already existing businesses.
  • Promote Self-reliance: The Udyogini scheme reduces dependency on informal credit sources that often charge higher interest rates.
  • Special Considerations: It offers additional advantages for women from SC/ST communities and BPL families.
  • Accessible Financing: The Udyogini yojana scheme eliminates collateral requirements, making business loans more accessible to women without significant assets.
  • Skill Enhancement: To help women enhance their expertise in their chosen fields, it provides skill development and training programs.
  • Reduced Financial Pressure: The low interest rate allows businesses to expand and grow without the burden of excessive debt.

How to Apply for Udyogini Scheme?

Follow these steps for an easy Udyogini Yojana scheme apply online application process:

  1. Ensure Eligibility: You must be a woman between 18 and 55 years old with Indian citizenship and an income below ₹1.5 lakh.
  2. Gather all Necessary Documentation: Gather documents like identity proof, address proof, income proof, business documentation and banking information.
  3. Visit a Participating Financial Institution: There are many banks, NBFCs and financial institutions that participate in the Udyogini Scheme. Visit the lender with the required documents.
  4. Fill out the Application Form: Collect the Udyogini scheme application form from the participatory lender or institution and fill it out with the correct details.
  5. Attach all Required Documents: Attach all the required documents you have brought with you, ensuring accuracy and completeness.
  6. Submit the Application: Finally, submit the Udyogini Scheme loan application with the form and required documents.
  7. Await Verification and Approval: Your documents and details will now go under verification. Once the bank or lender approves your application, you will receive the money.

Business Sectors Covered Under the Udyogini Scheme

The Udyogini Scheme supports multiple industries, enabling women to establish and grow businesses across diverse sectors:

1. Agriculture and Allied Activities

Women entrepreneurs engaged in poultry, dairy, farming, fisheries, and other agricultural ventures can utilise funds from the Udyogini Scheme to purchase equipment, seeds, fertilisers, and livestock. This financial support enables rural women to improve productivity and enhance the profitability of their agricultural businesses.

2. Retail and Trading Businesses

The Udyogini yojana scheme provides valuable support for small retail establishments, grocery shops, Kirana shops, textile businesses, and various trading activities. Women entrepreneurs can use these business loans to expand operations, stock inventory and build a stronger customer base for their retail enterprises.

3. Handicrafts, Textiles, and Cottage Industries

Traditional crafts such as handloom weaving, pottery, embroidery, and other artisanal activities receive support under the Udyogini Scheme. These loans enable women artisans to acquire raw materials, upgrade machinery, and develop marketing resources to scale their ventures effectively.

4. Service-Based Businesses

Women operating beauty parlours, tailoring units, repair shops, or small service centres can benefit significantly from the scheme. You can use the funds for equipment purchases, infrastructure improvements, and skill development initiatives to enhance service quality.

Entrepreneurship Development Program (EDP) Under Udyogini

The Entrepreneurship Development Program (EDP) is both an integral and mandatory component of the Udyogini Scheme. This training initiative is for qualified applicants who can access it after loan sanction but before fund disbursement. The program equips women entrepreneurs with essential business management skills, including:

  • Financial record-keeping
  • Marketing strategies
  • Inventory management
  • Business planning
  • Customer relationship management
  • Basic accounting principles

The EDP serves as a crucial educational platform that prepares women to effectively manage their day-to-day business operations, enhancing their chances of entrepreneurial success. This holistic approach combines financial support with practical knowledge. It creates a comprehensive system that addresses both monetary and skill-based challenges faced by women entrepreneurs.

Functional Literacy Support

Beyond financial assistance, the Udyogini Scheme incorporates skill development programs to promote functional literacy among women in economically disadvantaged regions. This educational component helps semi-literate adult women perform essential business tasks independently, including:

  • Basic reading and writing
  • Fundamental calculations
  • Record maintenance
  • Simple business correspondence

This functional literacy support represents the scheme’s commitment to holistic empowerment, recognising that entrepreneurial success requires both financial resources and practical capabilities.

The Udyogini Scheme has made significant contributions to women’s entrepreneurship in India, particularly in rural and semi-urban areas. Its impact extends beyond mere financial assistance:

The scheme’s multifaceted approach addresses not only the financial barriers but also the educational and social challenges that have traditionally limited women’s participation in entrepreneurial activities.
