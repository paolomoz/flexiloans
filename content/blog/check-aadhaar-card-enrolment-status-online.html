What is Aadhaar?

Aadhaar is a unique 12-digit identification number issued to residents of India by the UIDAI. It captures your demographic and biometric data, including fingerprints and iris scans, and stores this information in the Resident Database. This number is crucial for accessing various government and private services.

How to Check Aadhaar Card Update Status via Mobile Phone

Keeping your Aadhaar card details up to date is essential, especially if you’ve recently updated information such as your address, name, or contact details. Luckily, you can easily check the status of your Aadhaar update using your mobile phone. Follow these steps to find out how:

Step 1: Open the UIDAI Website

Start by opening your mobile browser and navigating to the official website of the Unique Identification Authority of India (UIDAI) at uidai.gov.in. The site is optimised for mobile use, ensuring a smooth browsing experience.

Step 2: Navigate to the Update Status Section

Once you’re on the UIDAI homepage, locate the “My Aadhaar” section. Tap on it, and from the menu that appears, choose “Check Aadhaar Update Status.” This will direct you to the page where you can input your details to see the status of your update request.

Step 3: Input Your URN or SRN

You’ll need to enter your Update Request Number (URN) or Service Request Number (SRN), which you received when you submitted your update. Along with this, provide your Aadhaar number in the respective fields.

Step 4: Complete the Security Verification

To verify your identity, you’ll be prompted to complete a CAPTCHA verification. Type in the CAPTCHA code as it appears on the screen, then click the “Check Status” button.

Step 5: Review Your Update Status

After submitting the necessary details, the status of your update will be displayed on the screen. This status will indicate whether your request has been approved, is still being processed, or has been rejected, along with the reason for rejection if applicable.

How Can I Check Aadhaar Card Enrolment Status Online

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Head to the official UIDAI website. This is the primary portal for all Aadhaar-related services, including online verification.
  2. On the UIDAI homepage, find the section titled ‘Check Aadhaar Status’. Click on it to proceed.
  3. You will need your Enrolment ID (EID), given on the acknowledgment slip you received at the Enrolment Center. Enter the 14-digit EID and the 14-digit date and time of enrolment printed on the slip.
  4. Enter the security captcha as shown on the screen to verify you are not a robot.
  5. Once you have entered all required details, click on the ‘Check Status’ button.

What Your Status Can Indicate

Understanding the status of your Aadhaar card application helps you know where you stand in the process and what actions, if any, you need to take. Here’s a more detailed look at what each status means:

  1. Under ProcessingIf your status shows “Under Processing,” this means that your application is still in the queue for verification and approval. The UIDAI is reviewing the details you submitted, including your biometric data and documents.
  2. Action Required: You don’t need to take immediate action during this phase. However, periodically check the status on the UIDAI website to stay updated on your application’s progress. If the processing time exceeds the usual duration, contact the UIDAI helpline for more information.
  3. GeneratedWhen the status indicates “Generated,” it means that your Aadhaar number has been successfully created. This is a positive sign, as it indicates that your application has been processed and approved.
  4. Action RequiredYou can now proceed to download your Aadhaar card from the UIDAI website. To do this, visit the “Download Aadhaar” section on the UIDAI homepage, enter your Aadhaar number, and complete the necessary verification steps. Downloading the card allows you to have a digital copy for immediate use, which can be crucial for various applications, including applying for a loan for business.
  5. RejectedIf your status shows “Rejected,” it indicates that there were issues with your application that prevented its approval. This could be due to incorrect information, incomplete documents, or issues with your biometric data.
  6. Action RequiredYou will need to visit an Enrolment Centre to re-enrol and correct any errors. Bring along the correct documents and ensure that all details are accurately provided. The Enrolment Centre staff will assist you in reapplying and address any issues that led to the rejection of your previous application. Re-enrolling is essential to ensure that you receive a valid Aadhaar card, which is important for availing services such as government subsidies and business loans.

By understanding these statuses, you can effectively manage your Aadhaar application process and ensure timely receipt of your card. If you encounter any issues, timely action can help you resolve them and proceed with necessary applications and services smoothly.

Checking Aadhaar Enrolment Status Without Enrolment Number

  1. Visit the UIDAI Website Click on “Retrieve EID/UID” under the “My Aadhaar” section.
  2. Select Retrieval Option Select whether you want to retrieve your Aadhaar number or enrolment number.
  3. Enter Your Details Enter your name, email address or mobile number registered with UIDAI, and the security code.
  4. You will receive an OTP on your registered mobile number.
  5. Retrieve Your Number Upon successful verification, your Aadhaar number or enrolment number will be sent to your registered mobile number or email address.
  6. Check Your Status Use the retrieved enrolment number on the UIDAI website by entering it along with the date and time mentioned on your acknowledgment slip.

Downloading Your Aadhaar Card

  1. Visit the UIDAI Website Go to the UIDAI homepage and select ‘Download Aadhaar’.
  2. Enter Your Aadhaar Number, Enrolment ID, or Virtual ID, and complete the security captcha.
  3. You will receive a one-time password (OTP) on your registered mobile number. Enter this OTP to proceed.
  4. Once authenticated, you can download the PDF version of your card. Use the password provided by UIDAI to open the file, and print your card.

Updating Your Aadhaar Details

If you need to update your Aadhaar details, such as your address or phone number, follow these steps:

  1. Visit the UIDAI Website Go to the ‘Update Your Aadhaar’ section on the UIDAI homepage.
  2. Input your Aadhaar number and request an OTP, which will be sent to your registered mobile number.
  3. Enter the OTP and click on ‘Login’.
  4. Select the Fields to Update Choose the fields you wish to update and upload the necessary documents.
  5. Submit the Request Submit your update request and note the Update Request Number (URN) for tracking your update status.

How can I Verify my Aadhaar PVC Card Order Status

  1. Visit the official Aadhaar website and locate the “Check Aadhaar PVC Card Order Status” section.
  2. Enter your SRN (Service Request Number) and complete the captcha verification.
  3. Click on the “Submit” button to proceed tracking your Aadhaar PVC card order.
  4. The system will display the current status of your Aadhaar PVC card order, indicating whether it is in process, dispatched, or delivered.

How Can I Check My Aadhaar Card Complaint Status

  1. Visit the Official UIDAI Website: Navigate to the “Check Complaint Status” option under the “Aadhaar Services” section.
  2. Enter Your Details: Input your Complaint ID or Service Request Number (SRN) received when you filed the complaint.
  3. Captcha Verification: Provide the captcha code for verification and click on “Submit”.
  4. The website will display the current status of your Aadhaar card complaint, indicating whether it is in process, resolved, or if any further action is required.

If you don’t have the Complaint ID, you can track the status using your Aadhaar number or enrolment ID (EID) by visiting the “Check Aadhaar Status” section on the UIDAI website. Alternatively, you can check the status by calling the UIDAI helpline number 1947 or visiting an Aadhaar enrolment centre in person.

How Can I Check Aadhaar Card Lock Status

To check the lock status of your card, follow these steps:

  1. Access the UIDAI website and find the section dedicated to Aadhaar services.
  2. Locate the option to check the biometric lock or unlock status of your Aadhaar card.
  3. Input your 4-digit PIN and complete the necessary verification steps to confirm your identity.
  4. The system will display the lock status of your biometrics, indicating whether they are locked or unlocked.

How to Check Biometric Lock/Unlock Status

To check, you can follow these steps:

  1. Visit the UIDAI Website: Click on “My Aadhaar”.
  2. Enter Your 4-Digit PIN: Input your PIN to log in.
  3. Check the Biometrics Lock Icon: The Biometrics Lock icon will appear in red on the screen if your biometrics are locked.
  4. A red Biometric Lock icon will be displayed when you log in to your UIDAI account.
  5. Authentication Restriction: When you lock your biometrics, you cannot authenticate using your fingerprints or iris.
  6. Use mAadhaar App: You can also check through the mAadhaar app by clicking on “My Aadhaar” and entering your 4-digit PIN.

How can I Check Aadhaar Bank Linking Status

To check, you can follow these steps:

  1. Visit UIDAI Website: Go to the official UIDAI website and navigate to the “Check Aadhaar Bank Linking Status” section.
  2. Enter your Aadhaar number and captcha code, then click on “Send OTP” to receive a one-time password on your registered mobile number.
  3. Input the OTP to confirm your identity and continue.
  4. Click on the “Bank Seeding Status” tab to track, indicating whether the process is complete or pending.
