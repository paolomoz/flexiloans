What is NEFT and How Does it Work?

NEFT stands for National Electronic Funds Transfer, which is a payment system in India that allows bank-to-bank transfers. The Reserve Bank of India operates the payment system and allows customers to transfer money from one bank account to another.

It is a popular method for transferring money between banks in India. It is vital for electronic banking and enables secure, digital transactions. Throughout the day, NEFT transactions are carried out hourly and handled in batches. It is a popular and safe way to transfer money for a number of uses, including online shopping, bill payment, and salary payments.

NEFT transactions are processed in batches of 30 minutes. A NEFT transfer that is started at 10:02 AM, for instance, will be settled by 10:30 AM. If a transfer is requested at 10:25 AM, it will also be handled by 10:30 AM.

How NEFT Works?

In NEFT, transactions are processed in batches. They are also settled in hourly intervals throughout the day. Here is how NEFT works:

  • Initiate Transfer: Start the transfer by adding the beneficiary’s name, account number, IFSC code, and transfer amount.
  • Bank Verification: The bank verifies the beneficiary’s information. After verification, your bank deducts the designated amount from the account.
  • Transaction Sent to RBI NEFT System: The Reserve Bank of India’s NEFT clearing centre receives the transaction data from the bank.
  • RBI Batches Transactions: 48 times a day, including weekends and holidays, RBI gathers all transfer requests and processes them in half-hourly batches.
  • RBI Notifies Recipient Bank: The RBI notifies the destination bank to credit the beneficiary’s account with the payment instructions.
  • Recipient Bank Credits Funds: As soon as the receiving bank gets the RBI’s directive, it credits the funds to the beneficiary’s account.
  • Transfer Complete: Depending on batch time, the transaction is now successfully finished, often in a few hours.

How to Check NEFT Status and Transaction Timing?

Batches of NEFT transactions are completed every 30 minutes. Tracking your NEFT transaction status helps you check if your transaction is processed or rejected. Here is how you can check NEFT transaction status:

NEFT Status via Mobile Banking App

  • Log in to Mobile App: Open the mobile banking app for your bank and log in using your login information or biometric verification.
  • Go to Fund Transfer: To access the Fund Transfer/Payments section, tap on the “Transfer” or “Payments” area and choose NEFT.
  • Add or Select Beneficiary: Enter the beneficiary’s name, account number, and IFSC code to add a new beneficiary or select an existing one.
  • Wait for Activation: Before starting the transfer, new beneficiaries must wait the bank’s required cooling-off time.
  • Enter Amount and Notes: Enter the transfer amount, together with any optional notes or the reason for the payment.
  • Verify Transaction: To approve the transfer, use biometric authentication or an OTP.
  • Obtain Confirmation: After the NEFT is executed, you will get a notice or a success message.

NEFT Stats using Net Banking

  • Open Net Banking and Log in: Go to the online banking interface of your bank and enter your login credentials.
  • Navigate to Funds Transfer: Click the “Payments” or “Funds Transfer” tab, then choose NEFT from the list of alternatives.
  • Choose or Add Beneficiary: Choose an existing beneficiary or add a new one with all of their account and IFSC information.
  • Await Beneficiary Approval: Before enabling transfers to new beneficiaries, banks often need a short activation period.
  • Complete the Transfer Information: Choose the beneficiary, enter the transfer amount, and add any optional notes.
  • Confirm using OTP: Use an OTP that was issued to the email address or cellphone number you registered with to verify the transaction.
  • Get Acknowledgement: You will see or get an email with a confirmation message that includes the transaction reference.

NEFT Status via Bank’s Website

  • Visit the Official Bank Website: Log in and find the NEFT or money transfer option on your bank’s official website.
  • Secure Login: To access the NEFT portal, enter your registered login information.
  • Add/Select Beneficiary: Enter or choose the beneficiary’s information, such as their account number and IFSC.
  • Verify Beneficiary Registration: Prior to starting the transaction, wait for the beneficiary to be activated and validated.
  • Enter Transfer Details: Select your account, enter the required amount, and, if necessary, add comments.
  • Authenticate Transfer: Use the transaction PIN or OTP to authorise the transfer.
  • Track Transaction: Download the transaction receipt, if it is available, and see the real-time confirmation. 

Checking Delay and Contacting Support

If your NEFT transfer is delayed:

  • Step 1: Check transaction status using the steps above.
  • Step 2: Still unresolved? Contact your destination bank branch’s customer support via the bank’s helpline.

Benefits of Using NEFT for Fund Transfers

Here are the key advantages of NEFT transactions you must know:

  • 24/7 Accessibility: You have 24/7 access to the NEFT payment system. What this means is that you can transfer money throughout the year.
  • Safe Money Transfer: NEFT transactions guarantee safe settlement and almost instant money transfer to the beneficiary’s account. Safeguarded by the Reserve Bank of India.
  • Nationwide Reach: NEFT has a vast network of different banks and branches you can transfer to and from.
  • Convenience: You can transfer money from anywhere using online fund transfers and without standing in long queues.
  • Free Transactions: Online NEFT transactions for savings bank account holders are free.
  • No Minimum Limit: Unlike some payment methods that require a minimum transaction limit, NEFT allows the transfer of any amount. This is perfect for both small and large transactions.

In addition to money transfers, the NEFT system may be utilized for a number of other operations, such as inbound foreign currency remittances, loan EMI payments, and credit card payments to the card issuers. This is what the differences of NEFT vs RTGS vs IMPS are.

Common NEFT Errors and How to Avoid Them

While the NEFT is a useful bank transfer method, there are still common errors you will encounter. Here are all the common NEFT errors and the ways to avoid them:

  • Incorrect Beneficiary Details: One of the most frequent NEFT mistakes is entering the wrong beneficiary information. Ensure you double-check the account number and IFSC code before proceeding.
  • Insufficient Balance: Transactions fail if there isn’t enough money in your account. Always confirm your balance before initiating a transfer.
  • Bank-Specific Issues: Different banks, such as HDFC Bank and ICICI Bank, sometimes face technical issues or system outages. Keep updated via your bank’s notifications or customer service.
  • System Outages: The Reserve Bank of India schedules periodic maintenance, which can cause service downtimes. Be aware of such timings on national holidays like Gandhi Jayanti.
