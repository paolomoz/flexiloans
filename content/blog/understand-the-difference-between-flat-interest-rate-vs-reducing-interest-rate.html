What is Flat Rate and Reducing Interest Rate

Non-banking financial companies (NBFCs) and banks offer different types of financial assistance for fulfilling the applicants’ monetary needs. The financial aid includes an MSME loan for business, a home loan, a corporate loan, or a personal loan. When we apply for a loan, lenders give us the option of a flat rate or a reduced interest rate. We fail to understand the implications of the interest rate choices, which result in losses, causing us to fear taking loans in the future.

When you apply for a business loan, one of the most critical factors to consider is the interest rate. The interest rate determines how much you’ll pay over and above the principal amount borrowed. Two common types are the flat interest rate and the reducing interest rate. Understanding the difference between these two can significantly impact your business loan EMI repayment and the overall cost of borrowing.

Flat Rate and Reducing Balance: Know Everything

To help make a sound decision regarding the interest rate to select while going in for a loan, let us take a closer look at both flat rate and reducing interest rate options. Whenever we take a loan of a specified amount, we have to pay interest on the same. However, some lenders try to misguide borrowers. Therefore, it is crucial to learn the difference between a flat interest rate and a reducing interest rate. Let us first start by comprehending what interest is in loan amounts. It is a small portion of the loan amount that lenders ask the borrower to pay with the principal amount. Interest is calculated as a certain percentage of the loan amount. It is also known as the annual percentage rate and is a part of the monthly EMIs.

What is Flat Interest Rate?

A flat interest rate is a type of interest calculated on the entire amount of the loan. If you have applied for a business loan, the interest rate for business loans in India remains the same through tenure. Usually, flat interest rates are higher compared to reducing interest rates.

A flat interest rate is calculated on the entire principal amount for the full tenure of the loan, regardless of the outstanding balance. In other words, you’ll pay the same interest amount each month, even as your principal reduces with every EMI payment.

Here’s an example to illustrate:

You take a business loan of ₹10,00,000 at a flat interest rate of 12% per annum for a tenure of 5 years. Here is how the calculation of your EMI works:

Monthly EMI = [Principal + (Principal x Interest Rate x Tenure in Years)] / (Tenure in Months)
= [10,00,000 + (10,00,000 x 12% x 5)] / (5 x 12)
= [10,00,000 + 6,00,000] / 60
= 16,00,000 / 60
= ₹26,667 (rounded off)

In this case, you’ll pay ₹26,667 every month for 5 years. The total interest outgo over the tenure would be ₹6,00,000 (12% of ₹10,00,000 for 5 years).

Benefits of Flat Interest Rate Loan

Easy to Track and Calculate

The flat rate calculation is quite simple. Loan commitments made per a flat interest rate are transparent and easily tracked by both parties, the lender and the borrower. All the semi-financial institutions, such as village banks, self-help groups, and ASCA, offer flat MSME and corporate loan interest rates in India.

Flat Rate Loans Work Well to Meet the Financial Needs of Farmers

Many borrowers, including farmers, in developing countries look for loans that accept balloon payments. This is because a flat rate calculation is easier to understand.

Flat Rate Loans Favor In-kind Loan Transactions

The flat rate of interest concept came into existence before the invention of currency. It is the most common way of making regular instalments to repay the loan amount.

What is Reducing Rate of Interest?

A reducing rate of interest(also known as diminishing or declining interest rate) is calculated on the outstanding principal balance, which reduces with each EMI payment. As a result, the interest component of your EMI decreases over time, while the principal component increases. Here, the interest rate is calculated on the outstanding loan amount each month. When the principal amount reduces annually, it is known as annual rest.

Let’s consider the same example as above, but with a reducing interest rate of 12% per annum:

Formula for Calculating the Reducing Interest Rate

Interest payable per Instalment = (Original loan amount x No. of years x Interest rate p.a.) / Number of instalments

EMI = [P x Ix (1+I) ^T]/ [((1+I) ^T)-1)]

where

  • P = principal amount
  • I = rate of interest / (100×12)
  • T = number of years x 12

The EMI for a principal of ₹10,00,000 with a reducing interest rate of 12% per annum and a tenure of 5 years is approximately ₹22,244 per month

This process continues each month, with the interest component decreasing and the principal repayment component increasing. Over the 5-year tenure, the total interest outgo would be approximately ₹3,34,667.

Benefits of Reducing Balance Loan Interest Rate

The primary benefit associated with a reducing balance interest rate is that as time passes, the applicant has to pay lesser interest compared to flat interest rate loans. However, in the case of a flat rate, the loan will be repaid in a shorter duration, so the interest for the months that have been paid in advance need not be paid. However, in reducing interest rate, the duration of repayment and the interest component will also be impacted.

Factors Affecting Business Loan Interest Rates

  1. Credit Score: A good credit score indicates a lower risk for the lender, which can result in more favourable business loan interest rates.
  2. Business Vintage: Lenders often offer better rates to established businesses with a proven track record.
  3. Loan Amount and Tenure: The loan amount and tenure can impact the interest rate offered by lenders.
  4. Collateral: Secured loans, where you provide collateral, generally have lower interest rates compared to unsecured loans.
  5. Lender’s Policies: Interest rates usually vary among lenders based on their risk assessment and lending policies.

How to Choose the Right Business Loan Interest Rate?

When choosing between a flat interest rate and a reducing interest rate for your business loan, consider the following factors:

  1. Loan Tenure: If you opt for a longer loan tenure, a reducing balance rate may be more beneficial as it results in lower total interest paid over time.
  2. Cash Flow: Assess your business’s cash flow projections to determine which interest rate option aligns better with your repayment capacity.
  3. Total Cost of Borrowing: Use a business loan interest calculator to compare the total cost of borrowing under both interest rate options, factoring in the principal amount, tenure, and processing fees.
  4. Prepayment Options: Check if the lender allows prepayment of the loan and any associated charges. Prepaying a loan with a reducing balance rate can lead to significant savings on interest.
