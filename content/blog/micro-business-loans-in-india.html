What Is a Micro Business Loan?

A micro business loan is a small-ticket business loan typically ranging from ₹50,000 to ₹5 lakhs, explicitly designed for businesses, local traders, self-employed professionals, and MSMEs with limited credit history or collateral.

These loans address short-term working capital needs such as purchasing inventory, managing cash flow, upgrading tools or equipment, hiring seasonal staff, or expanding a local shop or home-based business.

Unlike traditional business loans, micro business loans are easier to access, involve minimal paperwork, and are often approved within 48–72 hours by digital NBFCs like FlexiLoans.

Practical Use Cases for Micro Business Loans:

1. A small retail shop borrows ₹1.2 lakhs to stock seasonal products ahead of Diwali.

2. A home-based bakery uses a ₹75,000 loan to upgrade its oven and packaging setup.

3. A mobile repair shop expands to a second kiosk using a ₹2 lakh micro loan.

These targeted, purpose-driven loans help business owners seize opportunities without waiting for bulky bank processes or risking personal assets.

Eligibility Criteria for Micro Business Loans in India

Micro business loans are specifically designed to support small enterprises that may not have solid assets or a lengthy credit history. While these loans are generally collateral-free, borrowers must meet specific eligibility criteria to qualify.

Here are the common requirements most lenders in India expect:

  • Age Limit: The applicant must be at least 21 years old at the time of application and not exceed 65 years old at the time of loan maturity.
  • Business Type: Loans are typically extended to sole proprietors, private limited companies, partnerships, and registered MSMEs involved in manufacturing, trading, or service sectors.
  • Business Vintage: Most lenders expect a business to be operational for a minimum of one year, although some may prefer two to three years for larger amounts.
  • Minimum Turnover: A monthly turnover of ₹2,00,000 or more is commonly required to assess repayment capability.
  • Credit History: While a CIBIL score of 700 or higher is generally preferred, some fintech lenders also evaluate alternative data, such as banking activity and GST returns.
  • Industry Experience: Founders or owners with 3–5 years of experience in the industry are considered more favourably.
  • Financial Compliance: The business should be tax-compliant, maintain accurate financial records, and have a clean repayment history.

These requirements can vary slightly between banks, NBFCs, and digital lenders. If you meet most of these conditions, you can explore various loan providers for a tailored offering.

Tip: Digital lending platforms like FlexiLoans evaluate applications online and often consider businesses that are newer or digitally active, even without extensive collateral.

Documents Required for a Micro Business Loan

While micro business loans are designed to be hassle-free and collateral-free, lenders still require some basic documents to assess your creditworthiness and business viability. The paperwork ensures that both the borrower and the lender are protected throughout the loan lifecycle.

Below are the most commonly required documents to apply for a micro business loan in India:

1. Identity Proof

  • Aadhaar Card.
  • PAN Card (mandatory for both individuals and businesses).
  • Voter ID / Passport / Driving License (any one as additional proof).

2. Business Proof

  • GST Registration Certificate (if applicable).
  • Udyam Registration / MSME Certificate.
  • Trade License or Shops & Establishment Certificate.
  • Business PAN (if separate from individual PAN).

3. Address Proof

  • Utility bills (e.g., electricity, water).
  • Rent agreement or property documents (if owned).

4. Financial Documents

  • Last 6–12 months’ current account bank statements.
  • Income Tax Returns (ITR) for the past 1–2 years (in some cases).
  • Profit & Loss statement or audited financials (optional but helpful).

5. Other Documents (if applicable)

  • Partnership deed, MOA/AOA (for partnerships or companies).
  • Post-dated cheques or ECS mandate.
  • Loan application form (duly filled and signed).

Note: Many digital lenders allow soft copies of these documents to be uploaded online. You don’t always need to visit a branch, especially for micro or small business loans.
