What is an Aadhaar Card?

An Aadhaar card is a legal document issued by the Unique Identification Authority of India on behalf of the Indian government to every resident. It contains a 12-digit number that is unique for all. The Aadhaar card works as identity proof and is valid for a lifetime. 

The Aadhaar card is attached with biometric verification, making it unique. It is required to open a bank account, apply for a driving licence or passport, and apply for a ration card because it serves the necessary KYC norms. Mostly, all government-related tasks require an Aadhaar card. 

The individuals who want to apply for an Aadhaar card will require some documents such as identity proof (passport, PAN, driving licence), address proof (electricity bill, mobile bill, bank statement), and biometric proof (scanned fingerprints and iris). 

What is a ration card?

A ration card is a legal document issued by the state governments in India. It is issued under the National Food Security Act, which aims to provide subsidised food grains to households at the lowest cost. The Public Distribution System issues it to all households that are eligible for it. 

The other segment is the Antyodaya Anna Yojana ration card, issued to the poorest households and helps them get 35 kg of food grains per month. Another ration card is the One Nation One Ration Card, issued to all, especially internal migrants in India. 

How can one link an Aadhaar card with a ration card offline?

There are two methods of linking an Aadhaar card with a ration card – one is offline, and the other is online. Linking an Aadhaar card with a ration card helps save the government from fraudulent activities of duplicate ration cards. People can apply for only one ration card after getting it linked with their Aadhaar card. 

The offline process to Link an Aadhaar Card with a ration card is as follows:

  • Step 1:

There are several Public Distribution System centres in city areas. One can find the one that is in their vicinity.  

  • Step 2:

An individual has to carry a photocopy of the Aadhaar cards of each family member, a ration card, and a passport-size photo of all family members. 

  • Step 3:

One must have their bank account linked with their Aadhaar card; if not, they must carry a photocopy of their bank’s passbook. 

  • Step 4:

One must submit all the above documents to the Public Distribution System office for verification. 

  • Step 5:

After submitting the documents, an individual must undergo biometric verification for authentication. One gets an email or SMS on their registered mobile number notifying them when their Aadhaar card gets linked to their ration card.

How to link an Aadhaar card with a ration card online?

The government has advanced the features of linking an Aadhaar card with a ration card. An individual can link their Aadhaar card with a ration card from anywhere and at any time through the online process. 

To link an aadhaar card with a ration card online, an individual has to follow a few steps:

  • Step 1:

Firstly, an individual has to visit the official website of the Aadhaar card and click on the “start now” button. Then, they must enter their full address, including the state and the district. 

  • Step 2:

After that, one needs to select the benefit type from the available options, i.e., ration card, and select the scheme name, i.e., ration card. 

  • Step 3:

In the next option, an individual has to enter their ration card number, email address, Aadhaar card number, and registered mobile number. 

  • Step 4:

The individual will receive a one-time password on their registered mobile number, which they must enter in the given columns. After entering the OTP, the screen will display the notification that the application has been processed successfully. 

  • Step 5:

It is the last step under which an authorised official verifies the submitted documents. If the details are correct, you will receive a notification of a successful aadhaar linking with a ration card on your registered mobile number. 

What are the benefits of linking an Aadhaar card with a ration card?

After linking Aadhaar cards with ration cards, the state governments in India will be able to see a decrease in fake ration card holders. The Aadhaar cards are verified with the biometric delivery system, and there is no chance of repetition because each person has unique fingerprints. It will make the system reliable and expand the proficiency of the PDS system.
