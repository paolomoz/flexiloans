Reasons Why Collateral-Free Business Loan Demand Is Popular In India

Posted Aug 28, 2024 · Updated Dec 2, 2025 · 10 min read

In recent years, India’s demand for collateral-free loans has surged dramatically. Entrepreneurs and small company owners are looking for funding alternatives that don’t need them to mortgage their assets. A business loan without collateral is a relatively secure alternative for organisations to obtain quick business loans without pledging any assets. The increasing demand is being driven by a number of factors, including the expansion of micro, small and medium-sized organisations (MSMEs), increased access to these loans, and government schemes like Udyam Yojana and Mudra Yojana providing unsecured business loans to facilitate expansion and growth. Let us explore collateral-free loans and why collateral-free business loans are so popular in India.

Let us understand what is a collateral-free loan and what it entails. A collateral-free loan is a kind of financing in which the borrower is not required to pledge any assets or mortgage any property. Unlike secured loans, which require assets such as real estate or machinery as security, collateral-free business loans are acquired solely on the borrower’s creditworthiness and business potential. This form of loan for business funding is very useful for small enterprises and startups that do not have significant assets to present and require MSME loans collateral-free.

Eligibility Criteria for Collateral-Free Business Loan in India

While collateral-free business loans are designed to be more accessible, they still come with specific eligibility criteria that borrowers must meet. These may vary from lender to lender. Some of the common requirements are as follows:

  • Business Vintage: Organisations applying for collateral-free loan must have minimum operational history of at least 1-3 years. It enables lenders to assess business performance, growth potential and stability of the borrower.
  • CIBIL Score: A strong credit history reflects positively to the lender, representing the borrower’s creditworthiness and healthy repayment history. It enables the lender to assess the risk of lending. A credit score of 650 or above is generally considered favorable for loan approval.
  • Annual Turnover: Although not a necessary requirement, lenders may require a minimum annual turnover criterion to be assured of the business’s financial soundness and that it will remain profitable before releasing funds. The turnover threshold varies depending on the lender and the loan amount.
  • Business Plan: A well-structured business plan and reliable financial documents are required for loan clearance. These documents give insights into the company’s prospects, cash flow, and profitability, allowing lenders to make more informed decisions.
  • Financial Statements: Recent financial statements, such as balance sheets, profit and loss accounts, and cash flow statements, may be required.
  • KYC documents: Standard Know Your Customer (KYC) documentation, such as PAN card, Aadhaar card, business registration certificate, identity proof, etc.

Pros and Cons of a Business Loan without Collateral

With comprehensive knowledge of what is collateral-free loan and its nuances, let us examine the possible pros and cons of securing a business loan without collateral:

Pros:

  • No Collateral Required: The primary advantage of collateral-free loan is that borrowers are not required to put their personal or corporate assets at risk.
  • Quick Approval: With minimal paperwork requirements and no assets to verify, lenders can process and approve loans more quickly.
  • Promotes Small Enterprises: Fuels growth of startups and SMEs by providing easy access to capital even if they lack significant assets.
  • Flexible Use: The funds can be utilised for a variety of business activities with no constraints set by the lender.
  • Improves Credit Score: Repaying collateral-free loans on time can assist in enhancing the company’s credit score, facilitating a healthy credit history and paving way for future funding opportunities.

Cons:

  • Higher Interest Rates: Collateral-free loan put lenders under higher with no asset to retrieve losses and that is why these unsecured business loans offer higher interest rates compared to secured loans.
  • Limited Loan Amount: Lenders may offer a limited sum compared to secured loans, restricting the financing options.
  • Stringent Eligibility Criteria: As there is no collateral, lenders may impose tougher eligibility restrictions.
  • Shorter Payback Terms: Although repayment terms are flexible, collateral-free loans have shorter payback terms than secured loan, putting a time-bound fulfilment burden on the borrower’s finances.
  • Personal Assurances: Certain lenders may request personal guarantees, placing the borrower’s credit at risk and may charge additional fees.
