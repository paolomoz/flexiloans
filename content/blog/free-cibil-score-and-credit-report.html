What is a CIBIL Score?

A CIBIL score is a three-digit numerical assessment of your creditworthiness ranging between 300 to 900. It is generated by the Credit Information Bureau (India) Limited (CIBIL) based on your credit history and financial behaviour. Lenders, including banks and financial institutions, use this score to assess the risk of lending you money. The higher your score, the greater your creditworthiness and the better your chances of securing your desired loan for business.

What is a Credit Report?

A credit report is generated when you perform CIBIL check and contains a thorough overview of your credit history. It contains information on your credit accounts, payment history, outstanding obligations, and any defaults or late payments. Personal information on your credit report includes your name, date of birth, and identity information. Lenders examine your credit report to analyse your financial behaviour and determine whether to approve your line of credit loan application. Why Is It Important to Maintain a Good CIBIL Score and What Are the Benefits?

  • Quick Business Loan Approval: Regularly checking your CIBIL score helps ensure it stays strong, increasing your chances of securing the working capital loan you need. Lenders prefer consumers with strong credit, as they are deemed low-risk.
  • Lower Business Loan Interest Rates: A strong CIBIL score often results in access to loans and credit cards with lower interest rates. Lenders offer better terms and reduced interest rates on business loans to individuals with good credit ratings.
  • Higher Credit Limits: A good CIBIL score can also lead to higher credit limits on your credit cards, giving you greater financial flexibility.
  • Quick Processing: A good credit score speeds up the processing of your working capital loan, leading to faster approvals and disbursements.
  • Enhanced Negotiation: Before you apply for business loan, generate free CIBIL report to evaluate your financial soundness and gain insights into negotiating desired loan conditions, interest rates, and other financial terms.


5 Essential Tips to Maintain a Good CIBIL Score

  1. Timely Payment of Dues: Always pay your credit card bills and loan EMIs on time. Late payments can significantly lower your CIBIL score. Set up alarms or automate payments to never miss out on deadlines.
  2. Low Credit Utilisation: Keep your credit utilisation ratio (the amount of credit used compared to your credit limit) under 30%. High credit utilisation signals higher lending risk.
  3. Healthy Credit combination: Maintain a healthy mix of secured loans, unsecured business loans, and personal loans. A diverse credit profile can positively impact your CIBIL score.
  4. CIBIL Check: Regularly make free CIBIL check to identify any discrepancies or errors that may harm your CIBIL score. In case of discrepancies, report to the Credit Bureau for rectification.
  5. Credit Limitation: Avoid applying for too many credit cards or loans, as this can negatively affect your CIBIL score. Only apply for and use credit as needed.
