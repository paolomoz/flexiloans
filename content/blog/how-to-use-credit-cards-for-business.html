How to Use Credit Cards for Business

Businesses need credit cards for a variety of reasons. They may need to make large purchases, such as equipment or inventory, and may not have the cash on hand to do so. Credit cards can also help businesses manage their cash flow by allowing them to make smaller purchases over time. Additionally, credit cards can provide businesses with rewards and perks, such as cashback or travel points.

Posted Sep 7, 2022 · Updated Jun 26, 2025 · 5 min read

Without a credit card, businesses may miss out on opportunities to make large purchases or take advantage of rewards and perks. This can ultimately hinder their growth and success.

Choosing the right business credit card is important if you’re a business owner.

What Is A Business Credit Card?

A business credit card is a type of credit card specifically designed for businesses. Business credit cards offer a variety of features and benefits that can be helpful for businesses, such as the ability to make large purchases, manage cash flow, and earn rewards.

Some benefits of using a credit card for business  include:

  • Using them to make online and in-person purchases
  • Rewards and perks that can save money, such as cashback on common business expenses like office supplies, gas, and travel
  • Building the business credit score
  • Managing the expenses with features like online account management and spending alerts
  • Creating a positive payment history that can reflect positively on the credit score will help qualify for better financing terms in the future.

How Do Business Credit Cards Work?

A business credit card in India works the same way as a personal credit card. Businesses can use their business credit card to make purchases and either pay the balance in full each month or carry a balance from month to month. Business credit cards also typically have higher credit limits than personal credit cards.

One major difference between business and personal credit cards is that businesses are often required to provide a personal guarantee when they apply for a business credit card. This means the business owner is responsible for repaying the debt if the business cannot do so.

How to choose the best business credit card

When choosing the best business credit card, businesses should consider their spending habits, business type, and the perks and rewards they’re looking for.

1. Find the right business credit card for your needs by providing the documents needed for the credit card. 

There are a lot of different business credit cards on the market, so it’s important to find one that meets your specific needs. 

For example, businesses that make large purchases regularly may benefit from a business credit card with a high credit limit. Those looking to manage their cash flow may benefit from a business credit card with 0% APR for an introductory period, while those that travel frequently may benefit from a business credit card that offers travel rewards.

2. Compare different business credit cards.

Once the options are narrowed down, compare different cards side-by-side. This will help you find the best card for business.

3. Fill out an application. 

Once you’ve found the right card, it’s time to fill out an application. You can apply for a business credit card online or offline by including all the required information. 

The documents required for a credit card are: 

  • Personal identification, such as a driver’s licence or passport
  • Business identification, such as a business licence or incorporation papers
  • Financial statements, such as bank statements or tax returns
  • Personal credit report
  • Business credit report
  • Application form
  • Annual fee, if applicable
  • Security deposit, if applicable
  • Co-signer, if applicable

4. Wait for approval. 

After applying, wait for the approval. If approved, you’ll receive the new business credit card in the mail within a few weeks.

5. Start using the business credit card 

Now that you have a business credit card, it’s time to use it. Use it wisely and only charge what you can afford to pay back.
