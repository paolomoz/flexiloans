Who is Eligible for a ₹50 Lakh Loan?


The ₹50 lakh business loan eligibility varies from lender to lender. Some common requirements include:

  • Business Vintage: The company must be operational for at least 2-3 years.
  • Turnover: The company should have a sustained yearly turnover of ₹1 crore or higher.
  • Credit Score: A decent credit score (650 or above) is required for business loan eligibility.
  • Profitability: The company should demonstrate sustained profitability over the years.

Eligibility Criteria for Business Loan

  • Age: Your age must be between 25 and 65 years old.
  • Business Type: You can apply for ₹50 lakh MSME loan without collateral if your company is a sole proprietorship, partnership firm, private limited company, or public limited company.

Documents Required for Business Loan

The following documents are required when applying for a ₹50 lakh business loan:

  1. KYC Documents: PAN card, Aadhaar card, voter ID, or passport
  2. Business Proof: Certificate of Incorporation, registration certificate, or GST registration.
  3. Financial Documents: Audited financial statements (balance sheet, profit and loss statement) for the last 2-3 years, bank statements (last 6 months).
  4. Income Tax Returns: For the last 2-3 years.
  5. Ownership Documents: Registration papers or lease agreements for the premises.
  6. Photographs: Passport-sized photographs of the applicant.

What Will Be the Monthly EMI for ₹50 Lakhs?

The monthly EMI for a debt of ₹50 lakh depends on the business loan interest rate and loan tenure. EMI is calculated using the following formula:

EMI = P x R x (1+R)^N / [(1+R)^N-1]

​Where:

  • P is the principal loan amount (₹50 lakh in this case).
  • r is the monthly business loan interest rate (annual interest rate divided by 12).
  • n is the loan tenure in months.

For example, if you take a ₹50 lakh loan at an interest rate of 12% per annum for 5 years (60 months), your EMI would be approximately ₹1,11,000 per month.
