Entrepreneurs are the backbone of any growing nation today. Their ability to contribute to society from job creation and innovation to philanthropic activities is well recognized. Entrepreneurial skill today goes far beyond traditional business skills of effective leadership qualities and strategic planning skills. Instead, an aspiring entrepreneur must have skills ranging from financial literacy and business planning to marketing and risk management skills.

In this article, we will understand the top 10 entrepreneurial skills and how we can improve them.

1. Financial Literacy

As an MSME, financial literacy is crucial entrepreneurial skill to secure business loan and manage finances . Lenders require you to submit business-specific documents, such as financial statements, cash flow projections, and budgets, to assess the eligibility for a business loan.

Entrepreneurs struggle to communicate their business’s key metrics to lenders because of inadequate financial knowledge. Consequently, this often leads to one of two outcomes:

  1. Lenders rejecting the MSME loan application.
  2. The MSME accepting unfavorable terms and conditions for the business loan, such as higher interest rates or significantly shorter repayment tenures.

To improve financial literacy, you may consider one of the following approaches:

Enrolling in online courses: Short-term and long-term courses are available online. These courses teach fundamentals of finance, such as accounting and bookkeeping, tax planning, fundraising, and financial modeling.

Attending workshops: While workshops often do not provide comprehensive financial knowledge, they can help MSME gain a basic understanding of balance sheets, income statements, and cash flow statements through real-life case studies and examples.

Hiring a financial advisor: Financial advisors offer personalized guidance based on the MSME’s unique business situation, objectives, risk tolerance, and investment goals.

2. Business Planning

This is an important entrepreneurial skill that showcases your ability to execute business strategy and operations. A business plan is one of the documents a lender requires when applying for an MSME loan.

In the absence of a well-crafted business plan:

  • Investors and lenders may reject your business proposition, considering it flawed and risky.
  • You may struggle to hire talented employees.
  • You may lose valuable customers and partnership opportunities with fellow entrepreneurs.

A well-thought-out business plan should typically include three main topics:

Market analysis: A thorough market analysis describes the target market in which you operate by answering the following questions:

  • Who are your target customers? For instance, the target customers of a meal delivery service company would be individuals residing within a 15KM radius of its central kitchen.
  • What are the demographics of these customers? This includes their age, gender, income level, etc.
  • What is the market share of your competitors?
  • How do you plan to differentiate your product or service from your competitors?

Financial projections: You also need to estimate future business performance backed by solid assumptions and data. Lenders expect you to estimate the following metrics in your business plan:

Sales forecast – Revenue estimates for the next three to five years.

Cost of goods sold – Estimated costs of producing your business’s goods or services.

Operating expenses – Estimated expenses such as salaries, rent, marketing, etc.

Income statements – Estimated net income.

Cash slow statements – Estimated inflow and outflow of cash.

Break-Even analysis – Estimated timeline for when the company reaches the break-even point (no profit or loss).

Operational strategy: This section of the business plan explains how you will execute the day-to-day operations of your business by answering below questions:

  • How will you produce the goods or deliver the service? The details include the manufacturing process, supply chain, and quality control measures.
  • Where will you produce the goods? This information includes the size, layout, and maintenance plans for the premises.
  • How will you fulfill deliveries to your customers? This detail includes transportation, warehousing, and order fulfillment processes.

4. Credit Management Skills

Access to any form of line of credit has become a lot simpler nowadays. Thanks to the growing popularity of credit cards, term loan and personal loans, etc. However, with easy access to capital also comes the risk of poorly managed credit score.

Late payments and fully utilised credit card limits affect your credit score negatively. A poor credit score may impact your future financing needs, higher interest rate, and opportunity to attract new clients and partners.

You can improve on credit management skills by following a few simple tips as given below:

  • Timely bill payment.
  • Using your credit card responsibly.
  • Monitoring credit reports.
  • Building credit history.
  • Separating business and personal loans.

5. Technology Adoption

Technology is significantly impacting the day-to-day functioning of business. As an entrepreneur, your business acumen to choose the right set of technology tools can put you ahead of your competitors. With the help of these tools, you can automate bookkeeping, invoicing, tax preparation, expense tracking, cash flow, financial reports, and many other jobs of your business.

Below are a few examples of how these tools can free up your resources and time:

  • Automatic invoicing and expense tracking tools help save time and resources by eliminating manual data entry processes.
  • Cash flow projections and budgeting tools help manage your business’ day-to-day liquidity requirements.

6. Data Skill

To grow your venture, you often need to develop new products or expand into a new market. You can’t rely on your gut instinct to make these critical business decisions.

As per Forbes, data skill is among the top 10 most-in demand and future skills. Lenders, investors, or clients feel confident about your business decision when enough facts, patterns, and trends are shown to them. 

You can adopt the below 3-step process to cultivate the data-driven entrepreneurial skill in your company:

  1. Learn to gather data from internal and external data sources. Internal sources include sales figures, customer feedback, and other operational metrics. External sources include industry reports, market research, and demographic data, etc..
  2. Leveraging data analytics tools can turn gathered raw data into meaningful insights in the form of charts and graphs.
  3. And lastly, no insight is meaningful unless you train yourself to see the results from a cause-and-effect perspective. Hence, cultivating an analytical mindset is important to drive data-driven business decision making.

7. Market research and opportunity identification

Market research is a critical entrepreneurial skill to understand target customers, industry trends, and competitors. Absence of thorough market research may lead you to following consequences:

  • Offering a product or service that has already saturated.
  • Offering a product that is overpriced or underpriced.
  • Run an ineffective marketing campaign leading to wastage of resources.

You can follow the below strategy to gather market intelligence:

  • Conduct customer surveys to understand their pain points and preferences.
  • Monitoring competitors’ product offerings and marketing campaigns to understand gaps and areas of differentiation.
  • Attending industry events and conferences in order to gain insights from experts.

8. Marketing and Branding Strategy

With the growing usage of social media and reducing span of attention, you may lose your loyal customers to competitors. Effective marketing skills can help your business in following ways:

  • Increasing brand awareness.
  • Attracting new customers.
  • Differentiating your product and service from competitors.

You can adopt the below-highlighted steps to equip yourself with this critical entrepreneurial skill:

  • Create a compelling story by designing a visually attractive brand identity like logo, color, and other design elements.
  • Differentiate your unique value proposition through marketing channels by highlighting customers’ problems.
  • Develop the marketing content that educates and entertains the customers yet informs them about your product or services. 
  • Continuously measure the performance of marketing campaigns using data and analytics.

9. Networking 

As an entrepreneur, you want to keep yourself updated with latest industry trends, expand your talent and secure funding. Effective networking skill can solve few of your business challenges by introducing you to someone who has already been in your shoes.

You can follow the below best practices of networking to improve on this skill:

  • Building connections with fintech companies, crowdfunding platforms, and angel investors can help you with business loan. Often the line of credit given by these connections is flexible.
  • Attending industry events and conferences may lead you to potential investors or pitch your product or service to a large forum.  
  • Creating a strong online personal brand on platforms like LinkedIn and Instagram establishes your credibility and expertise.

10. Risk Management 

As an entrepreneur, you don’t know what the future lies. Risk management skill enables you to plan for future unfavorable conditions. The adverse business situation may lead to following financial and operational challenges to your business:

  • Decreasing demand for a particular product
  • Decreasing cash reserves.
  • The outdated technology platforms, make business vulnerable to attacks from cyber threats.

You can follow the below step to equip yourself with this entrepreneurial skill:

  • Diversifying the revenue streams ensures multiple sources of income for your business.
  • Maintaining sufficient cash reserves helps you maintain cash flows during the off-season.
  • Cyber security measures eliminate customer data leaks, and system failures, etc.
