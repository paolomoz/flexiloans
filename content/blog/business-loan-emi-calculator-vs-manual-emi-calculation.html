What Is EMI and Why Does Accuracy Matter?

EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment) is the fixed monthly amount you repay towards your business loan. It includes both the principal and the interest.

Getting this number wrong, even by a small margin, can cause severe financial strain. For example:

  • Overestimating EMI may discourage you from borrowing enough.
  • Underestimating EMI could lead to missed payments and penalties.

Accurate EMI planning helps you:

  • Manage your working capital better.
  • Negotiate loan terms confidently.
  • Avoid repayment stress.

Manual EMI Calculation: Formula and Limitations

The standard EMI formula is: [P x r x (1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n – 1]

Where:

  • P = Principal loan amount
  • r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100)
  • n = Loan tenure in months

Example:

  • Loan Amount (P) = ₹5,00,000
  • Annual Rate = 12% (r = 0.01)
  • Tenure = 24 months (n = 24)

EMI ≈ ₹23,537/month

Limitations of Manual Calculation

  • Complex for non-finance users.
  • Time-consuming, especially when testing multiple scenarios.
  • High chance of errors in formula application.
  • Doesn’t instantly show interest vs principal breakup.
