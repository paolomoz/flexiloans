Appropriately Choose the Business Type and Structure

There is no one right answer that applies to everyone when determining the optimum company type for travel enterprises because everyone’s financial situation is unique. A professional lawyer and accountant can advise you on the ideal business entity for your travel company. They are the best and only people to do so.

Both an accountant and a lawyer should be consulted before you launch your travel company. Making the right choices from the beginning will help you avoid problems down the road with taxes and liabilities. You will have to spend some money to get this guidance, but it will be well spent. Also, you would want to ask yourself a few questions, like do you have partners in your travel firm, or do you intend to operate it alone?

You can register the business as either a private limited company or a limited liability partnership(LLP), depending on your ownership type. However, be aware that registration requirements will differ depending on your choice. Additionally, advantages and incentives like reasonable business loan interest rates will vary among lenders. 

Register with the Right Authorities

It makes no sense to start a travel agency that exclusively sells rail tickets and trip packages in the current tourist landscape. Rail, land, sea, and, most significantly, air are all necessary modes of transportation that you must discuss. Road, rail, and water travel do not need to be registered because most of it is domestic. Still, international air travel reservations need the International Air Transport Association (IATA) logo. 

IATA currently decides who should distribute air travel items and how they should be done. It sets guidelines for managing the travel industry. IATA codes, for instance, are employed wherever air travel and travel occur. Consider airline designator codes for scheduling, ticketing, and paperwork; a prefix code to categorise passengers and cargo; a baggage tag issuer code to streamline the handling of bags; and a location code to facilitate handling packages. Airlines that haven’t signed a contract with IATA can use these codes. 

You should also be registered as a travel agent with the Government of India. The Owner or a full-time employee knowledgeable about ticketing, itineraries, transportation, lodging options, currencies, customs laws, tourism and travel-related services should be in charge of the travel agency. Additionally, stronger emphasis might be placed on good communication abilities and knowledge of languages other than English.

A minimum of four qualified employees are also required. At least one must hold a diploma or degree in tourism and travel management from a university or another organisation recognised by the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE). One of the qualified employees would be the business’s owner.

Finally, you will need a GST registration. Without this registration, you will be unable to collect any Goods and Services Tax, a mandatory requirement. You will receive a unique GSTIN (Goods and Services Tax Identification Number) upon registration.
