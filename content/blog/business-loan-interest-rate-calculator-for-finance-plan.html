What Is a Business Loan Interest Rate Calculator?

A business loan interest rate calculator is an online financial tool that helps you quickly determine your EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment), total interest payable, and overall repayment amount based on the loan amount, tenure, and interest rate you enter.

Instead of relying on manual formulas or guesswork, the calculator gives you instant, accurate results, allowing you to:

  • Calculate Interest rates & EMI before applying for a loan.
  • Get a business loan repayment estimate in seconds.
  • Compare different lenders or loan products.
  • Align your loan choice with your business cash flow.

This is especially useful for MSMEs and SMEs in India, where loan terms can vary widely between banks and NBFCs. Running multiple scenarios ensures you choose the most cost-effective borrowing option.

How a Business Loan Interest Rate Calculator Works?

The Business Loan EMI calculator provides accurate repayment estimates based on three simple inputs:

  • Loan Amount (₹) – The total principal you plan to borrow.
  • Annual Interest Rate (%) – The yearly rate charged by your lender.
  • Loan Tenure (Months) – The duration for repayment.

Formula Used:
EMI = [P × R × (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N – 1]

Where:

  • P = Loan Amount
  • R = Monthly Interest Rate (Annual Rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100)
  • N = Loan Tenure in Months

Results You Get Instantly:

  • Monthly EMI – Your fixed monthly repayment amount.
  • Total Interest Payable – The total borrowing cost over the tenure.
  • Total Payment – Principal plus interest.

Pro Tip: Test different combinations of loan amount, interest rate, and tenure to find a repayment plan that aligns with your cash flow.

Glossary – Key Terms Explained

Term
Definition
EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment)
The fixed monthly amount you repay towards your loan, including both principal and interest.
Principal Amount
The original amount borrowed from the lender.
Interest Rate
The percentage charged by the lender on the loan amount.
Loan Tenure
The period over which the loan is to be repaid, usually in months or years.
Processing Fee
A one-time charge levied by the lender for processing the loan application.
Amortisation
The process of gradually reducing the loan balance through periodic payments.
Total Interest Payable
The total amount of interest paid over the loan tenure, excluding the principal.
