What Is Digital Lending?

Before you can understand anything, you need to understand the digital lending definition. Digital lending refers to the process in which lenders use online platforms, mobile applications, and financial technologies to offer loans to borrowers. Unlike conventional loans, where you have to fill out lengthy forms, digital lenders automate the lending process right from application to closure.

The ease and convenience of the application is what makes digital lending popular in India. Let’s take the example of personal loans. Fintech lenders currently dominate the personal loan market, as they are the ones sanctioning 76% of personal loans.

Rather than spending days waiting to sit down with loan officers and complete forms, you can now borrow loans from home. Fintech firms, online lending platforms, and digital loan providers are filling the void left by traditional banks. They provide faster, easier, and more convenient lending.

One of the most important benefits of digital lending is that it can include previously marginalized segments like first-time borrowers or even small businesses without any credit history. Leveraging alternate data inputs and AI-driven assessments, lenders can make rapid decisions.

Traditional Loan Approval Process vs Digital Lending

Whether getting a loan for business or personal needs, traditional banks often face a higher degree of rejection rate due to strict eligibility criteria for business loans and personal loans. The traditional loan approval process, in that, is not just long but tiring, as well.

Traditional banks also necessitate submitting a long list of documents, which makes loans inaccessible. This is the sole reason why getting a loan from traditional banks is lengthy and full of hassles. The most frustrating pain point is the long traditional loan approval time.

Traditional Loan Application Process

Here is what you must do if you need a personal, home or business loan from a traditional bank:

  • Visit a branch of the bank in person to initiate a loan application.
  • Fill out a long list of paperwork, including loan applications and detailed financial disclosures.
  • Submit income tax returns, payslips, address proof, identity proof and often collateral documents.
  • Wait for the manual verification of submitted documents and the physical checking of assets.
  • Undergo a lengthy credit evaluation, whereby the bank determines the credit rating and history of the applicant based on limited financial information.
  • Wait for approval, which could sometimes take a few days to two weeks.
  • Wait a few days more for the release of funds, which can again be delayed due to bureaucratic procedures.

The process is not only time-consuming but also full of challenges. Manual verification results in human error, data abuse, or even application rejection due to small discrepancies. The majority of credit-worthy borrowers, particularly gig workers, freelancers, and small businessmen without documented income records, tend to get rejected by traditional banks.

Digital Loan Approval Process: Quick, Automated & Consumer-Centric

Digital lending, however, offers a completely different experience when you are applying for a loan for business you are running:

  • You can apply for a loan online through websites or mobile platforms.
  • Minimal documentation is needed. Usually, a PAN card, Aadhaar card, and bank statements scanned and uploaded are enough to process the loan.
  • E-KYC provides instant verification without any requirement to meet in person.
  • Machine learning algorithms evaluate creditworthiness for a business loan based on a broader set of data points. It includes not only credit scores but also transaction histories, utility bills, online purchase behaviour, and so forth.
  • The digital loan approval process is usually instant, with pre-approved offers to suitable borrowers.
  • You can get the money in your bank account within hours.

The digital loan approval process not only makes borrowing easy but also creates avenues for financial inclusion. It allows those with non-conventional incomes, small business entrepreneurs, and rural residents to get financing at low costs and with ease as well.

Key Technologies Driving Digital Lending

Online lending in India is gaining a significant competitive advantage because of the technologies that it leverages. It is the technology behind digital lending that is speeding up the application and approval process and everything in between. Here are the key technologies that are driving digital lending in India:

1. Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Traditional lending uses bank statements, credit scores, and collateral for loan approval. AI in loan approval, on the other hand, analyses loan applications based on a broad range of parameters and criteria. These parameters include credit history, spending patterns and income streams to gauge risk. AI in loan approval also enables quicker, data-driven decisions and less human bias.

2. Machine Learning (ML)

Besides AI, digital lenders also utilise machine learning for loan approval. ML algorithms learn from experience with historical data to predict borrower spending and behaviour. Machine learning for loan approval has proved very beneficial in this regard. It can flag probable defaults by looking at trends in comparable profiles so that lenders can make better decisions.

3. Big Data

Big data is a crucial technology behind digital lending and approval. It provides actionable insights to lenders that help them improve efficiencies and mitigate risks. Lenders also use big data to access non-traditional sources such as payment history of electricity bills, mobile recharge history, or transaction history to ascertain creditworthiness.

4. Blockchain

Blockchain in digital lending provides higher transparency and security for lending. It can authenticate identities, maintain immutable loan histories, and enable cross-border lending. This solution reduces the burden of regulations while streamlining the loan origin, monitoring, and administration procedures.

5. Cloud Computing

The adoption of cloud computing in digital lending is streamlining loan applications and approvals. This technology not only reduces infrastructure costs but also ensures a smoother loan process. Thanks to cloud systems, lenders can access a borrower’s financial information instantly, which expedites the approval process.

Benefits of Digital Lending in Loan Approvals

The benefits of digital lending have far-reaching implications than traditional lenders. From flexible eligibility requirements for business loans to accelerated speed, these benefits help digital lenders stand out from conventional lenders.

1. Speed and Efficiency

One of the main digital lending advantages that you benefit from as a borrower is faster processing times. What used to take weeks because of strict verification processes and document submission now takes minutes or hours. Automation, AI-driven decision-making, and online document submission result in:

  • Faster loan approvals.
  • Credit scores and financial information are retrieved in real-time.
  • Approval is typically generated in real-time, particularly for pre-approved borrowers.

The speed and efficiency are really helpful and effective in time-based needs, such as medical expenses or business operational capital needs.

2. Ease of Access

Another benefit of digital lending is ease of access. You can be anywhere in India and still apply and get a business loan with just a few clicks and uploads. You can apply for a loan with just your smartphone or laptop and an internet connection. You can make a super easy loan application online:

  • While at home or the office
  • Weekends, holidays, or late nights
  • Without having to go or stand in line at bank branches

Easy loan application online is a revolution for rural areas where traditional banks are inaccessible. Additionally, self-employed or freelance workers who do not have a 9-5 schedule can enjoy the convenience of online lending.

3. Reduced Documentation

Submitting a long list of documents has long been a struggle for borrowers who apply for financing ranging from personal to business loans. Digital lenders eliminate the need to carry bundles of papers and photocopies with you for loans. Online loan applications only need:

  • Aadhaar number for e-KYC
  • PAN card for tax identification
  • Your salary slip or bank account details

Some of the top online lending platforms even have integrated data retrieval systems that automatically fetch your data for approval. This simplifies the process and reduces delays caused by missing or wrong documents.

4. Improved Customer Experience

Online business loan applications are not just easy to apply but they also improve your customer experience. Online lending platforms provide:

  • EMI calculators to assist in the calculation of payments each month
  • Eligibility checks in real-time
  • Comparison of loans for an informed decision
  • Chatbots and call centres in real-time

As a borrower, you can also track your loan applications, view the status, and get reminders on EMIs. Such ease of communication and convenience was not possible through traditional banking.

Challenges in Digital Lending

The extensive benefits of digital lending make them ideal for various borrowers and financing needs. However, there are still a few challenges in digital lending you have to overcome. From security issues to divergent regulations, here are the challenges in digital lending that still persist:

1. Data Privacy and Security

One of the major challenges in digital lending is the privacy of personal and financial information. Lenders must guarantee adequate encryption, multi-factor authentication, and adherence to data protection legislation.

2. Digital Literacy

Not all borrowers in India are digitally savvy, especially in rural areas. Digitally unlearned borrowers, low-income groups and those with less or no familiarity with digital gadgets might not be able to run these websites on their own.

3. Regulation and Compliance

Digital lenders need to operate in changing digital loan regulations. Failing proper regulation can lead to predatory lending, high interest rates, and abusive debt collection. All these eventually nullify the digital lending benefits.

4. Credit Risk Assessment

Even with technological advancements, there’s still some degree of inaccuracy and digital loan risks while evaluating creditworthiness. This is particularly true in markets where conventional credit information is poor or limited.

How Digital Lending is Revolutionising Small Business Loans?

Small businesses have long struggled to get a business loan in India due to the lack of collateral or proper documents. Here’s how digital lending for small business loans has changed everything:

  • Access to Financing: SMEs can now take loans online from platforms which determine the health of a business based on cash flow, online transactions, and customer ratings rather than balance sheets.
  • Peer-to-Peer Lending & Crowdfunding: Peer-to-peer (P2P) lending and crowdfunding are two other alternative models that give small businesses the facility to raise funds from individual investors, bypassing the traditional bank altogether.
  • Tailored Offerings: SME loans through digital platforms offer tailor-made loan products like short-term lines of credit, flexible repayment terms and invoice financing that are tied to business requirements.

Future of Digital Lending and Loan Approvals

Redseer Strategy Consultants anticipate that by FY28, digital lending will make up 5% of total retail loans, which is up from 2.5% in FY24. While the trends in digital lending keep unfolding, the future of digital lending looks promising.

  • Open Banking: Open banking allows third-party banks to access consumer banking information (with consent) to develop tailored lending products, boosting a more competitive market.
  • Real-Time Data Processing: One of the most significant digital lending innovations is the next-generation platforms that are changing the future of loan approvals with real-time underwriting.
  • Digital Wallets and Neobanks: Neobanks (online-only banks) and e-wallets are introducing credit products to their platform, further blurring the lines between banking, finance, and technology.
  • Hyper-Personalization: The future of digital lending will provide hyper-personalised behaviour, occupation, location, and even season-based loans thanks to advanced AI and ML algorithms.
