How to Download an Aadhaar Card with a UID number?

  1. Visit the official UIDAI website: Start by accessing the UIDAI website
  2. Navigate to the e-Aadhaar section: Under ‘My Aadhaar’, select ‘Download Aadhaar’.
  3. Choose ‘Aadhaar’ as your reference: Select ‘Aadhaar’ in the ‘I Have’ section.
  4. Enter your UID number: Input your 12-digit UID number.
  5. Fill in the captcha: Enter the captcha code displayed on the screen.
  6. Request OTP: Click ‘Send OTP’ to receive a one-time password on your registered mobile number.
  7. Enter OTP and download: Input the OTP and click ‘Verify and Download’.

You’ll receive your e-Aadhaar as a PDF file. Open it using your e Aadhaar password, which combines the first four letters of your name (in uppercase) and your birth year.

How to Download an Aadhaar Card by Using Your Enrolment ID?

  1. Access the UIDAI website: Start by visiting the UIDAI portal
  2. Go to the ‘Download Aadhaar’ section: Choose the ‘Enrolment ID’ option.
  3. Input your Enrolment ID: Enter the 14-digit EID, found on the acknowledgement slip provided during enrolment.
  4. Enter captcha and request OTP: Fill in the captcha and click ‘Send OTP’ to your registered mobile number.
  5. OTP verification: Enter the OTP and click ‘Verify and Download’.

Your e-Aadhaar will download, and you can access it using the e Aadhaar password.

How to Download e-Aadhaar by Using Virtual ID?

  1. Visit the UIDAI portal: Access the UIDAI website.
  2. Navigate to ‘Download Aadhaar’: Select ‘Virtual ID’ from the dropdown menu.
  3. Enter your VID: Input the 16-digit VID generated through the UIDAI portal.
  4. Fill in the captcha and request OTP: Enter the captcha code and click ‘Send OTP’.
  5. OTP verification: Enter the OTP and click ‘Verify and Download’.

This approach helps maintain your UID number’s confidentiality while accessing your e-Aadhaar card.

How to Download e-Aadhaar by Using Name and Date of Birth?

If you’ve misplaced your UID number or Enrolment ID, you can download your e-Aadhaar using your name and date of birth:

  1. Visit the UIDAI website: Start by visiting the UIDAI portal.
  2. Select ‘Retrieve Lost or Forgotten EID/UID’: You can find this option under the ‘My Aadhaar’ tab.
  3. Enter your details: Input your full name, registered mobile number, and date of birth.
  4. Request OTP: Click ‘Send OTP’ to receive a One-Time Password on your registered mobile number.
  5. Retrieve EID/UID: Enter the OTP and click ‘Verify’. You’ll receive your Enrolment ID or UID number on your mobile number.
  6. Use the retrieved ID to download Aadhaar: Follow the earlier steps to download your e-Aadhaar using the retrieved EID/UID.

This method offers a quick solution if you’ve lost your UID number or Enrolment ID.

How to Print Your Aadhaar Card?

  1. Open the PDF: Locate the downloaded file on your device.
  2. Enter the e Aadhaar password: Open the file using the password (a combination of the first four letters of your name in uppercase and your birth year).
  3. Connect to a printer: Ensure your device connects to a printer.
  4. Print the document: Select the print option from the PDF viewer and adjust the settings as needed.

Printing your Aadhaar card provides a physical copy for identification purposes, considered valid for all official uses.

How to Download Online PDF File of Aadhaar Card?

Downloading the PDF version of your Aadhaar card online ensures you always have a digital copy available:

  1. Access the UIDAI website: Start by visiting the UIDAI website.
  2. Go to ‘Download Aadhaar’: Choose a method (UID number, Enrolment ID, or Virtual ID) to download your e-Aadhaar.
  3. Enter the required information: Input the relevant details like UID number, EID, or VID.
  4. Request and enter OTP: Request an OTP, enter it, and verify your identity.
  5. Download the PDF file: After verification, your e-Aadhaar will download in PDF format.

You can easily open this password-protected PDF version using the e Aadhaar password.

How to Download e-Aadhaar Without OTP?

Downloading it without OTP becomes possible if your mobile number isn’t registered or accessible:

  1. Visit the UIDAI’s resident portal: Start by visiting the UIDAI Resident Portal.
  2. Use the ‘TOTP’ method: Generate a Time-based One-Time Password (TOTP) using the mAadhaar app instead of an OTP.
  3. Download e-Aadhaar: Enter your UID number or Enrolment ID, the TOTP, and complete the captcha verification.
  4. Verify and download: Click ‘Verify and Download’ to obtain your e-Aadhaar without needing an OTP.

This method proves useful if you face issues with your registered mobile number or prefer not to use SMS-based OTPs.
