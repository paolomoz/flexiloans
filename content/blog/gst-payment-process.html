GST Payment Process

Posted Sep 1, 2023 · Updated Nov 28, 2025 · 16 min read

In today’s dynamic business landscape, the Goods and Services Tax (GST) has emerged as a crucial component of India’s tax system. Designed to simplify tax compliance and unify the country’s indirect taxation structure, GST has transformed how businesses handle financial transactions. Central to this process is the GST payment process, which contains several essential steps like GST challan generation, GST challan creation, and GST challan verification.

The GST Payment Process begins with GST challan generation, where taxpayers generate a challan online through the GST portal. This challan acts as a receipt and contains all the necessary tax liability details, such as the tax amount, interest, penalties, and other charges. With the challan, businesses are well-equipped to initiate the payment process.

In this article, we will explore the complexities of the GST payment process, explore each GST payment process step in detail, and provide valuable insights to empower you with a better understanding of GST compliance. Whether you are an experienced business owner or just beginning your entrepreneurial journey, unravelling the mysteries of GST will undoubtedly lead to more confident and booming financial management.

Register for the GST Payment Process

The initial and vital step in the GST payment process is to register for GST. Businesses in India with an annual turnover exceeding the prescribed threshold need to register for GST. For most states, the threshold limit is set at Rs. 40 lakhs, while certain special category states have a lower threshold of Rs. 20 lakhs. This GST registration is crucial as it establishes your presence as a GST taxpayer and enables you to claim input tax credits.

The threshold limits for GST registration vary from state to state and depend on the nature of the business and the annual turnover. For certain states and Union Territories, the threshold limit for registration as a supplier of goods and/or services is Rs. 10 lakhs and for others, it is Rs. 20 lakhs and Rs. 40 lakhs

A step-by-step guide to making an online GST payment through the GST portal:

  1. Visit www.gst.gov.in and log in to access the GST portal.
  2. Navigate to the “Services” tab and click on “Payments,” then choose the “Create Challan” option.
  3. Enter your relevant details in the provided fields.
  4. If you have already created a challan, select the “Saved Challan” option.
  5. Under the “E-Payment” section, choose your preferred payment method.
  6. Fill in all the required details and click on “Generate Challan” for the GST challan generation.
  7. Select the “Net Banking” option to proceed with the payment.
  8. Choose the bank through which you want to make the payment.
  9. Review and agree to all the terms and conditions.
  10. Make the payment using your selected bank’s net banking facility.
  11. Download the challan for future reference and complete the GST challan creation.

Here is a step-by-step guide to making an offline GST payment through NEFT/RTGS:

  1. Log in to the GST portal and select NEFT/RTGS as your preferred payment mode.
  2. Choose your bank from the list of Remitting Banks provided.
  3. Click on the “Generate Challan” option to initiate the GST challan generation.
  4. Print the challan and visit your chosen bank branch.
  5. Make the payment at the bank using either a cheque or the account transfer facility.
  6. The bank will process the transaction, and within 2 hours, the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) will confirm it.
  7. Check the payment in the Electronic Cash Ledger under its respective head.
  8. By following these steps, you can successfully complete the GST payment process through NEFT/RTGS, providing a convenient and secure mode of payment for your tax obligations.

Here is a step-by-step guide for making an over-the-counter GST payment:

  1. Log in to the GST portal and choose the “Over The Counter” option as your preferred payment method.
  2. From the drop-down list, select the bank where you intend to make the deposit.
  3. Next, select the type of instrument you wish to use for payment – cheque, cash, or demand draft.
  4. Click on the “Generate Challan” option to initiate the GST challan generation.
  5. Check the challan once the portal generates it with all the relevant details.
  6. Print out the challan and proceed to your respective bank.
  7. At the bank counter, make the payment using the chosen instrument within its validity date.
  8. By following these steps, you can easily complete the GST payment process at your preferred bank, providing a convenient and straightforward method to fulfil your tax obligations.

Determining Tax Liabilities in the GST Payment Process

Understanding your tax liability is essential to ensure accurate compliance in the GST payment process. GST, the Goods and Services Tax, is categorised into various components based on the nature of the supply. These components include Central GST (CGST), State GST (SGST), Integrated GST (IGST), and Union Territory GST (UTGST). The categorisation helps in the efficient allocation of tax revenue between the central and state governments, as well as in cases of interstate and international trade.

Aggregate Turnover

Aggregate turnover is a critical parameter for businesses to determine their eligibility for threshold exemption under GST registration. It includes various elements of a taxpayer’s financial activities while excluding certain components to arrive at a comprehensive figure. 

Inclusions

The aggregate turnover includes the total value of all outward taxable supplies made by you with the same PAN on an India-wide basis. This involves considering supplies that attract GST on the sale of goods or services. Additionally, it comprises the value of all outward exempt supplies made by business entities with the same PAN across India.

Exempt supplies refer to those transactions that do not attract GST and do not require GST challan creation. Moreover, the value of exports made by entities with the same PAN on an India-wide basis is also included in the aggregate turnover.

Finally, the value of interstate supplies, which refers to the movement of goods or services from one state to another, made by persons under the same PAN across India is incorporated in the aggregate turnover calculation.

Exclusions

There are certain exclusions when it comes to aggregate turnover calculation. These exclusions consist of taxes like CGST (Central Goods and Services Tax), SGST (State Goods and Services Tax), UTGST (Union Territory Goods and Services Tax), and IGST (Integrated Goods and Services Tax), which are payable by the taxpayer to the government as part of GST compliance. Additionally, the value of inward supplies, where the tax is payable under reverse charge, is not considered in the aggregate turnover. The reverse charge mechanism mandates the recipient of goods or services to pay GST instead of the supplier.

GST Challan Generation

Once you have calculated your tax liability, the subsequent step is to generate the GST challan, a crucial document that serves as a payment voucher for GST dues. The GST challan contains essential details, including your GSTIN (Goods and Services Tax Identification Number), the amount of tax liability, and the specific type of tax to pay (CGST, SGST, IGST, or UTGST). Accuracy in filling out the challan is of utmost importance to avoid any discrepancies in the payment process.

For the GST challan generation, you can utilise the official GST portal or authorised banking channels. Online platforms provide a user-friendly interface, making the process seamless and efficient. It is essential to double-check the information provided in the challan before proceeding with the payment. Verify the correctness of your GSTIN, ensuring that it matches your registration details accurately. Additionally, carefully enter the tax liability amount, cross-referencing it with your calculated tax liability to avoid any errors.

By completing the GST challan generation accurately, you ensure that your payment reaches the right tax component, and you comply with the GST regulations.

GST Challan Verification

Before finalising the GST payment process, it is crucial to carry out a thorough verification of the generated GST challan. This step ensures that every detail is accurate in your tax liability, ultimately directing your payment to the right tax component.

Think of the GST challan verification as a checkpoint before embarking on the payment journey. It helps in preventing any potential errors and ensures a smooth and error-free payment process.

Next, review the tax liability amount mentioned in the challan. This is the actual tax amount you owe as per your calculations. Confirming its accuracy minimises the risk of overpaying or underpaying, thus maintaining your financial records precisely.
