The introduction of the Goods and Services Tax (GST) on July 1, 2017, brought about significant changes in the taxation of various goods and services, including gold. As a precious metal with cultural and investment value, understanding the impact of GST on gold purchases is crucial for consumers, investors, and businesses alike. In this article, we delve into the intricacies of GST on gold, its effects on business loans, and its implications for the Indian gold market in 2024.

Rate of GST on Gold

Under the GST regime, gold and gold jewelry attract a tax rate of 3%, with an additional 5% levied on making charges. This rate applies to gold bullion, gold coins, and gold ornaments. Before GST, the tax on gold varied from state to state, with VAT rates ranging from 1% to 5%, and an excise duty of 1%.

Impact on Gold Prices

The implementation of GST on gold has led to an increase in the overall price of gold for consumers. While the GST rate on gold is 3%, the effective tax rate is higher when considering the 5% GST on making charges. This has resulted in gold jewellery becoming more expensive compared to the pre-GST era.

For example, if the price of gold is ₹30,000 per 10 grams and the making charges are ₹2,000, the total cost before GST would be ₹32,000. After GST, the price of gold would be ₹30,900 (3% GST on gold), and the making charges would be ₹2,100 (5% GST on making charges), resulting in a total cost of ₹33,000. The effective tax rate in this case is 3.125%.

Positive Aspects of GST on Gold

  1. Uniform Taxation: GST has brought uniformity in the taxation of gold across the country, eliminating the variation in tax rates that existed under the previous tax regime.
  2. Transparency: The GST system has increased transparency in the gold supply chain, as all transactions are now recorded and tracked through the GST network. This helps in curbing tax evasion and ensuring compliance.
  3. Reduction in Tax Evasion: The implementation of GST on gold has made it more difficult for unscrupulous traders to evade taxes, as the movement of gold is now monitored more closely. This has led to a more level playing field for businesses.

Challenges and Concerns

  1. Increase in Gold Smuggling: The higher effective tax rate on gold under GST has led to concerns about increased gold smuggling activities to avoid paying taxes. This poses a challenge for authorities in curbing illegal activities.
  2. Impact on Demand: The increased GST on gold may lead to a reduction in demand, particularly among price-sensitive consumers. This could have implications for the growth of the gold industry in India.
  3. Compliance Burden: Jewellers and gold traders face an increased compliance burden under the GST regime, as they are required to maintain detailed records and file regular returns. This has led to some resistance and challenges in adapting to the new system.
