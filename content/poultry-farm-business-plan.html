What is a Poultry Farming Business?

Poultry farming is the rearing of birds for the sake of consumption. People use the meat and the eggs from the birds reared in such farms to cook and eat. Poultry includes chicken, duck, geese and, in most cases, turkey.

A poultry farm business refers to starting a poultry farm for commercial purposes. Chickens are the most popular poultry bird due to their meat and eggs. This has given rise to several small and large chicken poultry farms in India. Because it comes under agricultural activity, the government has initiated several schemes for the sector.

Most people start a poultry farm to sell the products and gain a profit from it. This, of course, means that the product will have to be of solid quality. This requires good quality food for the birds, plenty of water (for drinking and cleansing the poultry), a stable living environment, and expert care as the basics.

All the above aspects are neither easy nor cheap to arrange for, so a poultry farming business plan is essential before you start your journey.

Type of Poultry Farms

The first of your many decisions will be choosing the kind of poultry farm you want to start. There are broadly two types of chicken farms:

  • Layer farms: These farms raise chickens or other poultry for their eggs. These farms don't provide chicken meat and rear their birds solely to sell their eggs. Such a farm is a good option in a locality where you will find a lot of health establishments or stores selling eggs but not other meat.
  • Broiler farms: These farms rear chickens for their meat. This kind of poultry farm will settle well in areas with more butcher shops and a higher density of restaurants. An offshoot of a broiler farm is a breeder farm. This farm breeds chicken and supplies it to broiler farms.
  • Pullet farms: A less popular kind of poultry farm is a pullet farm. These farms produce chickens and send them to layer farms or breeder farms.

This is only a brief overview of the types of farms. Each has its pros and cons. Make sure to do further research to determine which type suits you best.

Types of Chicken/Poultry

A huge variety of birds, especially chicken, is in demand in the market that you can rear for a chicken business. You can categorise chicken into three main categories:

  • Broiler chicken: It has a full growth period of about eight weeks and produces a good amount of meat to be sold.
  • Layer chicken: These start laying eggs at 18 weeks and can continue until about 78 weeks. They produce about 250 eggs per year.
  • Rooster chicken: Farms use rooster chickens to guard the chickens that lay eggs because of their natural protective instinct.

Even under these types, you can choose many local and international chicken breeds that have specific qualities and requirements. You must thoroughly research the breeds to choose which one to include in your chicken poultry farm.

Location

The location you select for starting your hen farmhouse will depend on a few different factors, such as:

  • The scale of your poultry farming business
  • The type of farm you want to set up
  • Safety of the area
  • Level of pollution
  • How much of your finances can be used for land
  • Ease of access to a market and other amenities.

Based on the area of land you buy, these are the types of farms you can set up:

  1. Battery cage farm: This kind of farm needs around 6,000 sq. ft. of space. Of this, 4,000 sq. ft. is for the birds and another 2,000 sq. ft. for other construction.
  2. Semi-range farm: The coops and the penn made for the birds in this kind of farm require about 8,000 sq. ft. of land.
  3. Free-range farm: Anywhere between 12,000 sq. ft. to 36,000 sq. ft. of land is enough for the chicken to be left loose in this farm
  4. Wild poultry farm: This system requires around 44,000 sq. ft. of land to allow the chicken to grow in a completely wild environment.

Some poultry farmers also start small poultry farms or home poultry farms.

Outdoor processes will also be different from the processes used for indoor poultry. Again, it is best to consult an expert or a practicing poultry farmer.

Things To Consider Before Starting A Poultry Farm Business

While owning a poultry farming company comes with many benefits, the industry has a few potential downsides you might want to consider.

  • Outbreaks: History has witnessed several serious poultry and cattle disease outbreaks. Viruses like the swine flu and H1N1 have caused a lot of chaos and high losses for poultry farmers. To avoid such epidemics, ensuring the quality of poultry food and maintaining strong and responsible hygiene practices is essential.
  • Costs: The expenses you incur on the rearing may vary from time to time as the supplies necessary for the upkeep fluctuate from time to time. This may affect the profitability of the business. The prices of the products also show changes based on market conditions. This will affect the profits too.
  • Investment: Even though the poultry farming business requires less capital than other cattle activities, it is still capital-intensive. The initial capital required will be quite high.
  • Trends: The market sees ever-changing trend cycles now and then. New trends like veganism or going dairy-free might show an effect on the profits of a poultry farm.

Nothing comes without its fair share of risks, and a poultry business's benefits are impressive. A great example of a successful poultry farm business that has grown to great heights is the Sneha Chicken franchise, known to have the best chicken in Hyderabad, India.

Licences (Documents) Required For A Poultry Farm Business

Starting a poultry farm company for products such as poultry, which people will directly consume, will need a few certificates to ensure that the farm is following appropriate practices and not putting anyone at a health risk. For this purpose, your poultry farm will need these licences:

  • A NOC (no objection certificate) from the relevant governing body
  • A NOC from the pollution board
  • Permission for electrical use
  • licence from the groundwater department
  • Business registration
  • MSME Registration: Non-mandatory but will help with benefits like a Mudra loan for poultry farms.
  • Trademark registration: Also non-mandatory but beneficial for your brand.
